Section C – [General Math]

1. A shopkeeper cheats to the extent of 10% while buying and selling, by using false weights. His total gain is? [একজন দোকানদার মিথ্যা ওজন ব্যবহার করে ক্রয়-বিক্রয়ের সময় ১০% প্রতারণা করে। তার মোট লাভ কত?]

  • 19
  • 20
  • 21
  • 22

Solution:

Cheating while buying (takes more): Instead of taking 100 units, takes 100 + 10 = 110 units, but pays for 100 units.

Cheating while selling (gives less): Instead of giving 100 units, gives 100 - 10 = 90 units, but charges for 100 units.

Let CP of 1 unit be Re 1.

Buying: He buys 110 units for Rs 100 (Cost per unit = 100/110).

Selling: He sells 90 units for Rs 100 (Selling Price per unit = 100/90).

Consider selling the 110 units he bought. He sells these units in batches of 90 units charged as 100 units.

Alternative approach: Let the true weight be 100g.

Buying: Pays for 100g but gets 110g.

Selling: Charges for 100g but gives 90g.

Effectively, he buys 110g and sells 90g for the same amount of money (price of 100g).

Let CP of 1g = Rs 1.

He buys 110g for Rs 100 (actual cost of 100g).

He sells 90g for Rs 100 (pretending it's 100g).

To compare profit, consider the cost and selling price for the same quantity, e.g., 990g (LCM of 110 and 90).

Cost of 990g = (Cost of 110g) * 9 = 100 * 9 = Rs 900.

Selling Price of 990g = (SP of 90g) * 11 = 100 * 11 = Rs 1100.

Profit = SP - CP = 1100 - 900 = Rs 200.

Profit % = (Profit / CP) * 100 = (200 / 900) * 100 = (2/9) * 100 = 22.22...%

Using successive percentage formula for gain: \(x+y + \frac{xy}{100}\)

Gain while buying = \(\frac{\text{Error}}{\text{True Value - Error}} \times 100\) is not right. Gain = \(\frac{\text{Extra Quantity}}{\text{Quantity Paid For}} \times 100 = \frac{10}{100} \times 100 = 10\%\)? No.

Gain while buying = \(\frac{\text{Error}}{\text{False Weight}} \times 100 = \frac{10}{100} \times 100 = 10\%\) ? No.

Gain % while buying = \(\frac{110-100}{100} = 10\%\)? Cost is lower.

Gain % while selling = \(\frac{100-90}{90} \times 100 = \frac{10}{90} \times 100 = 11.11\%\).

Total Gain = \(10 + 11.11 + \frac{10 \times 11.11}{100}\) is not correct application.

Let True Weight (TW) = 100, False Weight (FW). Error = 10.

Buying: Uses FW = 110. Gain % = \( \frac{110-100}{100} \times 100 = 10\% \) (Gets 10% more goods for the price).

Selling: Uses FW = 90. Gain % = \( \frac{100-90}{90} \times 100 = \frac{10}{90} \times 100 = 11.11\% \).

Total Gain % = \( \frac{\text{Total Gain}}{\text{True Cost}} \times 100\). From 990g example: Gain = 200, Cost = 900. Gain % = 22.22%. Closest option is 22.

Let's use formula: Gain% = \( \frac{\text{Error %}}{100 - \text{Error %}} \times 100 \) for selling. \( \frac{10}{100-10} \times 100 = 11.11\% \).

For buying, Gain% = \( \frac{\text{Error %}}{100} \times 100 \)? No. Let CP=100. Buys 110 units. Sells 90 units for price of 100 units. Profit = \( \frac{SP-CP}{CP} \times 100\). SP=Price of 100. CP = cost of 90. Requires relation. Let CP of 100 units = 100. Buys 110 for 100. Sells 90 for 100. Cost of 90 = \( \frac{100}{110} \times 90 = \frac{900}{11} \). SP=100. Profit = \( 100 - \frac{900}{11} = \frac{1100-900}{11} = \frac{200}{11} \). Profit % = \( \frac{200/11}{900/11} \times 100 = \frac{200}{900} \times 100 = 22.22\% \).

Answer: d. 22 (Closest integer to 22.22)

2. If the cost price is 25% of selling price. Then what is the profit percent? [যদি ক্রয় মূল্য বিক্রয় মূল্যের ২৫% হয়। তাহলে লাভের শতাংশ কত?]

  • 25
  • 50
  • 100
  • 300

Solution:

Let Selling Price (SP) = 100.

Cost Price (CP) = 25% of SP = 0.25 × 100 = 25.

Profit = SP - CP = 100 - 25 = 75.

Profit Percent = \( \frac{\text{Profit}}{CP} \times 100 \)

Profit Percent = \( \frac{75}{25} \times 100 = 3 \times 100 = 300\% \)

Answer: d. 300

In April, 330 of 4400 patient of a hospital were women. In May, 220 of 5000 patient of a hospital were women. According to Govt if a hospital has 10% or more women patient it will get a fund. In which moth the hospital will get the Fund? [জানুয়ারিতে হাসপাতালের ৫০০০ রোগীর মধ্যে ৫৫০ জন নারী। ফেব্রুয়ারিতে, ১৪০০০ রোগীর মধ্যে ৭০০ জন মহিলা ছিলেন। মার্চে, হাসপাতালের ৮৭০০ রোগীর মধ্যে ৭৫০ জন নারী। এপ্রিলে, হাসপাতালের ৪৪০০ রোগীর মধ্যে ৩৩০ জন নারী। মে মাসে, হাসপাতালের ৫০০০ রোগীর মধ্যে ২২০ জন নারী। সরকারের মতে যদি একটি হাসপাতালে ১০% বা তার বেশি মহিলা রোগী থাকেলে তা অর্থ সাহায্য পাবে। কোন মাসে হাসপাতাল অর্থ সাহায্য পাবে?]

  • February

4. A car driver, driving in a fog, passes a pedestrian who was walking at the rate of 2 km/h in the same direction. The pedestrian could see the car for 6 minutes and it was visible to him up to distance of 0.6 km. what was speed of the car? [একজন গাড়ি চালক, কুয়াশায় গাড়ি চালাচ্ছেন, একজন পথচারীকে পাশ কাটিয়ে যাচ্ছেন যিনি একই দিকে ২ কিমি/ঘণ্টা বেগে হাঁটছেন। পথচারী গাড়িটিকে ৬ মিনিটের জন্য দেখতে পারে এবং তার কাছে দৃশ্যমান ছিল ০.৬ কিমি দূরত্ব পর্যন্ত। গাড়ির গতি কত ছিল?]

  • 30 kmph
  • 15 kmph
  • 20 kmph
  • 8 kmph

Solution:

Let the speed of the car be \(S_C\) km/h.

Speed of the pedestrian \(S_P = 2\) km/h.

They are moving in the same direction.

Relative speed of the car with respect to the pedestrian = \(S_{rel} = S_C - S_P = S_C - 2\) km/h.

The pedestrian can see the car for 6 minutes. During this time, the car travels 0.6 km *relative* to the pedestrian (the distance increases from 0 to 0.6 km).

Time (t) = 6 minutes = 6/60 hour = 0.1 hour.

Distance (d) = 0.6 km.

Distance = Relative Speed × Time

0.6 = \(S_{rel} \times 0.1\)

0.6 = \((S_C - 2) \times 0.1\)

Divide by 0.1: \( 0.6 / 0.1 = S_C - 2 \)

6 = \(S_C - 2\)

\(S_C = 6 + 2 = 8\) km/h.

Answer: d. 8 kmph

5. A cyclist moving on a circular track of radius 100 meters complete one revolution in 2 minutes. What is the average speed of cyclist (approx.)? [100 মিটার ব্যাসার্ধের একটি বৃত্তাকার ট্র্যাকে চলন্ত একজন সাইক্লিস্ট 2 মিনিটে একটি পূর্ণ ঘূর্ণন সম্পন্ন করে। সাইক্লিস্টের গড় গতি কত (প্রায়)?]

  • 314 m/min
  • 200 m/min
  • 300 m/min
  • 900 m/min

Solution:

Average speed = Total Distance / Total Time.

One revolution covers the circumference of the circular track.

Radius (r) = 100 meters.

Circumference (Distance) = \(2 \pi r = 2 \pi (100) = 200 \pi\) meters.

Time = 2 minutes.

Average Speed = \( \frac{200 \pi \text{ meters}}{2 \text{ minutes}} = 100 \pi \) m/min.

Using \(\pi \approx 3.14159\):

Average Speed \(\approx 100 \times 3.14159 = 314.159\) m/min.

This is approximately 314 m/min.

Answer: a. 314 m/min

6. A and B completed work together in 5 days. had A worked at twice the speed and B at half the speed, it would have taken them four days to complete the job. How much time would it take for A alone to do the job? [A এবং B মিলে একটি কাজ ৫ দিনে সম্পন্ন করে। যদি A দ্বিগুণ গতিতে এবং B অর্ধেক গতিতে কাজ করত, তাহলে কাজটি সম্পন্ন করতে তাদের চার দিন লাগত। A একা কাজটি করতে কত সময় নেবে?]

  • 10 days
  • 20 days
  • 25 days
  • 15 days

Solution:

Let A's normal rate be \(a\) jobs/day and B's normal rate be \(b\) jobs/day.

Case 1: Working together, time = 5 days.

Combined rate \(a+b\). Work = 1 job.

\( (a+b) \times 5 = 1 \implies a+b = \frac{1}{5} \) (Equation 1)

Case 2: A's new rate = \(2a\), B's new rate = \(b/2\). Time = 4 days.

New combined rate \(2a + b/2\). Work = 1 job.

\( (2a + b/2) \times 4 = 1 \)

\( 8a + 2b = 1 \) (Equation 2)

Solve the system of equations:

From Eq 1: \(b = \frac{1}{5} - a\)

Substitute into Eq 2:

\( 8a + 2(\frac{1}{5} - a) = 1 \)

\( 8a + \frac{2}{5} - 2a = 1 \)

\( 6a = 1 - \frac{2}{5} = \frac{3}{5} \)

\( a = \frac{3}{5 \times 6} = \frac{3}{30} = \frac{1}{10} \) job/day.

Time for A alone = 1 / Rate of A = 1 / (1/10) = 10 days.

Answer: a. 10 days

15. A boat of length 3 m and breadth 2 m is floating on a lake. The boat sinks by 1 cm when a man gets into it. What is the mass of the man? [3 মিটার দৈর্ঘ্য এবং 2 মিটার প্রস্থের একটি নৌকা একটি হ্রদে ভাসছে। একজন লোক নৌকাটিতে উঠলে নৌকাটি 1 সেমি ডুবে যায়। লোকটির ভর কত?]

  • 50 Kg
  • 60 Kg
  • 70 Kg
  • 80 Kg

Solution:

According to Archimedes' principle, the buoyant force on the submerged part of the boat equals the weight of the water displaced.

When the man gets in, the boat sinks further, displacing an additional volume of water. The weight of this additional displaced water is equal to the weight of the man.

Additional volume displaced = Area of boat base × Sink depth

Length = 3 m, Breadth = 2 m.

Area of base = 3 m × 2 m = 6 m2.

Sink depth = 1 cm = 0.01 m.

Additional volume displaced (V) = 6 m2 × 0.01 m = 0.06 m3.

Density of water (\(\rho\)) ≈ 1000 kg/m3.

Mass of displaced water = Volume × Density = V × \(\rho\)

Mass of displaced water = 0.06 m3 × 1000 kg/m3 = 60 kg.

Weight of displaced water = (Mass of displaced water) × g = 60g Newtons.

Weight of the man = Mass of man (m) × g = mg Newtons.

Weight of man = Weight of additional displaced water

mg = 60g

m = 60 kg.

Answer: b. 60 Kg

10. In which system, there is no change in internal energy? [কোন প্রক্রিয়ায় অভ্যন্তরীণ শক্তির কোন পরিবর্তন হয় না?]

  • Isothermal Process
  • Isobar Process
  • Isochoric process
  • Adiabatic Process

Solution:

Internal energy (U) of an ideal gas depends only on its temperature (T).

  • Isothermal Process: Temperature is constant (\(\Delta T = 0\)). Since internal energy depends only on temperature for an ideal gas, the change in internal energy is zero (\(\Delta U = 0\)).
  • Isobaric Process: Pressure is constant (\(\Delta P = 0\)). Temperature and volume can change, so internal energy generally changes.
  • Isochoric Process: Volume is constant (\(\Delta V = 0\)). Temperature and pressure can change, so internal energy generally changes.
  • Adiabatic Process: No heat exchange (\(Q = 0\)). Temperature, pressure, and volume can all change. Work done changes the internal energy (\(\Delta U = -W\)).

Therefore, there is no change in internal energy in an isothermal process (for an ideal gas).

Answer: a. Isothermal Process

  • 3
  • 4
  • 5

Solution:

There are two main types of dialysis used to treat kidney failure:

  1. Hemodialysis: Blood is filtered outside the body using an artificial kidney machine.
  2. Peritoneal dialysis: The lining of the abdomen (peritoneum) is used as a natural filter, with dialysis fluid introduced into the abdominal cavity.

Answer: a. 2

04. Which is not a classified form of conjugated proteins? [কোনটি সংযোজিত প্রোটিনের শ্রেণীভুক্ত রূপ নয়?]

  • Glycoproteins
  • Lipoproteins
  • Complete proteins
  • Metalloproteins

Solution:

As explained previously (Page 127 Q2), conjugated proteins (like glycoproteins, lipoproteins, metalloproteins) are classified based on the non-protein group attached. "Complete proteins" is a nutritional term referring to amino acid content, not a structural classification.

Answer: c. Complete proteins

05. in which stage of mitosis cell division does the chromatid start to look like an 'L'? [মাইটোসিস কোষ বিভাজনের কোন পর্যায়ে ক্রোমাটিড দেখতে 'L'-এর মত লাগে?]

  • Telophase
  • Metaphase
  • Anaphase
  • Prometaphase

Solution:

During Anaphase of mitosis, sister chromatids separate and are pulled towards opposite poles by the spindle fibers attached to their centromeres. Depending on the position of the centromere, the separating chromatids (now considered individual chromosomes) can appear V-shaped (metacentric), J-shaped, or L-shaped (submetacentric) as they are dragged through the cytoplasm with the centromere leading and the arms trailing.

Answer: c. Anaphase

06. Which one is the chemical formula of baking powder? [বেকিং পাউডারের রাসায়নিক সংকেত কোনটি?]

  • NaCl
  • Na2SO4
  • Na2CO3
  • NaHCO3

Solution:

Baking powder is typically a mixture, not a single compound. However, its primary active ingredient responsible for leavening (producing CO2 gas) is usually sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO3). Baking powder also contains an acid salt (like cream of tartar or sodium aluminum sulfate) and a filler (like cornstarch). Baking *soda* is pure sodium bicarbonate.

Given the options, NaHCO3 represents the key chemical component often associated with baking powder's function.

Answer: d. NaHCO3

07. Possible Isomers of C2H6O— [C2H6O-এর আইসোমার সংখ্যা-]

  • 1
  • 3
  • 2
  • 9

Solution:

The molecular formula C2H6O corresponds to two common structural isomers:

  1. Ethanol (Ethyl Alcohol): CH3CH2OH
  2. Dimethyl Ether: CH3OCH3

These are the only two stable structural isomers with this formula.

08. Which one is the weakest base? [কোনটি সবচেয়ে দুর্বল ক্ষার?]

  • NH4OH
  • KOH
  • NaOH
  • Ca(OH)2

Solution:

Bases are classified by their strength (degree of dissociation in water).

  • KOH (Potassium Hydroxide): Strong base (Group 1 hydroxide).
  • NaOH (Sodium Hydroxide): Strong base (Group 1 hydroxide).
  • Ca(OH)2 (Calcium Hydroxide): Strong base (Group 2 hydroxide, though less soluble than Group 1).
  • NH4OH (Ammonium Hydroxide): This represents ammonia (NH3) dissolved in water, which forms ammonium ions (NH4+) and hydroxide ions (OH-) in equilibrium. It is a weak base, meaning it only partially dissociates.

Therefore, ammonium hydroxide is the weakest base among the options.

Answer: a. NH4OH

09. Milk is one type of- [দুধ হচ্ছে এক প্রকার-]

  • jel.
  • Colloid.
  • Emulsion.
  • Suspension

Solution:

Milk is a complex mixture, but it is primarily classified as an emulsion. An emulsion is a type of colloid where small droplets of one liquid (like milk fat) are dispersed throughout another liquid (like water) with which it is immiscible. Proteins in milk act as emulsifying agents to stabilize the emulsion.

Answer: c. Emulsion.

10. Which one opposes the reflected UV-radiation from earth's surface to be radiated to space? [কোনটি পৃথিবী পৃষ্ঠ থেকে প্রতিফলিত UV-বিকিরণকে মহাকাশে বিকিরণ করায় বিরোধিতা করে?]

  • CH4
  • O2
  • NO2
  • CH2Cl2

Solution:

The question asks what opposes *reflected* UV radiation from escaping to space. The primary absorber of incoming UV radiation is the ozone layer (O3) in the stratosphere. However, the question asks about radiation reflected *from* the surface.

Gases that absorb outgoing radiation (primarily infrared, but some overlap exists) are greenhouse gases. Common greenhouse gases include:

  • Water vapor (H2O)
  • Carbon dioxide (CO2)
  • Methane (CH4)
  • Nitrous oxide (N2O)
  • Ozone (O3)
  • Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) and related compounds like Dichloromethane (CH2Cl2)

Oxygen (O2) is not a significant greenhouse gas. Nitrogen dioxide (NO2) absorbs visible light but is less significant for outgoing radiation compared to others.

Both Methane (CH4) and Dichloromethane (CH2Cl2) are greenhouse gases that absorb outgoing radiation. Methane is a much more significant greenhouse gas in terms of overall atmospheric effect. Dichloromethane also contributes. Ozone (not listed) also absorbs UV.

Given the options, Methane (CH4) is a major greenhouse gas that traps outgoing radiation, contributing to the greenhouse effect. While the question specifically mentions UV, greenhouse gases primarily trap infrared radiation (heat), which is what reflected sunlight is converted to at the surface. If interpreting "reflected UV-radiation" literally, Ozone (O3) would be the main absorber, but it's not an option. Among the given options, CH4 is the most significant gas that traps outgoing radiation.

Answer: a. CH4
