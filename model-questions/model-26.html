Section C – [General Math]

1. Subha buys a stock for 5000 taka, If the stock grows at a 10% rate, after 10 years the value of the stock will be? [ শুভা 5000 টাকায় একটি স্টক কিনে, যদি স্টকটি 10% হারে বৃদ্ধি পায়, তাহলে 10 বছর পর স্টকের মূল্যমান কত হবে?] (Assuming Compound Interest)

  • 12959.96
  • 12968.7
  • 12976.56
  • 12347.67

Solution:

Stock growth is usually calculated using compound interest.

Formula: \( A = P (1 + r)^n \)

Where:

  • A = Amount after n years
  • P = Principal amount = 5000 taka
  • r = Annual growth rate = 10% = 0.10
  • n = Number of years = 10

\( A = 5000 (1 + 0.10)^{10} = 5000 (1.1)^{10} \)

Calculate \( (1.1)^{10} \):

\( (1.1)^2 = 1.21 \)

\( (1.1)^4 = (1.21)^2 = 1.4641 \)

\( (1.1)^8 = (1.4641)^2 \approx 2.1435888 \)

\( (1.1)^{10} = (1.1)^8 \times (1.1)^2 \approx 2.1435888 \times 1.21 \approx 2.59374 \)

\( A = 5000 \times 2.59374 \approx 12968.7 \)

Answer: b. 12968.7

2. A train, 200m long, passed a girl, walking along the line in the same direction at the rate of 4 km/h in 20 seconds. The speed of the train is: [200 মিটার দীর্ঘ একটি ট্রেন, একই দিকে 4 কিমি/ঘন্টা বেগে লাইনে হাঁটা একটি মেয়েকে 20 সেকেন্ডে অতিক্রম করে যায়। ট্রেনের গতি কত?]

  • 32 km/h
  • 28 km/h
  • 35 km/h
  • 30 km/h

Solution:

Length of train (Distance to be covered relative to the girl) = 200 m.

Time taken = 20 seconds.

Speed of girl (\(S_G\)) = 4 km/h = \( 4 \times \frac{1000}{3600} = 4 \times \frac{5}{18} = \frac{10}{9} \) m/s.

Let the speed of the train be \(S_T\) m/s.

Since they are moving in the same direction, the relative speed (\(S_{rel}\)) = \(S_T - S_G\).

Distance = Relative Speed × Time

\( 200 = S_{rel} \times 20 \)

\( S_{rel} = 200 / 20 = 10 \) m/s.

So, \( S_T - S_G = 10 \)

\( S_T - \frac{10}{9} = 10 \)

\( S_T = 10 + \frac{10}{9} = \frac{90 + 10}{9} = \frac{100}{9} \) m/s.

Convert \(S_T\) to km/h:

\( \frac{100}{9} \text{ m/s} \times 3.6 \text{ km/h per m/s} = \frac{100}{9} \times \frac{36}{10} = \frac{10}{1} \times \frac{4}{1} = 40 \) km/h.

None of the options match 40 km/h.

Answer: (Calculated answer 40 km/h does not match options)

4. A bag contains 5 red balls and 3 blue balls. What is the probability of drawing a red ball if one ball is drawn randomly? [একটি ব্যাগে 5টি লাল বল এবং 3টি নীল বল রয়েছে। ব্যাগ থেকে আন্দাজে একটি বল উঠালে সেটি লাল রঙের হওয়ার সম্ভাবনা কত?]

5. A man buys 6000 carrots for 10,000 taka, for how much taka he should sell each carrot to make a 50% profit? [এক ব্যক্তি 10,000 টাকায় 6000টি গাজর কিনে। 50% লাভের জন্য প্রতিটি গাজর কত মূল্যে বিক্রয় করতে হবে?]

  • 2.5 taka
  • 1.5 taka loss
  • 2 taka
  • 1.75 taka

Solution:

Total Cost Price (CP) = 10,000 taka for 6000 carrots.

Desired Profit = 50%.

Total Selling Price (SP) required = CP + 50% of CP

Total SP = 10000 + 0.50 * 10000 = 10000 + 5000 = 15000 taka.

This is the total revenue needed from selling all 6000 carrots.

Selling price per carrot = Total SP / Number of carrots

SP per carrot = 15000 taka / 6000 carrots = 15 / 6 = 5 / 2 = 2.5 taka.

Answer: a. 2.5 taka

6. Seema and Meena divide a sum of Tk.25000 in the ratio of 3:2 respectively. If Tk.5000 is added to each of their shares, what would be the new ratio formed? [সীমা ও মীনা 25000 টাকা যথাক্রমে 3:2 অনুপাতে ভাগ করে নেয়। যদি তাদের প্রত্যেকের অংশে 5000 টাকা যোগ করা হয়, তাহলে নতুন অনুপাত কত হবে?]

  • 2:3
  • 3:4
  • 5:4
  • 4:3

Solution:

Total sum = 25000 Tk.

Ratio Seema : Meena = 3 : 2.

Total ratio parts = 3 + 2 = 5.

Seema's initial share = (3/5) × 25000 = 3 × 5000 = 15000 Tk.

Meena's initial share = (2/5) × 25000 = 2 × 5000 = 10000 Tk.

Tk 5000 is added to each share.

Seema's new share = 15000 + 5000 = 20000 Tk.

Meena's new share = 10000 + 5000 = 15000 Tk.

New ratio (Seema : Meena) = 20000 : 15000.

Simplify by dividing by 5000:

New ratio = 4 : 3.

Answer: d. 4:3

7. The angle of a elevation of the sun, when the length of the shadow of a tree is 3 times the height of the tree is [ একটি গাছের ছায়ার দৈর্ঘ্য যখন গাছটির উচ্চতার 3 গুণ, তখন সূর্যের উন্নতি কোণ কত?]

  • 30°
  • 44°
  • 60°
  • 90°

Solution:

Let h be the height of the tree.

Let L be the length of the shadow.

Given: L = 3h.

Let \(\theta\) be the angle of elevation of the sun.

In the right-angled triangle formed by the tree, its shadow, and the sun's ray:

tan(\(\theta\)) = Opposite / Adjacent = Height / Shadow Length

tan(\(\theta\)) = h / L = h / (3h) = 1/3.

\( \theta = \arctan(1/3) \).

We know tan(30°) = 1/√3 ≈ 1/1.732 ≈ 0.577.

We know tan(45°) = 1.

We know tan(60°) = √3 ≈ 1.732.

1/3 ≈ 0.333. This is smaller than tan(30°).

So the angle is less than 30°. None of the standard angles (30, 45, 60, 90) fit tan(θ)=1/3 exactly.

Could the question mean shadow is 1/3 times height? L = h/3. Then tan(θ) = h/(h/3) = 3. Angle would be arctan(3) ≈ 71.6°.

Could the question mean shadow is √3 times height? L = h√3. Then tan(θ) = h/(h√3) = 1/√3. Angle would be 30°.

Given that 30° is an option, it's highly likely the question intended the shadow length to be √3 times the height, not 3 times.

Answer: a. 30° (Assuming shadow length is √3 * height)

9. A and B can complete a task together in 8 days. If A alone can complete the task in 12 days, how many days will it take B to complete it alone? [A, B একত্রে একটি কাজ ৮ দিনে করতে পারে। যদি A একা কাজটি ১২ দিনে করতে পারে তাহলে B একা কাজটি কতদিনে শেষ করবে?]

  • 10 days
  • 16 days
  • 24 days
  • 30 days

Solution:

Let rates be \(R_A\) and \(R_B\) (tasks/day).

Combined rate \(R_{A+B} = R_A + R_B\).

Time taken together = 8 days => \(R_{A+B} = 1/8\).

Time taken by A alone = 12 days => \(R_A = 1/12\).

Substitute \(R_A\) into the combined rate equation:

\( 1/8 = R_A + R_B = 1/12 + R_B \)

\( R_B = 1/8 - 1/12 \)

Find common denominator (24):

\( R_B = 3/24 - 2/24 = 1/24 \) task/day.

Time taken by B alone = 1 / \(R_B\) = 1 / (1/24) = 24 days.

Answer: c. 24 days

    15. Triangles on the same base and between the same parallels are – [একই ভূমি এবং একই সমান্তরাল রেখার মধ্যে অবস্থিত ত্রিভুজগুলো-]

    • smaller in area

    05. For which value of a the function \( f(x) = \frac{2x+3}{5x+a} \) is the inverse function of itself? [a এর কোন মানের জন্য \( f(x) = \frac{2x+3}{5x+a} \) ফাংশনটি তার নিজের বিপরীত ফাংশন?]

    • 2
    • -3
    • 5
    • -2

    Solution:

    A function \( f(x) = \frac{px+q}{rx+s} \) is its own inverse if \( s = -p \).

    Here, \( p = 2, q = 3, r = 5, s = a \).

    For f(x) to be its own inverse, we need \( s = -p \).

    \( a = -2 \).

    Let's verify by finding the inverse:

    Let \( y = \frac{2x+3}{5x+a} \).

    Swap x and y: \( x = \frac{2y+3}{5y+a} \).

    \( x(5y+a) = 2y+3 \)

    \( 5xy + ax = 2y + 3 \)

    \( 5xy - 2y = 3 - ax \)

    \( y(5x - 2) = 3 - ax \)

    \( y = \frac{3 - ax}{5x - 2} = \frac{-ax + 3}{5x - 2} \).

    So, \( f^{-1}(x) = \frac{-ax + 3}{5x - 2} \).

    We want \( f(x) = f^{-1}(x) \):

    \( \frac{2x+3}{5x+a} = \frac{-ax + 3}{5x - 2} \).

    For this identity to hold, the corresponding coefficients must match (or be proportional). Comparing the numerator constant term (3) and the denominator coefficient of x (5), they match. We need the numerator x coefficient and the denominator constant term to match.

    Numerator x coeff: \( 2 = -a \implies a = -2 \).

    Denominator constant: \( a = -2 \).

    Both conditions give a = -2.

    Answer: d. -2

    06. For CO2 gas what is the ratio of isothermal slope and adiabatic slope? [CO2 গ্যাসের জন্য সমোষ্ণ ঢাল এবং রুদ্ধতাপীয় ঢালের অনুপাত কত?]

    • 4/3
    • 3/4
    • 7/5
    • 3/2

    Solution:

    For a P-V diagram:

    Isothermal process: PV = constant. Differentiating: P dV + V dP = 0 => \( \frac{dP}{dV} = -\frac{P}{V} \). Slope = \( -\frac{P}{V} \).

    Adiabatic process: PVγ = constant. Differentiating: \( P(\gamma V^{\gamma-1} dV) + V^\gamma dP = 0 \). Divide by \(V^{\gamma-1}\): \( P\gamma dV + V dP = 0 \Rightarrow \frac{dP}{dV} = -\frac{\gamma P}{V} \). Slope = \( -\frac{\gamma P}{V} \).

    Ratio = Adiabatic Slope / Isothermal Slope = \( \frac{-\gamma P / V}{-P / V} = \gamma \).

    The question asks for the ratio of isothermal slope *to* adiabatic slope.

    Ratio = Isothermal Slope / Adiabatic Slope = \( \frac{-P / V}{-\gamma P / V} = \frac{1}{\gamma} \).

    CO2 is a triatomic linear molecule at normal temperatures. Degrees of freedom (f) = 3 (trans) + 2 (rot) = 5. (Vibrational modes usually ignored).

    If f=5, \(\gamma = C_P/C_V = (\frac{f+2}{2}R) / (\frac{f}{2}R) = \frac{f+2}{f} = \frac{5+2}{5} = \frac{7}{5} = 1.4\).

    The required ratio is \( 1/\gamma = 1 / (7/5) = 5/7 \).

    However, CO2 sometimes uses vibrational modes, making f=6 or 7. If f=6 (linear, +1 vib?), \(\gamma=8/6=4/3\). Ratio=3/4. If f=7 (non-linear?), \(\gamma=9/7\). Ratio=7/9.

    Let's assume f=5 is expected. Ratio = 5/7. Not in options.

    Let's assume f=6 is expected (linear with vibration?). \(\gamma=4/3\). Ratio = 3/4. This matches option b.

    If the question intended Adiabatic/Isothermal ratio, then \(\gamma=7/5=1.4\) (option c) or \(\gamma=4/3 \approx 1.33\) (option a).

    Given the options, it's likely the calculation for CO2 uses f=6 (or considers it generally polyatomic where γ ≈ 4/3), and asks for Isothermal/Adiabatic ratio = 1/γ = 3/4.

    Answer: b. 3/4

    08. A rope can bear maximum 306 N tension. What can be the maximum acceleration of a boy of mass 45 kg if he wants to go downward with the help of the rope? [একটি দড়ি সর্বোচ্চ 306 N টান সহ্য করতে পারে। 45 কেজি ভরের একটি বালক যদি দড়ির সাহায্যে নিচের দিকে যেতে চায় তাহলে তার সর্বোচ্চ ত্বরণ কত হতে পারে?]

    • 1 m/s2
    • 2 m/s2
    • 3 m/s2
    • 4 m/s2

    Solution:

    Mass of boy (m) = 45 kg.

    Maximum tension the rope can bear (T_max) = 306 N.

    Weight of the boy (W) = mg. Use g ≈ 9.8 m/s2.

    W = 45 kg × 9.8 m/s2 = 441 N.

    The boy wants to go downward with acceleration 'a'. The forces acting are:

    • Weight (W) downwards
    • Tension (T) upwards

    Net downward force = W - T.

    According to Newton's Second Law: F_net = ma.

    W - T = ma.

    The tension T must not exceed T_max = 306 N.

    To find the *maximum* downward acceleration, we consider the case where the tension is at its *minimum* required value to control the descent, but the question implies the rope *limits* the motion. Let's re-read. The rope can bear a maximum tension. If he accelerates downwards, the tension will be *less* than his weight.

    T = W - ma = mg - ma.

    We need T ≤ T_max.

    mg - ma ≤ T_max

    441 - 45a ≤ 306

    441 - 306 ≤ 45a

    135 ≤ 45a

    a ≥ 135 / 45 = 3 m/s2.

    This means the minimum acceleration needed to keep tension below max is 3 m/s^2. What is the *maximum* acceleration?

    The question phrasing is slightly ambiguous. Does it mean what's the max acceleration *the boy can achieve* while holding the rope (limited by strength?), or what's the max acceleration *allowed* before the rope breaks?

    If it means the maximum acceleration the rope allows *if he were pulled upwards*, then T_max = m(g+a) => 306 = 45(9.8+a) => 306/45 = 9.8+a => 6.8 = 9.8+a => a = -3 m/s^2 (upward deceleration / downward acceleration).

    Let's stick to the initial interpretation: boy going downward. Net force = mg - T = ma. To maximize 'a', we need to minimize T. The minimum possible tension is 0 (free fall, a=g). But the rope can withstand up to 306N. Any downward acceleration \(a\) where \(a < g\) is possible as long as \(T = m(g-a) \le 306\). Since \(mg=441N > 306N\), the boy cannot even hang stationary without breaking the rope. The premise seems flawed, unless he's using it to slow his descent from freefall.

    Let's assume the question means what is the maximum acceleration such that the tension *equals* 306N?

    If T=306N, then \(mg - T = ma\). \(441 - 306 = 45a\). \(135 = 45a\). \(a = 3\) m/s^2.

    This represents the *minimum* downward acceleration required for the tension to *not exceed* 306N. Any acceleration greater than this would result in less tension. So the boy *can* accelerate downwards at any rate up to g (freefall), but if he wants to use the rope to control his descent, the tension might exceed 306N if he brakes too hard (accelerates upwards / decelerates downwards too much). If he accelerates downwards at 3 m/s^2, the tension is exactly 306N. If he accelerates downwards faster (e.g., 4 m/s^2), the tension is lower (\(441 - 45*4 = 441-180=261N\)). The rope won't break.

    The phrasing "What can be the maximum acceleration" is ambiguous. Maybe it means the maximum acceleration where the rope is still taut and providing some support (i.e., T>0)? This occurs for a < g. Maybe it means the minimum acceleration he needs so the rope doesn't break? That is a>=3. Let's assume the question is asking for the acceleration 'a' when T=T_max.

    Answer: c. 3 m/s2

    09. In the vector 4i + 2j + 8k, what is the length in xy plane? [4i + 2j + 8k ভেক্টরে, xy সমতলে দৈর্ঘ্য কত?]

    • √10
    • b. 2√10
    • c. 2√5
    • d. √5

    Solution:

    The vector is \( \vec{V} = 4\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 8\hat{k} \).

    The "length in the xy plane" refers to the magnitude of the vector's projection onto the xy plane.

    The projection onto the xy plane is obtained by setting the z-component (k-component) to zero.

    Projection vector \( \vec{V}_{xy} = 4\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 0\hat{k} = 4\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} \).

    The length (magnitude) of this projection is:

    \( |\vec{V}_{xy}| = \sqrt{(\text{x-component})^2 + (\text{y-component})^2} \)

    \( |\vec{V}_{xy}| = \sqrt{4^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{16 + 4} = \sqrt{20} \).

    Simplify \(\sqrt{20}\): \( \sqrt{20} = \sqrt{4 \times 5} = 2\sqrt{5} \).

    Answer: c. 2√5

    10. A wooden block is pulling with an angle of 60° and Force of 200N. What will be the effective force in the horizontal direction? [একটি কাঠের ব্লক 60° কোণে এবং 200N বল দ্বারা টানা হচ্ছে। অনুভূমিক দিকে কার্যকর বল কত হবে?]

    • 200 N
    • 500 N
    • 100 N
    • 300 N

    Solution:

    The force F = 200 N is applied at an angle of 60° (presumably relative to the horizontal, although not explicitly stated - this is standard convention).

    The horizontal component of the force (\(F_x\)) is given by:

    \( F_x = F \cos\theta \)

    \( F_x = 200 \times \cos(60^\circ) \)
