Model Question - 10

Section A – [English Grammer]

Fill in the blanks:

The shepherds of the North Caucasus region of Europe 1. were only trying to 2. carry milk the 3. best way they knew how – in leather 4. pouches strapped to the side of donkeys – when they 5. made a 6. significant discovery. A fermentation 7. process would sometimes 8. inadvertently occur en route, and when the pouches were 9. opened upon arrival they would no longer 10. contain milk but rather a pungent, effervescent, low alcoholic substance instead.

1. shepherds ... ______ only trying

Options:

  • A. be
  • B. is
  • C. was
  • D. were

Solution: The subject "shepherds" is plural, and the context implies past tense. Therefore, the plural past tense verb "were" is required.

Answer: D. were

2. trying to ______ milk

Options:

  • A. bring
  • B. carry
  • C. have
  • D. transfer

Solution: The shepherds were transporting milk. "Carry" fits the context of moving milk in pouches.

Answer: B. carry

3. the ______ way they knew how

Options:

  • A. easier
  • B. best
  • C. effective
  • D. none

Solution: The phrase "the best way they knew how" is idiomatic, indicating the most effective method available to them.

Answer: B. best

4. in leather ______ strapped

Options:

  • A. pouches
  • B. bags
  • C. things
  • D. none

Solution: Leather containers used for carrying liquids are often called "pouches".

Answer: A. pouches

5. when they ______ a

Options:

  • A. made
  • B. had
  • C. have
  • D. showed

Solution: The standard collocation is "made a discovery". Past tense is required.

Answer: A. made

6. a ______ discovery

Options:

  • A. splendid
  • B. good
  • C. weird
  • D. significant

Solution: The discovery led to a new substance (like kefir), implying it was important or "significant".

Answer: D. significant

7. A fermentation ______

Options:

  • A. way
  • B. thing
  • C. process
  • D. none

Solution: Fermentation is a chemical or biological "process".

Answer: C. process

8. would sometimes ______ occur

Options:

  • A. have
  • B. is
  • C. inadvertently
  • D. specifically

Solution: The fermentation was accidental, not planned. The adverb "inadvertently" (unintentionally) modifies "occur".

Answer: C. inadvertently

9. when the pouches were ______ upon arrival

Options:

  • A. ready
  • B. made
  • C. open
  • D. opened

Solution: The passive voice "were opened" indicates the action taken upon arrival.

Answer: D. opened

10. no longer ______ milk

Options:

  • A. have
  • B. contains
  • C. Contain
  • D. has

Solution: The subject "they" (referring to the pouches) requires the base form of the verb after "would no longer". "Contain" is the appropriate verb.

Answer: C. Contain

  • b. Pleasant

Options:

  • a. Have
  • b. Has
  • c. is
  • d. Was

Solution: The subject is "The slaughter" (singular). The verb phrase "caused disturbance" suggests a completed action with ongoing relevance or a recent past event. The present perfect "has caused" fits this context.

13. Each of the chefs ______ responsible for their cooking.

Options:

  • a. Is
  • b. Had
  • c. were
  • d. Are

Solution: "Each" is a singular pronoun, requiring a singular verb. "Is" is the singular present tense verb.

Answer: a. Is

14. “Bus” is -

Options:

  • a. Common Noun
  • b. Abstract
  • c. uncountable
  • d. None

Solution: A "bus" is a general name for a type of vehicle, representing a class of objects. This makes it a common noun.

Answer: a. Common Noun

15. I am ______ my way.

Options:

  • a. At
  • b. In
  • c. towards
  • d. On

Solution: The correct idiom is "on my way", meaning in the process of travelling somewhere.

Section C – [General Math]

08. In a family of 10 members, 7 of them like tea or coffee, 4 of them like tea and 5 of them like coffee. How many of them like only coffee? [১০ জন সদস্যের একটি পরিবারে ৭ জন চা অথবা কফি, ৪ জন চা এবং ৫ জন কফি পছন্দ করে। তাদের কতজন কেবলমাত্র কফি পছন্দ করে?]

Options:

  • a. 2
  • b. 3
  • c. 4
  • d. 5

Solution:

Let T be the set of members who like tea, and C be the set of members who like coffee.

Total members = 10.

Number who like tea or coffee, \(|T \cup C| = 7\).

Number who like tea, \(|T| = 4\).

Number who like coffee, \(|C| = 5\).

We use the formula: \(|T \cup C| = |T| + |C| - |T \cap C|\)

\[ 7 = 4 + 5 - |T \cap C| \]

\[ 7 = 9 - |T \cap C| \]

\[ |T \cap C| = 9 - 7 = 2 \]

This means 2 members like both tea and coffee.

Number who like only coffee = (Number who like coffee) - (Number who like both)

Only Coffee = \(|C| - |T \cap C| = 5 - 2 = 3\)

Answer: b. 3

09. Two pipes, P and Q can fill a cistern in 12 and 15 minutes respectively. Both are opened together, but at the end of 3 minutes, P is turned off. In how many more minutes will Q fill the cistern? [দুটি পাইপ P ও Q একটি জলাধারকে যথাক্রমে ১২ ও ১৫ মিনিটে পূর্ণ করতে পারে। দুটিকে একত্রে খুলে দেওয়া হলো কিন্তু ৩ মিনিট পর P বন্ধ করে দেওয়া হয়। আর কত সময়ে Q দ্বারা জলাধারটি পূর্ণ হবে?]

Options:

  • a. 7 minutes
  • b. 7.5 minutes
  • c. 8 minutes
  • d. 8 ¼ minutes

Solution:

Rate of pipe P = 1/12 of cistern per minute.

Rate of pipe Q = 1/15 of cistern per minute.

Combined rate of P and Q = \( \frac{1}{12} + \frac{1}{15} = \frac{5+4}{60} = \frac{9}{60} = \frac{3}{20} \) of cistern per minute.

In the first 3 minutes, work done by P and Q together = Rate × Time = \( \frac{3}{20} \times 3 = \frac{9}{20} \) of the cistern.

Remaining work = \( 1 - \frac{9}{20} = \frac{11}{20} \) of the cistern.

Time taken by Q to fill the remaining work = \(\frac{\text{Remaining Work}}{\text{Rate of Q}}\) = \( \frac{11/20}{1/15} = \frac{11}{20} \times 15 = \frac{165}{20} = \frac{33}{4} \) minutes.

\[ \frac{33}{4} = 8 \frac{1}{4} \, \text{minutes} \]

Answer: d. 8 ¼ minutes

10. A cistern can be filled by two pipes in 20 and 30 minutes respectively. Both pipes being opened, when the first pipe must be turned off so that the cistern may be filled in 10 minutes more. [একটি জলাধার দুইটি পাইপ দিয়ে যথাক্রমে ২০ ও ৩০ মিনিটে পূর্ণ করা যায়। দুইটি পাইপই একত্রে খুলে দেওয়া হলো। প্রথম পাইপ কখন বন্ধ করলে জলাধারটি আর ১০ মিনিটে পূর্ণ হবে?]

Options:

  • a. After 10 minutes
  • b. After 12 minutes
  • c. After 20 minutes
  • d. After 8 minutes

Solution:

Let pipe 1 rate = 1/20 per min. Let pipe 2 rate = 1/30 per min.

Let the first pipe be turned off after 't' minutes. The second pipe runs for the entire duration needed to fill the remaining part in 10 minutes.

Work done by pipe 2 in the last 10 minutes = \( 10 \times \frac{1}{30} = \frac{1}{3} \).

Work done by both pipes together before pipe 1 was turned off = \( 1 - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{2}{3} \).

Combined rate of pipe 1 and pipe 2 = \( \frac{1}{20} + \frac{1}{30} = \frac{3+2}{60} = \frac{5}{60} = \frac{1}{12} \) per minute.

Time 't' for which both pipes were open = \(\frac{\text{Work Done Together}}{\text{Combined Rate}} = \frac{2/3}{1/12} = \frac{2}{3} \times 12 = 2 \times 4 = 8\) minutes.

Answer: d. After 8 minutes

11. The ratio of water and milk in a 30-liter mixture is 7 : 3. Find the quantity of water to be added to the mixture to make this ratio 6 : 1. [৩০ লিটারের একটি মিশ্রণে পানি ও দুধের অনুপাত ৭:৩। আর কি পরিমাণ পানি মিশালে এই অনুপাত ৬:১ হবে?]

Options:

  • a. 30
  • b. 32
  • c. 33
  • d. 35

Solution:

Total mixture = 30 liters. Ratio W:M = 7:3. Sum of parts = 10.

Initial Water = \( \frac{7}{10} \times 30 = 21 \) liters.

Initial Milk = \( \frac{3}{10} \times 30 = 9 \) liters.

Let 'x' liters of water be added. New Water = \( 21 + x \). Milk remains 9 liters.

New ratio (W+x) : M = 6 : 1.

\[ \frac{21 + x}{9} = \frac{6}{1} \implies 21 + x = 54 \implies x = 33 \]

Answer: c. 33

12. The incomes of A and B are in the ratio 3 : 2 and their expenditure are in ratio 5 : 3. If each saves Tk. 1000, then, A's income can be: [A ও B এর আয়ের অনুপাত ৩:২ এবং তাদের ব্যয়ের অনুপাত ৫:৩। যদি প্রত্যেকে ১০০০ টাকা সঞ্চয় করে তাহলে A এর আয় হতে পারে-]

Options:

  • a. Tk. 3000
  • b. Tk. 4000
  • c. Tk. 6000
  • d. Tk. 9000

Solution:

Let incomes be 3x, 2x. Let expenditures be 5y, 3y.

Savings = Income - Expenditure.

A: \( 3x - 5y = 1000 \) (1)

B: \( 2x - 3y = 1000 \) (2)

Multiply (1) by 3: \( 9x - 15y = 3000 \)

Multiply (2) by 5: \( 10x - 15y = 5000 \)

Subtract the first modified eq from the second: \( (10x - 15y) - (9x - 15y) = 5000 - 3000 \implies x = 2000 \).

A's income = 3x = \( 3 \times 2000 = 6000 \) Tk.

Answer: c. Tk. 6000

13. \(2 \tan 30^\circ / (1 + \tan^2 30^\circ) = ?\)

Options:

  • a. \(\sin 60^\circ\)
  • b. \(\cos 60^\circ\)
  • c. \(\tan 60^\circ\)
  • d. \(\sin 30^\circ\)

Solution:

Using the identity \(\sin(2\theta) = \frac{2 \tan \theta}{1 + \tan^2 \theta}\). Let \(\theta = 30^\circ\). The expression equals \(\sin(2 \times 30^\circ) = \sin 60^\circ\).

Answer: a. \(\sin 60^\circ\)

14. \(\sin 2A = 2 \sin A\) is true when A =

Options:

  • a. 30°
  • b. 45°
  • c. 0°
  • d. 60°

Solution:

Using \(\sin 2A = 2 \sin A \cos A\), the equation becomes \(2 \sin A \cos A = 2 \sin A\). \(2 \sin A (\cos A - 1) = 0\). This holds if \(\sin A = 0\) (A = 0°, 180°, ...) or \(\cos A = 1\) (A = 0°, 360°, ...). From the options, A = 0° works.

Answer: c. 0°

15. If 5 is added to twice a number, the result is 29. Determine the number. [যদি একটি সংখ্যার দ্বিগুণের সাথে ৫ যোগ করা হয়, ফলাফল হয় ২৯। সংখ্যাটি কত?]

Options:

  • a. 22
  • b. 23
  • c. 28
  • d. 12

Solution:

Let the number be x. \(2x + 5 = 29 \implies 2x = 24 \implies x = 12\).

Answer: d. 12

Section D – [Higher Math & Physics]

01. Evaluate \(\int dx\)

Options:

  • a. 1
  • b. c
  • c. x + c
  • d. x\(^2\)

Solution:

The integral of dx is the integral of \(1 \cdot dx\). Using the power rule for \(x^0\), \(\int x^0 \, dx = \frac{x^{0+1}}{0+1} + c = x + c\).

Answer: c. x + c

02. What is the value of \(\frac{d}{dx} (\ln(e^x))\)? [\(\frac{d}{dx} (\ln(e^x))\) এর মান কোনটি?]

Options:

  • a. \(e^x/\ln x\)
  • b. \(e^x\)
  • c. 1
  • d. 0

Solution:

Simplify the expression first: \(\ln(e^x) = x \ln(e) = x \times 1 = x\).

Then, \(\frac{d}{dx}(x) = 1\).

Answer: c. 1

03. What is the value of \(\lim_{x \to \infty} \left(\frac{2x}{x+1}\right)\)? [\(\lim_{x \to \infty} (\frac{2x}{x+1})\) এর মান কোনটি?]

Options:

  • a. 1
  • b. 2
  • c. 0
  • d. -1

Solution:

Divide numerator and denominator by the highest power of x, which is x:

\[ \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{2x/x}{(x/x) + (1/x)} = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{2}{1 + 1/x} \]

As \(x \to \infty\), \(1/x \to 0\).

\[ \text{Limit} = \frac{2}{1 + 0} = 2 \]

Answer: b. 2

04. What is the center of the circle which's equation is \(x^2 + y^2 + 2x + 3y + 5 = 0\)? [\(x^2 + y^2 + 2x + 3y + 5 = 0\), এই বৃত্তের কেন্দ্র কোনটি?]

Options:

  • a. (1, 2)
  • b. (2, 1)
  • c. (3, 2)
  • d. (-1, -3/2)

Solution:

The general equation of a circle is \(x^2 + y^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0\), where the center is \((-g, -f)\).

Comparing with the given equation \(x^2 + y^2 + 2x + 3y + 5 = 0\):

2g = 2 \(\implies g = 1\)

2f = 3 \(\implies f = 3/2\)

The center is \((-g, -f) = (-1, -3/2)\).

Answer: d. (-1, -3/2)

05. If \(f(x) = \frac{3x+2}{4x+3}\) then what which one of the following will be \(f^{-1}(x)\)? [যদি \(f(x) = \frac{3x+2}{4x+3}\) হয়, তাহলে নিচের কোনটি \(f^{-1}(x)\)?]

Options:

  • a. \(\frac{5x+2}{4x+3}\)
  • b. \(\frac{-3x+2}{4x-3}\)
  • c. \(\frac{5x-2}{4x+3}\)
  • d. \(\frac{3x-2}{3-4x}\)

Solution:

Let \(y = f(x) = \frac{3x+2}{4x+3}\).

To find the inverse, swap x and y and solve for y:

\[ x = \frac{3y+2}{4y+3} \]

\[ x(4y+3) = 3y+2 \]

\[ 4xy + 3x = 3y + 2 \]

\[ 4xy - 3y = 2 - 3x \]

\[ y(4x - 3) = 2 - 3x \]

\[ y = f^{-1}(x) = \frac{2 - 3x}{4x - 3} \]

Check the options. Option d is \(\frac{3x-2}{3-4x} = \frac{-(2-3x)}{-(4x-3)} = \frac{2-3x}{4x-3}\). This matches.

Answer: d. \(\frac{3x-2}{3-4x}\)

06. What is the equation for Huygens' principle? [হাইগেনসের নীতির সমীকরণ কী?]

Options:

  • a. d = vt
  • b. dSinθ =nλ
  • c. v = st
  • d. d = ½ at\(^2\)

Solution:

Huygens' principle is a conceptual model describing wave propagation. It doesn't have a single simple algebraic equation named after it in the same way Newton's laws do. However, consequences derived from it, like the condition for interference or diffraction, do have equations. The equation \(d \sin \theta = n \lambda\) describes the condition for constructive interference in phenomena like diffraction gratings or double slits, which can be explained using Huygens' principle. Options a, c, d are basic kinematic equations.

Answer: b. dSinθ =nλ (as the most relevant wave optics equation related to Huygens' ideas)

07. The position vector of the point (1, 2, 0) is: [(1, 2, 0) বিন্দুর অবস্থান ভেক্টর হল?]

Options:

  • a. i + j + k
  • b. i + 2j + k
  • c. i + 2j
  • d. 2j + k

Solution:

The position vector of a point (x, y, z) relative to the origin is given by \(x\mathbf{i} + y\mathbf{j} + z\mathbf{k}\).

For the point (1, 2, 0), the position vector is \(1\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 0\mathbf{k} = \mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j}\).

Answer: c. i + 2j

08. Which of the following is the correct formula for calculating the moment of inertia of a solid sphere of radius r and mass m about its diameter? [r ব্যাসার্ধের একটি কঠিন গোলকের মোমেন্ট অফ ইনার্শিয়া এবং এর ব্যাস সম্পর্কে m ভরের হিসাব করার জন্য নিচের কোন সূত্রটি সঠিক?]

Options:

  • a. I = (2/5)mr\(^2\)
  • b. I = (2/3)mr\(^2\)
  • c. I = (1/2)mr\(^2\)
  • d. I = mr\(^2\)

Solution:

The moment of inertia (I) of a solid sphere of mass m and radius r about an axis passing through its center (i.e., about a diameter) is given by the formula \(I = \frac{2}{5}mr^2\).

  • (2/3)mr\(^2\) is for a thin spherical shell about its diameter.
  • (1/2)mr\(^2\) is for a solid cylinder or disk about its central axis.
  • mr\(^2\) is for a point mass or a thin ring about its center.

Answer: a. I = (2/5)mr\(^2\)

09. A book of mass 1 kg is lifted to a height of 2 m. What is the potential energy of the book? [কেজি ভরের একটি বইকে ২ মিটার উচ্চতায় তোলা হয়। বইয়ের সম্ভাব্য শক্তি কত?]

Options:

  • a. 2 J
  • b. 9.8 J
  • c. 19.6 J
  • d. 20 J

Solution:

Gravitational Potential Energy (PE) is given by \(PE = mgh\).

Mass \(m = 1\) kg.

Height \(h = 2\) m.

Acceleration due to gravity \(g \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2\).

\[ PE = (1 \, \text{kg}) \times (9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2) \times (2 \, \text{m}) = 19.6 \, \text{J} \]

Answer: c. 19.6 J

10. The scale of temperature in which the temperature is only positive is? [তাপমাত্রার স্কেল যেখানে তাপমাত্রা শুধুমাত্র ধনাত্মক হয়?]

Options:

  • a. Farenheit
  • b. Celcius
  • c. Kelvin
  • d. Reaumur

Solution:

The Kelvin scale is an absolute temperature scale, meaning its zero point (0 K) corresponds to absolute zero, the theoretical lowest possible temperature where molecular motion ceases. All temperatures on the Kelvin scale are non-negative (0 K or positive). Fahrenheit and Celsius scales have negative values.

Answer: c. Kelvin

Section E – [Biology & Chemistry]

01. In which phase of cell division process, the chromosomes move towards the pole? [কোষ বিভাজনের কোন পর্যায়ে ক্রোমোজোমগুলি মেরুর দিকে সরে যায়?]

Options:

  • A. Prophase
  • B. Anaphase
  • C. Metaphase
  • D. Telophase

Solution:

During Anaphase of mitosis and meiosis II, sister chromatids separate and are pulled towards opposite poles of the cell by the spindle fibers. During Anaphase I of meiosis, homologous chromosomes separate and move towards opposite poles.

Answer: B. Anaphase

02. The general formula of Carbohydrates is- [কার্বোহাইড্রেটের সাধারণ সূত্র হল-]

Options:

  • A. (CH\(_{2}\)O)\(_{n}\)
  • B. (C\(_{n}\)H\(_{2}\)O)\(_{n}\)
  • C. (C\(_{n}\)H\(_{2n}\)O)\(_{n}\) COOH
  • D. (C\(_{n}\)H\(_{2n}\)O)\(_{n}\)

Solution:

Many simple carbohydrates (monosaccharides and disaccharides) have the empirical formula C\(_{m}\)(H\(_{2}\)O)\(_{n}\), often simplified to (CH\(_{2}\)O)\(_{n}\) when m=n (like glucose C\(_{6}\)H\(_{12}\)O\(_{6}\) which fits (CH\(_{2}\)O)\(_{6}\)). This represents the general ratio of Carbon to Hydrogen to Oxygen as 1:2:1. While not all carbohydrates strictly adhere to this (e.g., deoxyribose), it's the most common general representation.

Answer: A. (CH\(_{2}\)O)\(_{n}\)

03. Which one of the following is a viral disease? [নিচের কোনটি ভাইরাস জনিত রোগ?]

Options:

  • A. Malaria
  • B. Blight of Rice

Solution:

  • Ring Spot of Papaya: Caused by Papaya ringspot virus (PRSV).

05. Which one of the following is a diploid organism? [কোনটি ডিপ্লয়েড জীব?]

Options:

  • A. Riccia
  • B. Pteris
  • C. Ulothrix
  • D. Agaricus

Solution:

In plant life cycles involving alternation of generations:

  • Riccia (a bryophyte): The dominant, free-living stage is the haploid gametophyte. The diploid sporophyte is dependent on it.
  • Pteris (a fern, pteridophyte): The dominant, free-living stage is the diploid sporophyte (the leafy fern plant). The gametophyte (prothallus) is small and haploid.
  • Ulothrix (green algae): Primarily exists as a haploid filament; the zygote is the only diploid stage.
  • Agaricus (a fungus): Typically has a complex life cycle involving haploid and dikaryotic stages; the main vegetative mycelium is often haploid or dikaryotic.

Pteris represents the diploid sporophyte generation as the main organism.

Answer: B. Pteris

06. Corrosive chemical substances are marked with-

Options:

  • A. Flammable
  • B. Hazard
  • C. Non-toxic
  • D. Dangerous

Solution:

Safety data sheets and labels use standardized symbols (pictograms) and signal words. Corrosive substances, which can damage skin, eyes, or materials, are typically marked with a specific "Corrosive" pictogram (showing liquid damaging a hand and a surface) and signal words like "Danger" or "Warning". While they are certainly a type of "Hazard" and can be "Dangerous", the specific marking identifies the corrosive nature. Among the inadequate options, "Hazard" or "Dangerous" are general terms. The internationally recognized GHS symbol is specifically for "Corrosion".

Answer: B. Hazard / D. Dangerous (as general descriptors, though specific labels say "Corrosive")

07. Which group of elements are called chalcogens? [কোন গ্রুপের মৌলকে চ্যালকোজেন বলা হয়?]

Options:

  • A. Group 13
  • B. Group 14
  • C. Group 15
  • D. Group 16

Solution:

The elements in Group 16 of the periodic table are known as the chalcogens. This group includes Oxygen (O), Sulfur (S), Selenium (Se), Tellurium (Te), and Polonium (Po).

  • Group 13: Boron group
  • Group 14: Carbon group
  • Group 15: Pnictogens (Nitrogen group)
  • Group 16: Chalcogens (Oxygen group)
  • Group 17: Halogens
  • Group 18: Noble gases

Answer: D. Group 16

08. Which is the functional group of aldehyde? [কোনটি অ্যালডিহাইডের কার্যকরীমূলক?]

Options:

  • A. —COOH
  • B. —OH
  • C. —CO.
  • D. —CHO

Solution:

The functional group characteristic of aldehydes is the formyl group, written as —CHO. It consists of a carbonyl group (C=O) bonded to a hydrogen atom and an R group (or another hydrogen in formaldehyde).

  • —COOH: Carboxyl group (Carboxylic acid)
  • —OH: Hydroxyl group (Alcohol/Phenol)
  • —CO—: Carbonyl group within a molecule (Ketone)
  • —CHO: Formyl group (Aldehyde)

Answer: D. —CHO

09. What is the range for safe water? [নিরাপদ পানির সীমা কত?]

Options:

  • A. If BOD is less than 5 ppm
  • B. If BOD is less than 15 ppm
  • C. If BOD is less than 50 ppm
  • D. If BOD is less than 100 ppm

Solution:

Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) measures the amount of dissolved oxygen needed by microorganisms to break down organic material in water. High BOD indicates high levels of organic pollution.

  • BOD < 1 ppm: Very clean, suitable for drinking.
  • BOD 1-5 ppm: Generally considered good quality, often suitable for swimming/recreation.
  • BOD > 5 ppm: Indicates increasing levels of pollution.
  • BOD > 15-20 ppm: Often indicates significant pollution.

For general "safe water" (implying suitable for recreational use or generally low pollution), BOD less than 5 ppm is a common benchmark.

Answer: A. If BOD is less than 5 ppm

10. Which is not an oxidizing agent? [নিচের কোনটি জারক নয়?]

Options:

  • A. CO
  • B. MnO2
  • C. H2
  • D. H2O2

Solution:

An oxidizing agent causes oxidation in another substance and is itself reduced. A substance that primarily acts as a reducing agent is typically not considered an oxidizing agent.

  • CO (Carbon Monoxide): Carbon is in +2 state, can be oxidized to +4 (CO2). CO is a well-known reducing agent, especially in metallurgy. It rarely acts as an oxidant.
  • MnO2 (Manganese Dioxide): Mn is +4. Can be reduced (e.g., to Mn+2). Common oxidizing agent.
  • H2 (Hydrogen gas): H is 0. Can be oxidized to +1 (H+). Common reducing agent. Can act as an oxidant only in forming hydrides (H-), which is less common.
  • H2O2 (Hydrogen Peroxide): O is -1. Can be reduced to -2 (oxidizing agent) or oxidized to 0 (reducing agent). Acts as both.

Both CO and H2 are primarily reducing agents. CO is arguably less likely to act as an oxidant than H2. In many contexts, CO is the intended answer as something that is *not* an oxidizing agent.

Answer: A. CO
