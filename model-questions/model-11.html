Section C – [General Math]

1. When a blanket is bleached, its length shrinks by 20% and its breadth decreases by 15%. What is the percentage decrease in the area of the blanket? [যখন একটি কম্বল ব্লিচ করা হয়, এর দৈর্ঘ্য ২০% ও প্রস্থ ১৫% হ্রাস পায়। কম্বলের ক্ষেত্রফল কত শতাংশ হ্রাস পায়?]

Options:

  • a. 32%
  • b. 42%
  • c. 35%
  • d. 45%

Solution:

Let original Length = L, original Breadth = B.

Original Area A = L × B.

New Length L' = L - 0.20 L = 0.80 L.

New Breadth B' = B - 0.15 B = 0.85 B.

New Area A' = L' × B' = (0.80 L) × (0.85 B) = (0.80 × 0.85) × (L × B).

A' = 0.68 × A.

Decrease in Area = A - A' = A - 0.68 A = 0.32 A.

Percentage Decrease = \(\frac{\text{Decrease in Area}}{\text{Original Area}} \times 100\% = \frac{0.32 A}{A} \times 100\% = 32\%\).

Answer: a. 32%

2. The area of a parallelogram is 162 square meters, and its altitude is twice the length of its corresponding base. What is the length of the base? [একটি সামান্তরিকের ক্ষেত্রফল ১৬২ বর্গমিটার এবং এর একটি বাহুর দৈর্ঘ্য সংশ্লিষ্ট ভূমির দৈর্ঘ্যের দ্বিগুণ। ভূমির দৈর্ঘ্য কত?]

Options:

  • a. 8 m
  • b. 7 m
  • c. 9 m
  • d. 10 m

Solution:

Let the base be b meters.

Let the altitude (height) corresponding to that base be h meters.

Given: Altitude is twice the base, \(h = 2b\).

Area of parallelogram = base × altitude = \(b \times h\).

Given Area = 162 m\(^2\).

\[ b \times h = 162 \]

Substitute \(h = 2b\):

\[ b \times (2b) = 162 \]

\[ 2b^2 = 162 \]

\[ b^2 = \frac{162}{2} = 81 \]

\[ b = \sqrt{81} = 9 \, \text{meters} \]

The length of the base is 9 m.

Answer: c. 9 m

3. 5000 tk kept in a bank that gives 10% simple interest annually. What will be the total amount after 2 years? [৫০০০ টাকা এমন একটি ব্যাংকে রাখা হলো যেটি বছরে ১০% সরল সুদ দেয়। ২ বছর পর সর্বমোট টাকা কত হবে?]

Options:

  • a. 5500
  • b. 5750
  • c. 6000
  • d. 6050

Solution:

Principal (P) = 5000 Tk.

Rate (R) = 10% per annum = 0.10.

Time (T) = 2 years.

Simple Interest (SI) = P × R × T

\[ SI = 5000 \times 0.10 \times 2 = 500 \times 2 = 1000 \, \text{Tk.} \]

Total Amount = Principal + Simple Interest

Total Amount = 5000 + 1000 = 6000 Tk.

Answer: c. 6000

4. A number when 35 is subtracted from it becomes 80%. What is four-fifths of that number? [একটি সংখ্যা থেকে ৩৫ বিয়োগ করাতে সংখ্যাটি হয়ে যায় তার ৮০%। সংখ্যাটির ৪/৫ অংশ কত?]

Options:

  • a. 70
  • b. 90
  • c. 120
  • d. 140

Solution:

Let the number be x.

Subtracting 35 gives \(x - 35\).

This result is 80% of the original number: \(x - 35 = 0.80 x\).

Solve for x:

\[ x - 0.80 x = 35 \]

\[ 0.20 x = 35 \]

\[ x = \frac{35}{0.20} = \frac{35}{1/5} = 35 \times 5 = 175 \]

The number is 175.

We need to find four-fifths (4/5) of the number:

\[ \frac{4}{5} \times 175 = 4 \times (175 \div 5) = 4 \times 35 = 140 \]

Answer: d. 140

5. A trader mixes 26 kg of rice at Tk. 20 per kg with 30 kg of rice of other variety at Tk. 36 per kg and sells the mixture at Tk. 30 per kg. His profit percent is: [একজন বিক্রেতা প্রতি কেজি ২০ টাকা দরের ২৬ কেজি ও প্রতি কেজি ৩৬ টাকা দরের ৩০ কেজি চাল একত্রে মিশ্রিত করে। মিশ্রিত চাল প্রতি কেজি ৩০ টাকা দরে বিক্রয় করে। তার শতকরা লাভ-]

Options:

  • a. No profit/loss
  • b. 5%
  • c. 8%
  • d. 10%

Solution:

Cost of first type of rice = 26 kg × 20 Tk/kg = 520 Tk.

Cost of second type of rice = 30 kg × 36 Tk/kg = 1080 Tk.

Total Cost Price (CP) = 520 + 1080 = 1600 Tk.

Total quantity of mixture = 26 kg + 30 kg = 56 kg.

Selling Price (SP) of mixture = 56 kg × 30 Tk/kg = 1680 Tk.

Profit = SP - CP = 1680 - 1600 = 80 Tk.

Profit Percent = \(\frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{CP}} \times 100\% = \frac{80}{1600} \times 100\% \)

Profit Percent = \(\frac{8}{160} \times 100\% = \frac{1}{20} \times 100\% = 5\%\).

Answer: b. 5%

6. By selling 33 meters of cloth, one gains the selling price of 11 meters. What's the gain percent? [৩৩ মিটার কাপড় বিক্রি করায় ১১ মিটার কাপড়ের বিক্রয়মূল্য পাওয়া গেল। লাভের হার কত?]

Options:

  • a. 50
  • b. 45
  • c. 40
  • d. 60

Solution:

Let CP be the cost price per meter and SP be the selling price per meter.

Selling 33 meters: Total SP = 33 × SP. Total CP = 33 × CP.

Gain = Total SP - Total CP = (33 × SP) - (33 × CP).

Given: Gain = Selling Price of 11 meters = 11 × SP.

Equate the two expressions for Gain:

\[ 11 \times SP = (33 \times SP) - (33 \times CP) \]

\[ 33 \times CP = 33 \times SP - 11 \times SP \]

\[ 33 \times CP = 22 \times SP \]

\[ \frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{33}{22} = \frac{3}{2} = 1.5 \]

Gain Percent = \( \frac{\text{Gain}}{\text{CP}} \times 100\% = \frac{SP - CP}{CP} \times 100\% \)

Gain Percent = \( \left( \frac{SP}{CP} - 1 \right) \times 100\% = (1.5 - 1) \times 100\% = 0.5 \times 100\% = 50\% \).

Answer: a. 50

7. An ant climbing up a vertical pole ascends 12 meters and slips down 5 meters in every alternate hour. If the pole is 63 meters high how long will it take it to reach the top? [একটি পিপড়া একটি খাড়া খুঁটি বেয়ে উঠার সময় প্রতি ঘণ্টায় ১২ মিটার উঠে এবং পরবর্তী ঘণ্টায় ৫ মিটার নিচে নেমে যায়। খুঁটিটি ৬৩ মিটার উঁচু হলে এর শীর্ষে উঠতে কত সময় লাগবে?]

Options:

  • a. 18 hours
  • b. 17 hours
  • c. 16 hours 35 min
  • d. 16 hours 40 min

Solution:

Net progress in a 2-hour cycle (1 hour up, 1 hour down) = 12 m - 5 m = 7 meters.

The ant needs to reach 63 meters.

Consider the height reached just before the final climb. The ant must reach a point from where a 12m climb will take it to or past 63m. This point is \(63 - 12 = 51\) meters.

How long does it take to reach *at least* 51 meters?

Number of 2-hour cycles needed = \(\lceil 51 / 7 \rceil\). \(51 / 7 \approx 7.28\).

After 7 cycles (14 hours), height reached = \(7 \times 7 = 49\) meters.

After 14 hours, the ant is at 49 meters.

In the next hour (15th hour), the ant climbs 12 meters.

Height after 15 hours = 49 + 12 = 61 meters.

In the next hour (16th hour), the ant slips down 5 meters.

Height after 16 hours = 61 - 5 = 56 meters.

In the next hour (17th hour), the ant climbs 12 meters.

Height after 17 hours = 56 + 12 = 68 meters. The ant has reached the top (63m) during this hour.

How far into the 17th hour did it take to reach 63m?

At the start of the 17th hour, the ant is at 56 meters. It needs to climb \(63 - 56 = 7\) more meters.

Time needed = \(\frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{7 \, \text{meters}}{12 \, \text{meters/hour}} = \frac{7}{12}\) hours.

Convert 7/12 hours to minutes: \( \frac{7}{12} \times 60 = 7 \times 5 = 35 \) minutes.

Total time = 16 hours + 35 minutes.

Answer: c. 16 hours 35 min

Section C – [General Math] (Cont.)

8. A train, 300m long, passed a man, walking along the line in the same direction at the rate of 3 kmph in 33 seconds. The speed of the train is: [৩০০ মিটার দীর্ঘ একটি ট্রেন একই দিকে ৩ কিমি/ঘণ্টা বেগে হাঁটা এক ব্যক্তিকে ৩৩ সেকেন্ডে অতিক্রম করে। ট্রেনের গতিবেগ-]

Options:

  • a. 30 km/h
  • b. 32 km/h
  • c. 32 \(^8/_{11}\) km/h
  • d. 35 \(^8/_{11}\) km/h

Solution:

Length of train = 300 m.

Speed of man = 3 km/h. Convert to m/s: \( 3 \times \frac{1000}{3600} = 3 \times \frac{5}{18} = \frac{5}{6} \) m/s.

Time taken to pass the man = 33 seconds.

Let the speed of the train be \(V_T\) m/s.

Since they are moving in the same direction, the relative speed of the train with respect to the man is \(V_{rel} = V_T - \text{Speed of man} = V_T - \frac{5}{6}\) m/s.

The distance the train needs to cover relative to the man is its own length, 300 m.

Distance = Relative Speed × Time

\[ 300 = \left( V_T - \frac{5}{6} \right) \times 33 \]

\[ \frac{300}{33} = V_T - \frac{5}{6} \]

\[ \frac{100}{11} = V_T - \frac{5}{6} \]

\[ V_T = \frac{100}{11} + \frac{5}{6} = \frac{600 + 55}{66} = \frac{655}{66} \, \text{m/s} \]

Convert \(V_T\) to km/h by multiplying by \(\frac{18}{5}\):

\[ V_T (\text{km/h}) = \frac{655}{66} \times \frac{18}{5} = \frac{655}{5} \times \frac{18}{66} = 131 \times \frac{3}{11} = \frac{393}{11} \]

\[ \frac{393}{11} = 35 \frac{8}{11} \, \text{km/h} \]

Answer: d. 35 \(^8/_{11}\) km/h

10. A boy and a girl together fill a cistern with water. The boy pours 4 liters of water every 3 minutes, and the girl pours 3 liters every 4 minutes. How much time will it take to fill 100 liters of water in the cistern? [একটি ছেলে ও একটি মেয়ে একত্রে একটি জলাধারকে পানি দিয়ে পূর্ণ করে। ছেলেটি প্রতি ৩ মিনিটে ৪ লিটার পানি ঢালে ও মেয়েটি প্রতি ৪ মিনিটে ৩ লিটার পানি ঢালে। জলাধারটিতে ১০০ লিটার পানি পূর্ণ করতে কি পরিমাণ প্রয়োজন?]

Options:

  • a. 36 minutes
  • b. 42 minutes
  • c. 48 minutes
  • d. 44 minutes

Solution:

Boy's rate = 4 liters / 3 minutes.

Girl's rate = 3 liters / 4 minutes.

Combined rate = Boy's rate + Girl's rate = \( \frac{4}{3} + \frac{3}{4} \) liters per minute.

Find a common denominator (12):

Combined rate = \( \frac{16}{12} + \frac{9}{12} = \frac{25}{12} \) liters per minute.

Total volume to fill = 100 liters.

Time = \(\frac{\text{Total Volume}}{\text{Combined Rate}} = \frac{100}{25/12}\)

Time = \( 100 \times \frac{12}{25} = (100 \div 25) \times 12 = 4 \times 12 = 48 \) minutes.

Answer: c. 48 minutes

11. A, B and C completed a work costing Tk. 1800. A work for 6 days, B for 4 days and C for 9 days. If their daily wages are in the ratio of 5 : 6 : 4, how much amount will be received by A? [A, B, C একটি কাজ শেষ করাতে যার সম্মানী ১৮০০ টাকা। A কাজ করেছে ৬ দিন, B কাজ করেছে ৪ দিন এবং C কাজ করেছে ৯ দিন। যদি তাদের দৈনিক পারিশ্রমিকের অনুপাত ৫:৬:৪ তাহলে A টাকা পাবে?]

Options:

  • a. Tk 800
  • b. Tk 600
  • c. Tk 900
  • d. Tk 750

Solution:

Ratio of daily wages A:B:C = 5:6:4.

Ratio of days worked A:B:C = 6:4:9.

The total amount earned by each person is proportional to (Daily Wage × Days Worked).

Ratio of total earnings A:B:C = \((5 \times 6) : (6 \times 4) : (4 \times 9)\)

Ratio = 30 : 24 : 36.

Simplify the ratio by dividing by the greatest common divisor (6):

Simplified Ratio = 5 : 4 : 6.

Sum of ratio parts = 5 + 4 + 6 = 15.

Total cost (earnings) = 1800 Tk.

Amount received by A = \( \left( \frac{\text{A's ratio part}}{\text{Sum of parts}} \right) \times \text{Total Cost} \)

Amount for A = \( \frac{5}{15} \times 1800 = \frac{1}{3} \times 1800 = 600 \) Tk.

Answer: b. Tk 600

12. The present ratio of ages of A and B is 4 : 5. 18 years ago, this ratio was 11 : 16. Find the sum total of their present ages. [A, B এর বর্তমান বয়স ৪:৫। ১৮ বছর আগে, এই অনুপাত ১১:১৬ ছিল, তাহলে তাদের বর্তমান বয়সের যোগফল কত?]

Options:

  • a. 90 years
  • b. 105 years
  • c. 110 years
  • d. 80 years

Solution:

Let the present ages of A and B be 4x and 5x respectively.

18 years ago:

A's age = 4x - 18

B's age = 5x - 18

The ratio 18 years ago was 11 : 16.

\[ \frac{4x - 18}{5x - 18} = \frac{11}{16} \]

Cross-multiply:

\[ 16(4x - 18) = 11(5x - 18) \]

\[ 64x - 288 = 55x - 198 \]

\[ 64x - 55x = 288 - 198 \]

\[ 9x = 90 \]

\[ x = 10 \]

Present ages:

A's age = 4x = 4 × 10 = 40 years.

B's age = 5x = 5 × 10 = 50 years.

Sum of their present ages = 40 + 50 = 90 years.

Answer: a. 90 years

13. A dishonest milk man mixed 1 liter of water for every 3 liters of milk and thus made up 36 liters of milk. If he now adds 15 liters of milk to mixture, find the ratio of milk and water in the new mixture: [একজন অসাধু দুধ বিক্রেতা প্রতি ৩ লিটার দুধের সাথে ১ লিটার পানি মেশান এবং এভাবে ৩৬ লিটার মিশ্রণ তৈরি করে। এখন যদি সে মিশ্রণে ১৫ লিটার দুধ মেশায় তাহলে মিশ্রণে দুধ ও পানির অনুপাত নির্ণয় করো।]

Options:

  • a. 12:5
  • b. 14:3
  • c. 7:2
  • d. 9:4

Solution:

The mixture contains milk and water in the ratio 3:1 (Milk:Water).

Total parts in the ratio = 3 + 1 = 4.

Total initial mixture = 36 liters.

Initial amount of Milk = \( \frac{3}{4} \times 36 = 3 \times 9 = 27 \) liters.

Initial amount of Water = \( \frac{1}{4} \times 36 = 1 \times 9 = 9 \) liters.

Now, 15 liters of milk are added.

New amount of Milk = 27 + 15 = 42 liters.

Amount of Water remains the same = 9 liters.

New ratio of Milk to Water = 42 : 9.

Simplify the ratio by dividing by the greatest common divisor (3):

New Ratio = (42/3) : (9/3) = 14 : 3.

Answer: b. 14:3

01. Evaluate \(\int x^{1/2} \, dx\)

Options:

  • b. \(\frac{x^{3/2}}{3} + c\)
  • c. \(3x + c\)
  • d. \(\frac{2}{3} x^{3/2} + c\)

Solution:

Using the power rule \(\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + c\).

Here \(n = 1/2\).

\[ \int x^{1/2} \, dx = \frac{x^{(1/2)+1}}{(1/2)+1} + c = \frac{x^{3/2}}{3/2} + c = \frac{2}{3} x^{3/2} + c \]

02. Find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) where \(y = \tan(x^2 + 1)\). [\(\frac{dy}{dx}\) নির্ণয় করো যেখানে \(y = \tan(x^2 + 1)\)]

Options:

  • a. \(2x \cdot (\sec x + 1)\)
  • b. \(\sec^2(x + 1)\)
  • c. \(2x \sec^2(x^2 + 1)\)
  • d. \(2x \sec^2 (x^3 + 1)\)

Solution:

Use the chain rule. Let \(y = \tan u\) and \(u = x^2 + 1\).

\(\frac{dy}{du} = \sec^2 u = \sec^2(x^2 + 1)\)

\(\frac{du}{dx} = 2x\)

\[ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \cdot \frac{du}{dx} = \sec^2(x^2 + 1) \cdot 2x = 2x \sec^2(x^2 + 1) \]

Answer: c. \(2x \sec^2(x^2 + 1)\)

03. What is the value of \(\lim_{x \to \infty} \left(\frac{3x - 3}{3x + 3}\right)^x\)? [\(\lim_{x \to \infty} (\frac{3x - 3}{3x + 3})^x\) এর মান কোনটি?]

Options:

  • a. 1
  • b. 0
  • c. \(e^{-1}\)
  • d. \(1/3\)

Solution:

This limit is of the indeterminate form \(1^\infty\). Let \(L = \lim_{x \to \infty} \left(\frac{3x - 3}{3x + 3}\right)^x\).

Rewrite the base:

\[ \frac{3x - 3}{3x + 3} = \frac{3x + 3 - 6}{3x + 3} = 1 - \frac{6}{3x + 3} = 1 - \frac{2}{x + 1} \]

So, \(L = \lim_{x \to \infty} \left(1 - \frac{2}{x + 1}\right)^x\).

Use the standard limit form \(\lim_{n \to \infty} (1 + a/n)^n = e^a\).

Let \(n = x+1\), so \(x = n-1\). As \(x \to \infty\), \(n \to \infty\).

\[ L = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{-2}{n}\right)^{n-1} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{-2}{n}\right)^n \cdot \left(1 + \frac{-2}{n}\right)^{-1} \]

\[ = \left( \lim_{n \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{-2}{n}\right)^n \right) \cdot \left( \lim_{n \to \infty} \left(1 + \frac{-2}{n}\right)^{-1} \right) \]

\[ = (e^{-2}) \cdot (1 + 0)^{-1} = e^{-2} \cdot 1 = e^{-2} \]

Alternative Method using logs:

\[ \ln L = \lim_{x \to \infty} x \ln\left(\frac{3x - 3}{3x + 3}\right) = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{\ln\left(\frac{3x - 3}{3x + 3}\right)}{1/x} \]

This is 0/0 form. Use L'Hopital's rule.

Derivative of numerator: \(\frac{3x+3}{3x-3} \cdot \frac{3(3x+3) - 3(3x-3)}{(3x+3)^2} = \frac{3x+3}{3x-3} \cdot \frac{9x+9 - 9x+9}{(3x+3)^2} = \frac{18}{(3x-3)(3x+3)}\)

Derivative of denominator: \(-1/x^2\)

\[ \ln L = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{18 / ((3x-3)(3x+3))}{-1/x^2} = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{-18x^2}{(3x-3)(3x+3)} = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{-18x^2}{9x^2 - 9} \]

\[ = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{-18}{9 - 9/x^2} = \frac{-18}{9} = -2 \]

So, \(\ln L = -2\), which means \(L = e^{-2}\).

None of the options match \(e^{-2}\). There might be a typo in the question or options. If the expression was \(\left(\frac{x - 1}{x + 1}\right)^x\), the limit would be \(e^{-2}\). If it was \(\left(\frac{x - a}{x + a}\right)^x\), limit is \(e^{-2a}\). If it was \(\left(\frac{ax - b}{ax + b}\right)^x\), limit is \(e^{-2b/a}\). Here a=3, b=3, so \(e^{-2(3)/3} = e^{-2}\).

Re-checking option C: If it means \(e^{-1}\), it's incorrect. If it means -1, it's also incorrect.

Answer: None match, the limit is \(e^{-2}\).

04. What is the equation of a circle with its centre at (2, -3) if touches x-axis? [(2, -3) কেন্দ্রবিশিষ্ট ও x-অক্ষকে স্পর্শকারী বৃত্তের সমীকরণ কোনটি?]

06. A car travels a distance of 100 km in 2 hours towards the east and then 50 km in 1 hour towards the west. What is the average velocity of the car? [একটি গাড়ি পূর্ব দিকে ২ ঘণ্টায় ১০০ কিমি এবং পশ্চিম দিকে ১ ঘণ্টায় ৫০ কিমি দূরত্ব অতিক্রম করে। গাড়িটির গড় বেগ কত?]

Options:

  • a. 25 km/h towards the east
  • b. 25 km/h towards the west
  • c. 33.33 km/h towards the east
  • d. None

Solution:

Average velocity = \(\frac{\text{Total Displacement}}{\text{Total Time}}\).

Let East be the positive direction and West be the negative direction.

Displacement 1 = +100 km (East). Time 1 = 2 hours.

Displacement 2 = -50 km (West). Time 2 = 1 hour.

Total Displacement = Displacement 1 + Displacement 2 = (+100) + (-50) = +50 km.

Total Time = Time 1 + Time 2 = 2 + 1 = 3 hours.

Average Velocity = \(\frac{+50 \, \text{km}}{3 \, \text{hours}} \approx +16.67\) km/h.

The positive sign indicates the average velocity is towards the East.

Average Velocity ≈ 16.67 km/h towards the east.

None of the options match this result.

Answer: d. None

07. What did Huygen prove in his experiment? [হাইগেন তার পরীক্ষায় কী প্রমাণ করেছিলেন?]

Options:

  • a. Reflection of light
  • b. Refraction of light
  • c. diffraction of light
  • d. All of the above

Solution:

Christiaan Huygens developed a principle (now Huygens' principle or Huygens-Fresnel principle) stating that every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary spherical wavelets. The superposition of these wavelets forms the subsequent wavefront. Using this principle, Huygens was able to derive the laws of reflection and refraction of light, explaining them based on the wave nature of light. While diffraction is also explained by the wave nature of light and involves Huygens' principle, Huygens himself primarily focused on explaining reflection and refraction.

Answer: d. All of the above (as his principle forms the basis for explaining all these wave phenomena)

08. If the potential difference between the resistances 10\(\Omega\), 15\(\Omega\), 20\(\Omega\), 25\(\Omega\) remains same then in which resistance, more heat will be produced? [10\(\Omega\), 15\(\Omega\), 20\(\Omega\), 25\(\Omega\) চারটি রোধের বিভব পার্থক্য সমান হলে কোনটিতে বেশি তাপ উৎপন্ন হবে?]

Options:

  • a. 10 \(\Omega\)
  • b. 15 \(\Omega\)
  • c. 20 \(\Omega\)
  • d. 25 \(\Omega\)

Solution:

Heat produced (Power dissipated) in a resistor is given by \(P = I^2 R = \frac{V^2}{R} = VI\).

Since the potential difference (V) across each resistor is the same, we use the formula \(P = \frac{V^2}{R}\).

This formula shows that for a constant voltage V, the power (heat produced per unit time) is inversely proportional to the resistance R (\(P \propto 1/R\)).

Therefore, more heat will be produced in the resistor with the *lowest* resistance.

The lowest resistance among the options is 10 \(\Omega\).

Answer: a. 10 \(\Omega\)

09. What will be the work done if the angle between force and displacement is 0°? [যদি বল এবং সরণ এর মধ্যবর্তী কোণ ০° হয় তাহলে কৃত কাজ কি পরিমাণ হবে?]

Options:

  • a. Zero
  • b. Minimum
  • c. Maximum
  • d. Infinite

Solution:

Work done (W) by a constant force (F) causing a displacement (d) is given by \(W = F d \cos \theta\), where θ is the angle between the force vector and the displacement vector.

If the angle \(\theta = 0^\circ\), then \(\cos 0^\circ = 1\).

Work done \(W = F d \times 1 = Fd\).

Since the cosine function has a maximum value of 1 (at 0°), the work done is maximum when the force and displacement are in the same direction (\(\theta = 0^\circ\)).

Answer: c. Maximum

10. A satellite is revolving around the earth. After increasing how much velocity, it will not return to the earth? [একটি উপগ্রহ পৃথিবীর চারদিকে ঘুরছে। ইহার বেগ কতটুকু বৃদ্ধি করলে এটি আর পৃথিবীতে ফিরে আসবে না?]

Options:

  • a. 41.4%
  • b. 50%
  • c. 82.8%
  • d. 100%

Solution:

For a satellite in a circular orbit around the Earth, the orbital velocity (\(v_o\)) is given by \(v_o = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}\), where G is the gravitational constant, M is the mass of the Earth, and r is the orbital radius.

The escape velocity (\(v_e\)) from that same distance r is given by \(v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}\).

Notice that \(v_e = \sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}} = \sqrt{2} v_o\).

To escape Earth's gravity (i.e., not return), the satellite's velocity must be increased from \(v_o\) to at least \(v_e\).

The required increase in velocity is \(v_e - v_o = \sqrt{2} v_o - v_o = (\sqrt{2} - 1) v_o\).

The percentage increase required is \(\frac{\text{Increase}}{\text{Original Velocity}} \times 100\% = \frac{(\sqrt{2} - 1) v_o}{v_o} \times 100\%\)

Percentage Increase = \((\sqrt{2} - 1) \times 100\% \approx (1.414 - 1) \times 100\% = 0.414 \times 100\% = 41.4\%\).

Answer: a. 41.4%
