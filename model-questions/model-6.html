Section C – [General Math]

1. A jar contains 12 red marbles and 24 blue marbles. What percentage of all the marbles are red marbles? [একটি জারে ১২টি লাল মার্বেল ও ২৪টি নীল মার্বেল আছে। মোট মার্বেলের কত শতাংশ লাল মার্বেল?]

Options:

  • 10
  • 20
  • 33.33
  • 40

Solution: Total number of marbles = Number of red marbles + Number of blue marbles = 12 + 24 = 36.

Percentage of red marbles = \(\frac{\text{Number of red marbles}}{\text{Total number of marbles}} \times 100\).

Percentage of red marbles = \(\frac{12}{36} \times 100 = \frac{1}{3} \times 100 = 33.33\%\).

Answer: c. 33.33

2. 10 prizes were distributed among 2% of the contestants and no one get more than 1 prize. What is the total number of contestants? [যদি ২% প্রতিযোগীর মধ্যে ১০টি পুরস্কার বিতরণ করা হয় এবং কেউ ১টির বেশি পুরষ্কার না পায়, তাহলে মোট প্রতিযোগীর সংখ্যা কত?]

Options:

  • 500
  • 600
  • 800
  • 900

Solution: Let the total number of contestants be \(N\).

2% of the contestants received prizes, and this number is equal to 10.

\(2\% \text{ of } N = 10\).

\(\frac{2}{100} \times N = 10\).

\(N = \frac{10 \times 100}{2} = \frac{1000}{2} = 500\).

The total number of contestants is 500.

Answer: a. 500

3. The cost price of 15 articles is the same as the selling price of 10 articles. What is the profit percentage? [যদি ১৫টি আর্টিকেলের ক্রয়মূল্য ১০টি আর্টিকেলের বিক্রয়মূল্যের সমান, তাহলে লাভের শতকরা হার কত?]

Options:

  • 25%
  • 30%
  • 35%
  • 50%

Solution: Let the cost price of one article be CP and the selling price of one article be SP.

Given that the cost price of 15 articles is equal to the selling price of 10 articles.

\(15 \times CP = 10 \times SP\).

\(\frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{15}{10} = \frac{3}{2}\).

Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price = \(SP - CP\).

Profit percentage = \(\frac{SP - CP}{CP} \times 100 = (\frac{SP}{CP} - 1) \times 100\).

Profit percentage = \((\frac{3}{2} - 1) \times 100 = (\frac{3 - 2}{2}) \times 100 = \frac{1}{2} \times 100 = 50\%\).

Answer: d. 50%

4. A man covers half of his journey at 6 km/h and the remaining half at 3 km/h. His average speed is? [একজন লোক তার অর্ধেক যাত্রা ৬ কিমি/ঘণ্টা বেগে সম্পন্ন করে ও বাকি অর্ধেক ৩ কিমি/ঘণ্টা বেগে সম্পন্ন করে। তার গড় বেগ কত?]

Options:

  • 9 km/h
  • 4.5 km/h
  • 4 km/h
  • 3 km/h

Solution: Let the total distance of the journey be \(2d\) km. Then half of the journey is \(d\) km.

Time taken for the first half = \(\frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{d}{6}\) hours.

Time taken for the second half = \(\frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{d}{3}\) hours.

Total time taken = \(\frac{d}{6} + \frac{d}{3} = \frac{d + 2d}{6} = \frac{3d}{6} = \frac{d}{2}\) hours.

Average speed = \(\frac{\text{Total distance}}{\text{Total time}} = \frac{2d}{d/2} = 2d \times \frac{2}{d} = 4\) km/h.

Answer: c. 4 km/h

5. Two guns are fired from the same place at an interval of 6 minutes. A person approaching the place observes that 5 minutes 52 seconds have elapsed between the hearings of the sound of the two guns. If the velocity of the sound is 330 m/sec, the man was approaching that place at what speed (in km/h)? [দুটি বন্দুক একই স্থান থেকে ৬ মিনিটের ব্যবধানে ছোঁড়া হয়। ঐ স্থানের দিকে অগ্রসরমাণ একজন লোক বন্দুকের শব্দ দুটি ৫ মিনিট ৫২ সেকেন্ডের ব্যবধানে শুনতে পায়। যদি শব্দের বেগ ৩৩০ মি/সে হয়, তাহলে ঐ লোকটি ঘণ্টায় কত কিমি বেগে ঐ স্থানের দিকে অগ্রসর হচ্ছিল?]

Options:

  • 24 km/h
  • 27 km/h
  • 30 km/h
  • 36 km/h

Solution: Time interval between firings = 6 minutes = 360 seconds.

Time interval heard by the person = 5 minutes 52 seconds = \(5 \times 60 + 52 = 300 + 52 = 352\) seconds.

Difference in time = \(360 - 352 = 8\) seconds.

In 360 seconds, the first sound travels a distance \(D\).

The second sound travels the same distance \(D\) in 352 seconds as heard by the person. This means the person has moved closer to the source by a distance that sound travels in the time difference.

Distance moved by the person towards the place in 352 seconds = Distance covered by sound in 8 seconds = \(330 \text{ m/s} \times 8 \text{ s} = 2640\) meters.

Speed of the man = \(\frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{2640 \text{ meters}}{352 \text{ seconds}} = 7.5\) m/s.

Convert m/s to km/h: \(7.5 \times \frac{3600}{1000} = 7.5 \times 3.6 = 27\) km/h.

Answer: b. 27 km/h

6. Let U = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}, A = {1, 4} and B = {2, 3, 5}. Find A'∩B'.

Options:

  • {2,3,5,6}
  • {1,2,3}
  • {6}
  • {1,2,3,4,5,6}

Solution: Universal set U = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}.

Set A = {1, 4}.

Set B = {2, 3, 5}.

Complement of A (A') = U - A = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6} - {1, 4} = {2, 3, 5, 6}.

Complement of B (B') = U - B = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6} - {2, 3, 5} = {1, 4, 6}.

Intersection of A' and B' (A'∩B') = {2, 3, 5, 6} ∩ {1, 4, 6} = {6}.

Answer: c. {6}

7. If 10 men can do a piece of work in 12 days, the time taken by 12 men to do the same piece of work will be: [যদি ১০ জন লোক একটি কাজ ১২ দিনে করতে পারে, তাহলে ঐ একই কাজ ১২ জন লোকের করতে সময় লাগবে:]

Options:

  • 12 days
  • 10 days
  • 9 days
  • 8 days

Solution: Total work = Number of men × Number of days.

Total work = \(10 \text{ men} \times 12 \text{ days} = 120 \text{ man-days}\).

Let the time taken by 12 men to do the same work be \(x\) days.

Total work = \(12 \text{ men} \times x \text{ days} = 12x \text{ man-days}\).

Equating the total work: \(120 = 12x\).

\(x = \frac{120}{12} = 10\) days.

Answer: b. 10 days

8. To complete a work, A takes 50% more time than B. If together they take 18 days to complete the work, how much time shall B take to do it? [একটি কাজ শেষ করতে A, B এর থেকে ৫০% সময় বেশি নেয়। তারা যদি একত্রে কাজটি ১৮ দিনে শেষ করতে পারে, তাহলে B একা কাজটি কত সময়ে শেষ করতে পারবে?]

Options:

  • 30 days
  • 35 days
  • 40 days
  • 45 days

Solution: Let the time taken by B to complete the work be \(x\) days.

Then, the time taken by A to complete the work is \(x + 0.50x = 1.5x\) days.

Work done by B in 1 day = \(\frac{1}{x}\).

Work done by A in 1 day = \(\frac{1}{1.5x} = \frac{1}{3x/2} = \frac{2}{3x}\).

Work done by A and B together in 1 day = \(\frac{1}{x} + \frac{2}{3x} = \frac{3 + 2}{3x} = \frac{5}{3x}\).

Together they take 18 days to complete the work, so the work done by them in 1 day is \(\frac{1}{18}\).

\(\frac{5}{3x} = \frac{1}{18}\).

\(3x = 5 \times 18 = 90\).

\(x = \frac{90}{3} = 30\) days.

So, B alone shall take 30 days to do the work.

Answer: a. 30 days

9. In a school having roll strength 286, the ratio of boys and girls is 8 : 5. If 22 more girls get admitted into the school, the ratio of boys and girls becomes: [২৮৬ জন শিক্ষার্থীর একটি স্কুলে ছেলে ও মেয়ের অনুপাত ৮:৫। যদি ২২ জন অতিরিক্ত মেয়ে ঐ স্কুলে ভর্তি হয়, তাহলে ছেলে ও মেয়ের অনুপাত হবে:]

Options:

  • 12 : 7
  • 10 : 7
  • 8 : 7
  • 4 : 3

Solution: Let the number of boys be \(8x\) and the number of girls be \(5x\).

Total number of students = \(8x + 5x = 13x\).

Given that the total roll strength is 286.

\(13x = 286\).

\(x = \frac{286}{13} = 22\).

Number of boys = \(8x = 8 \times 22 = 176\).

Number of girls = \(5x = 5 \times 22 = 110\).

If 22 more girls get admitted, the new number of girls = \(110 + 22 = 132\).

The new ratio of boys and girls = \(176 : 132\).

Divide both by their greatest common divisor. \(176 = 4 \times 44 = 4 \times 4 \times 11 = 16 \times 11\).

\(132 = 12 \times 11\).

GCD(176, 132) = \(4 \times 11 = 44\).

Ratio = \(\frac{176}{44} : \frac{132}{44} = 4 : 3\).

Answer: d. 4 : 3

  • 36

Given that their LCM is 180.

\(20x = 180\).

\(x = \frac{180}{20} = 9\).

The two numbers are \(4x = 4 \times 9 = 36\) and \(5x = 5 \times 9 = 45\).

The smaller number is 36.

Answer: c. 36

    15. There is a rectangular grass field with a length of 85 meters and a breadth of 65 meters. Surrounding the field is a path that is 5 meters wide. Determine the area of the path and the cost of constructing it, given that the cost is Tk 5 per square meter. [একটি আয়তাকার ঘাসের মাঠের দৈর্ঘ্য ৮৫ মিটার এবং প্রস্থ ৬৫ মিটার। মাঠটিকে ঘিরে ৫ মিটার চওড়া একটি রাস্তা আছে। যদি প্রতিবর্গমিটার নির্মাণ করতে ৫ টাকা খরচ হয়, তাহলে রাস্তাটি নির্মাণে মোট কত টাকা খরচ হবে?]

    Options:

    • 4000 Tk
    • 6000 Tk
    • 8000 Tk
    • 7000 Tk

    Solution: Length of the grass field \(L = 85\) m, Breadth \(B = 65\) m.

    Area of the grass field = \(L \times B = 85 \times 65 = 5525\) m².

    Width of the path = 5 m.

    Outer length of the field including the path = \(L + 2 \times \text{width} = 85 + 2 \times 5 = 85 + 10 = 95\) m.

    Area of the field including the path = \(95 \times 75 = 7125\) m².

    Area of the path = Area of the field including the path - Area of the grass field = \(7125 - 5525 = 1600\) m².

    Cost of constructing the path = Area of the path × Cost per square meter = \(1600 \times 5 = 8000\) Tk.

    Answer: c. 8000 Tk

    Section D – [Higher Math & Physics]

    01. Evaluate \(\int (x^2 + 1)^2 \, dx\)

    Options:

    • \(\frac{x^5}{5} + \frac{2x^3}{3} + x + c\)
    • \(\frac{x^5}{5} + \frac{x^3}{3} + x + c\)
    • \(x^4 + x + c\)
    • \(x^2 + \frac{x^3}{3} + \frac{x^4}{4} + c\)

    Solution: \(\int (x^2 + 1)^2 \, dx = \int (x^4 + 2x^2 + 1) \, dx\).

    Using the power rule for integration: \(\int x^4 \, dx = \frac{x^5}{5}\), \(\int 2x^2 \, dx = \frac{2x^3}{3}\), \(\int 1 \, dx = x\).

    So, \(\int (x^4 + 2x^2 + 1) \, dx = \frac{x^5}{5} + \frac{2x^3}{3} + x + c\).

    Answer: a. \(\frac{x^5}{5} + \frac{2x^3}{3} + x + c\)

    02. What is the value of \(\frac{d}{dx} e^{\sin x}\)? [\( \frac{d}{dx} e^{\sin x}\)-এর মান কোনটি?]

    Options:

    • \(\sin x \, e^{\sin x}\)
    • \(e^{\sin x}\)
    • \(\cos x \, e^{\sin x}\)
    • 0

    Solution: Using the chain rule, \(\frac{d}{dx} e^{u} = e^{u} \frac{du}{dx}\), where \(u = \sin x\).

    Here, \(\frac{du}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx} (\sin x) = \cos x\).

    So, \(\frac{d}{dx} e^{\sin x} = e^{\sin x} \cdot \cos x = \cos x \, e^{\sin x}\).

    Answer: c. \(\cos x \, e^{\sin x}\)

    03. What is the area of the quadrilateral if 4 vertices of the quadrilateral are (0, 0), (2, 0), (2, 2) & (0, 2)? [একটি চতুর্ভুজের ৪টি কৌণিক বিন্দুর স্থানাঙ্ক (0, 0), (2, 0), (2, 2) ও (0, 2) হলে, চতুর্ভুজটির ক্ষেত্রফল কত?]

    Options:

    • 2 sq. unit
    • 3 sq. unit
    • 4 sq. unit
    • 5 sq. unit

    Solution: The given vertices are (0, 0), (2, 0), (2, 2), and (0, 2). Plotting these points shows that they form a square with side length 2.

    Area of a square = \((\text{side})^2 = 2^2 = 4\) square units.

    Answer: c. 4 sq. unit

    04. What is the value of \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin^{-1} x}{x}\)?

    Options:

    • 0
    • 1/0
    • 1
    • -1

    Solution: This is a standard limit. Let \(y = \sin^{-1} x\), then \(x = \sin y\). As \(x \to 0\), \(y \to \sin^{-1}(0) = 0\).

    So, \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin^{-1} x}{x} = \lim_{y \to 0} \frac{y}{\sin y}\).

    We know that \(\lim_{y \to 0} \frac{\sin y}{y} = 1\).

    Therefore, \(\lim_{y \to 0} \frac{y}{\sin y} = \frac{1}{\lim_{y \to 0} \frac{\sin y}{y}} = \frac{1}{1} = 1\).

    Answer: c. 1

    05. If \(f(x) = e^x\), then what is the value of \(f^{-1}(1)\)?

    Options:

    • 1
    • -1
    • 0
    • e
    • x

    Solution: If \(f(x) = e^x\), to find the inverse function \(f^{-1}(y)\), we set \(y = e^x\) and solve for \(x\).

    \(\ln y = \ln(e^x)\).

    \(\ln y = x\).

    So, \(f^{-1}(y) = \ln y\).

    We need to find \(f^{-1}(1)\), so we substitute \(y = 1\).

    \(f^{-1}(1) = \ln 1 = 0\).

    Answer: c. 0

    07. The magnitude of the vector \(6\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}\) is equal to: [\(6\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}\) ভেক্টরের মান কত?]

    Options:

    • 5
    • 1
    • 7
    • 12

    Solution: The magnitude of a vector \(\vec{v} = a\hat{i} + b\hat{j} + c\hat{k}\) is given by \(|\vec{v}| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}\).

    For the given vector \(6\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 3\hat{k}\), \(a = 6\), \(b = 2\), \(c = 3\).

    Magnitude = \(\sqrt{6^2 + 2^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{36 + 4 + 9} = \sqrt{49} = 7\).

    Answer: c. 7

    08. A body moving with a constant speed in a circular path is moving with a constant speed. It has? [একটি বস্তু বৃত্তাকার পথে ধ্রুব গতিতে চললে, ইহার কি আছে?]

    Options:

    • Acceleration of constant magnitude
    • Constant velocity
    • Constant acceleration
    • An acceleration which varies with time

    Solution: When a body moves with constant speed in a circular path, its direction of velocity is continuously changing. Since velocity is a vector quantity (having both magnitude and direction), a change in direction means a change in velocity. Therefore, the body has acceleration. The magnitude of this centripetal acceleration is constant (\(v^2/r\)), but its direction is always towards the center of the circle, which is continuously changing. Hence, the velocity is not constant, and the acceleration, while having constant magnitude, has a changing direction.

    Answer: a. Acceleration of constant magnitude

    09. A car starts from rest and moves with a uniform acceleration of 5 m/s². What is the velocity of the car after 10 seconds? [একটি গাড়ি স্থির অবস্থা থেকে 5 m/s² সমত্বরণে চলতে শুরু করে। 10 সেকেন্ড পর গাড়ির বেগ কত?]

    Options:

    • 500 m/s
    • 100 m/s
    • 50 m/s
    • 5 m/s

    Solution: We can use the first equation of motion: \(v = u + at\), where \(v\) is the final velocity, \(u\) is the initial velocity, \(a\) is the acceleration, and \(t\) is the time.

    Given: \(u = 0\) m/s (starts from rest), \(a = 5\) m/s², \(t = 10\) s.

    \(v = 0 + (5)(10) = 50\) m/s.

    Answer: c. 50 m/s

    10. If the radius of the earth is R and potential at the earth surface is V then what will be the potential at height R from the earth surface? [R ব্যাসার্ধের 'পৃথিবীর পৃষ্ঠে বিভব V হলে পৃথিবী পৃষ্ঠ থেকে R উচ্চতায় বিভবের মান কত?]

    Options:

    • 2V
    • 5V
    • 0.5V
    • V2

    Solution: The gravitational potential at the surface of the earth (distance R from the center) is \(V = -\frac{GM}{R}\).

    At a height R from the earth's surface, the distance from the center of the earth is \(R + R = 2R\).

    The gravitational potential at this height (V') is \(V' = -\frac{GM}{2R} = \frac{1}{2} \left(-\frac{GM}{R}\right) = \frac{1}{2} V = 0.5V\).

    Answer: c. 0.5V
