Section A – [English Grammer]

Fill in the blanks:

Critical patients with platelet 1. deficiency, shock 2. syndrome, and low blood pressure are mainly 3. referred here, thanks to its bed 4. sufficiency and its arsenal of 45 ICU beds – which 5. have been instrumental in 6. saving lives. 7. The most public hospitals are also quite well-staffed, with some 87 medical officers and a 8. good number of specialized doctors and nurses working around the 9. clock to treat patients. Their commitment as well as the relatively effective management of resources have been an example worth 10. emulating at this critical time.

1. Critical patients with platelet ______

Options:

  • A. efficiency
  • B. deficiency
  • C. losing
  • D. effects

Solution: Platelet deficiency (thrombocytopenia) is a medical condition where the blood has a lower than normal number of platelets. This fits the context of critical patients.

Answer: B. deficiency

2. shock ______

Options:

  • A. issue
  • B. syndrome
  • C. crisis
  • D. None

Solution: Shock syndrome is a recognized medical term referring to a state of insufficient blood flow to the tissues of the body.

Answer: B. syndrome

3. are mainly ______ here

Options:

  • A. came
  • B. come
  • C. referred
  • D. referring

Solution: The passive voice is needed ("are referred") indicating that patients are sent to this place by others.

Answer: C. referred

4. thanks to its bed ______

Options:

  • A. Shortage
  • B. inadequacy
  • C. lack
  • D. sufficiency

Solution: The context "thanks to" implies a positive attribute. Bed sufficiency (having enough beds) allows the place to handle referred patients.

Answer: D. sufficiency

5. which ______ instrumental

Options:

  • A. was
  • B. is
  • C. would
  • D. have been

Solution: The sentence describes the beds' role up to the present. "Have been" (present perfect) correctly links the past contribution to the current situation.

Answer: D. have been

6. instrumental in ______ lives

Options:

  • A. losing
  • B. effecting
  • C. saving
  • D. affecting

Solution: Being "instrumental in" means playing a key role. In the context of ICU beds and patient care, "saving lives" is the logical outcome.

7. ______ most public hospitals

Options:

  • A. like
  • B. The
  • C. Unlike
  • D. being

Answer: B. The

8. and a ______ number of specialized doctors

  • A. good
  • B. best
  • C. few
  • D. little

Solution: "A good number" is an idiom meaning a fairly large quantity, fitting the context of a well-staffed hospital.

Answer: A. good

9. working around the ______ to treat patients

Options:

  • A. hospital
  • B. clock
  • C. cities
  • D. areas

Solution: "Working around the clock" is an idiom meaning working continuously, day and night, which describes the commitment needed to treat critical patients.

Answer: B. clock

10. example worth ______ at this critical time

  • A. Initiating
  • B. Imagining
  • C. emulating
  • D. originating

Solution: "Worth emulating" means the example is good enough to be copied or imitated by others.

01. Srabon runs twice as fast as Subha and Subha runs thrice as fast as Nisat. The distance covered by Nisat in 72 minutes, will be covered by Srabon in: [শ্রাবণ শুভর চেয়ে দ্বিগুণ দ্রুত দৌড়ায় এবং শুভ নিশাতের চেয়ে তিনগুণ জোরে দৌড়ায়। নিশাত ৭২ মিনিটে যে দূরত্ব অতিক্রম করে, সেই একই দূরত্ব অতিক্রম করবে শ্রাবণ কত সময়ে?]

Options:

  • a. 18 min
  • b. 24 min
  • c. 16 minutes
  • d. 12 min

Solution:

Let the speeds be \(V_S\), \(V_{Su}\), and \(V_N\) for Srabon, Subha, and Nisat, respectively.

Given: \(V_S = 2 V_{Su}\)

Given: \(V_{Su} = 3 V_N\)

Substitute the second equation into the first:

\[ V_S = 2 (3 V_N) = 6 V_N \]

This means Srabon runs 6 times as fast as Nisat.

Let the distance be D. Time taken by Nisat, \(T_N = 72\) minutes.

Since Distance = Speed × Time, and the distance is the same for both:

\[ D = V_N \times T_N = V_S \times T_S \]

\[ V_N \times 72 = (6 V_N) \times T_S \]

Divide both sides by \(V_N\) (assuming \(V_N \neq 0\)):

\[ 72 = 6 \times T_S \]

\[ T_S = \frac{72}{6} = 12 \, \text{minutes} \]

Srabon will cover the distance in 12 minutes.

Answer: d. 12 min

02. A motorboat in still water travels at speed of 36 kmph. It goes 56 km upstream in 1 hour 45 minutes. The time taken by it to cover the same distance down the stream will be: [একটি মোটরবোট স্থির পানিতে ৩৬ কিমি/ঘণ্টা গতিবেগে যায়। স্রোতের প্রতিকূলে ৫৬ কিমি যেতে যদি ১ ঘণ্টা ৪৫ মিনিট প্রয়োজন হয়, তাহলে স্রোতের অনুকূলে একই দূরত্ব যেতে প্রয়োজন হবে?]

Options:

  • a. 2 Hours 25 Minutes
  • b. 3 Hours
  • c. 1 Hours 24 Minutes
  • d. 2 Hours 21 Minutes

Solution:

Let the speed of the boat in still water be \(B = 36\) kmph.

Let the speed of the stream be \(S\) kmph.

Upstream speed = \(B - S = 36 - S\).

Downstream speed = \(B + S = 36 + S\).

Distance upstream = 56 km.

Time upstream = 1 hour 45 minutes = \(1 + \frac{45}{60}\) hours = \(1 + \frac{3}{4} = \frac{7}{4}\) hours = 1.75 hours.

Using Time = Distance / Speed for upstream travel:

\[ 1.75 = \frac{56}{36 - S} \]

\[ 36 - S = \frac{56}{1.75} = \frac{56}{7/4} = 56 \times \frac{4}{7} = 8 \times 4 = 32 \]

\[ S = 36 - 32 = 4 \, \text{kmph} \]

Now, calculate the downstream speed:

Downstream speed = \(36 + S = 36 + 4 = 40\) kmph.

Calculate the time taken to cover 56 km downstream:

Time downstream = Distance / Downstream Speed = \(\frac{56}{40}\) hours.

\[ \frac{56}{40} = \frac{14}{10} = 1.4 \, \text{hours} \]

Convert 1.4 hours into hours and minutes:

1.4 hours = 1 hour + 0.4 hours

0.4 hours = \(0.4 \times 60\) minutes = 24 minutes.

Total time downstream = 1 Hour 24 Minutes.

Answer: c. 1 Hours 24 Minutes

03. Which of the following is not equal to set A?

Options:

  • a. \(A \cap U\)
  • b. \(A \cap \Phi'\)
  • c. \(A \cup A'\)
  • d. \(A \cup \Phi\)

Solution:

Let U be the universal set and \(\Phi\) be the empty set. A' is the complement of A.

  • a. \(A \cap U\): The intersection of set A with the universal set U contains all elements that are in both A and U. This is simply set A. (\(A \cap U = A\))
  • b. \(A \cap \Phi'\): The complement of the empty set (\(\Phi'\)) is the universal set U. So, \(A \cap \Phi' = A \cap U = A\).
  • c. \(A \cup A'\): The union of set A with its complement A' contains all elements that are in A or in A' (or both). By definition, this includes all elements in the universal set U. (\(A \cup A' = U\)). This is generally not equal to A, unless A is the universal set itself.
  • d. \(A \cup \Phi\): The union of set A with the empty set \(\Phi\) contains all elements that are in A or in \(\Phi\). This is simply set A. (\(A \cup \Phi = A\))

Therefore, \(A \cup A'\) is generally not equal to A.

Answer: c. \(A \cup A'\)

04. A completed 7/10 of a work in 15 days, then he completed the remaining work with the help of B in 4 days. In how many days A and B can complete the entire work together? [A একটি কাজের ৭/১০ অংশ শেষ করে ১৫ দিনে। এরপর সে B এর সহায়তায় বাকি কাজ শেষ করে ৪ দিনে। তারা একত্রে সম্পূর্ণ কাজটি কতদিনে শেষ করতে পারবে?]

Options:

  • a. 10 ½ days
  • b. 12 ⅔ days
  • c. 13 ⅓ days
  • d. 8 ¼ days

Solution:

Work done by A = 7/10 in 15 days.

Remaining work = \(1 - \frac{7}{10} = \frac{3}{10}\).

A and B together completed the remaining work (3/10) in 4 days.

Rate of work of (A + B) = \(\frac{\text{Work Done}}{\text{Time Taken}} = \frac{3/10}{4} = \frac{3}{10 \times 4} = \frac{3}{40}\) work per day.

To find the time taken for A and B to complete the entire work together, we use:

Time = \(\frac{\text{Total Work}}{\text{Rate of Work}} = \frac{1}{3/40}\) days.

Time = \(1 \times \frac{40}{3} = \frac{40}{3}\) days.

Convert \(40/3\) days to mixed fraction:

\[ \frac{40}{3} = 13 \frac{1}{3} \, \text{days} \]

Answer: c. 13 ⅓ days

05. While working 7 hours a day, A alone can complete a piece of work in 6 days and B alone in 8 days. In what time would they complete it together, 8 hour a day? [দিনে ৭ ঘণ্টা কাজ করে একটি কাজ A ৬ দিনে এবং B ৭ দিনে শেষ করতে পারে। তারা দিনে ৮ ঘণ্টা কাজ করে একত্রে কাজটি কতদিনে শেষ করতে পারবে?]

Options:

  • a. 3 days
  • b. 4 days
  • c. 2.1 days
  • d. 3.6 days

Solution:

Total hours taken by A to complete the work = \(7 \, \text{hours/day} \times 6 \, \text{days} = 42\) hours.

A's rate of work = \(\frac{1}{42}\) work per hour.

Total hours taken by B to complete the work = \(7 \, \text{hours/day} \times 8 \, \text{days} = 56\) hours.

B's rate of work = \(\frac{1}{56}\) work per hour.

Combined rate of work of A and B = A's rate + B's rate

\[ \text{Combined Rate} = \frac{1}{42} + \frac{1}{56} \]

Find the LCM of 42 and 56. \(42 = 2 \times 3 \times 7\), \(56 = 2^3 \times 7\). LCM = \(2^3 \times 3 \times 7 = 8 \times 21 = 168\).

\[ \text{Combined Rate} = \frac{4}{168} + \frac{3}{168} = \frac{7}{168} = \frac{1}{24} \, \text{work per hour} \]

Total time (in hours) required for A and B to complete the work together = \(\frac{1}{\text{Combined Rate}} = \frac{1}{1/24} = 24\) hours.

They work together 8 hours a day.

Number of days required = \(\frac{\text{Total hours required}}{\text{Hours worked per day}} = \frac{24}{8} = 3\) days.

Answer: a. 3 days

06. What must be added to each term of the ratio 7 : 11, So as to make it equal to 3 : 4? [৭:১১ অনুপাতের প্রতিটি পদের সাথে কত যোগ করলে অনুপাতটি ৩:৪ হবে?]

Options:

  • a. 8
  • b. 7.5
  • c. 6.5
  • d. 5

Solution:

Let the number added to each term be \(x\).

The original ratio is 7 : 11.

After adding \(x\) to each term, the new terms are \(7 + x\) and \(11 + x\).

The new ratio is \((7 + x) : (11 + x)\), which is equal to 3 : 4.

\[ \frac{7 + x}{11 + x} = \frac{3}{4} \]

Cross-multiply:

\[ 4(7 + x) = 3(11 + x) \]

\[ 28 + 4x = 33 + 3x \]

\[ 4x - 3x = 33 - 28 \]

\[ x = 5 \]

So, 5 must be added to each term.

Answer: d. 5

07. Two numbers are in ratio 7 : 11. If 7 is added to each of the numbers, the ratio becomes 2 : 3. The smaller number is [দুটি সংখ্যার অনুপাত ৭:১১। যদি প্রতিটি সংখ্যার সাথে ৭ যোগ করা হয়, তবে তাদের অনুপাত দাঁড়ায় ২:৩। ক্ষুদ্রতর সংখ্যাটি-]

Options:

  • a. 39
  • b. 49
  • c. 66
  • d. 77

Solution:

Let the two numbers be \(7k\) and \(11k\).

When 7 is added to each number, the new numbers are \(7k + 7\) and \(11k + 7\).

The new ratio is 2 : 3.

\[ \frac{7k + 7}{11k + 7} = \frac{2}{3} \]

Cross-multiply:

\[ 3(7k + 7) = 2(11k + 7) \]

\[ 21k + 21 = 22k + 14 \]

\[ 21 - 14 = 22k - 21k \]

\[ k = 7 \]

The original numbers are:

Smaller number = \(7k = 7 \times 7 = 49\).

Larger number = \(11k = 11 \times 7 = 77\).

The smaller number is 49.

Answer: b. 49

08. If \(\cos X = a/b\), then \(\sin X\) is equal to:

Options:

  • a. \((b^2 - a^2)/b\)
  • b. \((b - a)/b\)
  • c. \(\sqrt{(b^2 - a^2)}/b\)
  • d. \(\sqrt{(b - a)}/b\)

Solution:

Using the fundamental trigonometric identity:

\[ \sin^2 X + \cos^2 X = 1 \]

Substitute \(\cos X = a/b\):

\[ \sin^2 X + \left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^2 = 1 \]

\[ \sin^2 X + \frac{a^2}{b^2} = 1 \]

\[ \sin^2 X = 1 - \frac{a^2}{b^2} = \frac{b^2 - a^2}{b^2} \]

Take the square root of both sides. Assuming \(\sin X\) is positive (e.g., X is in the first or second quadrant):

\[ \sin X = \sqrt{\frac{b^2 - a^2}{b^2}} = \frac{\sqrt{b^2 - a^2}}{\sqrt{b^2}} = \frac{\sqrt{b^2 - a^2}}{b} \]

Note: Option C should likely be written as \(\frac{\sqrt{b^2 - a^2}}{b}\) rather than \(\sqrt{(b^2 - a^2)}/b\) which is ambiguous, but it's the mathematically correct result.

Answer: c. \(\sqrt{(b^2 - a^2)}/b\) (interpreted as \(\frac{\sqrt{b^2 - a^2}}{b}\))

09. The value of \(\sin 60^\circ \cos 30^\circ + \sin 30^\circ \cos 60^\circ\) is:

Options:

  • a. 0
  • b. 1
  • c. 2
  • d. 4

Solution:

Method 1: Using standard values

\(\sin 60^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\), \(\cos 30^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\), \(\sin 30^\circ = \frac{1}{2}\), \(\cos 60^\circ = \frac{1}{2}\)

\[ \sin 60^\circ \cos 30^\circ + \sin 30^\circ \cos 60^\circ = \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right) + \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) \]

\[ = \frac{3}{4} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{4}{4} = 1 \]

Method 2: Using sum formula

The expression matches the sine angle addition formula: \(\sin(A + B) = \sin A \cos B + \cos A \sin B\)

Here, \(A = 60^\circ\) and \(B = 30^\circ\).

\[ \sin 60^\circ \cos 30^\circ + \sin 30^\circ \cos 60^\circ = \sin(60^\circ + 30^\circ) = \sin(90^\circ) \]

\[ \sin(90^\circ) = 1 \]

Answer: b. 1

10. The present age of a father is three times the present age of his son. After 10 years, the age of the father will be five more than twice the age of his son. Find the present ages of father. [পিতার বর্তমান বয়স পুত্রের বর্তমান বয়সের ৩গুণ। ১০ বছর পর পিতার বয়স হবে পুত্রের বয়সের দ্বিগুণ অপেক্ষা ৫ বেশি। পিতার বর্তমান বয়স কত?]

Options:

  • a. 65
  • b. 35
  • c. 45
  • d. 30

Solution:

Let the present age of the son be \(S\) years.

Let the present age of the father be \(F\) years.

Given: \(F = 3S\) (Equation 1)

After 10 years:

Son's age = \(S + 10\)

Father's age = \(F + 10\)

Given: Father's age after 10 years = 5 + 2 × (Son's age after 10 years)

\[ F + 10 = 5 + 2(S + 10) \]

\[ F + 10 = 5 + 2S + 20 \]

\[ F + 10 = 2S + 25 \]

\[ F = 2S + 15 \] (Equation 2)

Substitute Equation 1 into Equation 2:

\[ 3S = 2S + 15 \]

\[ 3S - 2S = 15 \]

\[ S = 15 \]

The son's present age is 15 years.

The father's present age is \(F = 3S = 3 \times 15 = 45\) years.

Answer: c. 45

11. The cost of a book was 70tk. The price first increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%. What is the current price of the book? [একটি বইয়ের ক্রয়মূল্য ৭০ টাকা। মূল্য প্রথমে ২০% বৃদ্ধি করে পরে ২০% কমানো হলো। বইয়ের বর্তমান মূল্য কত?]

Options:

  • a. 65.5
  • b. 67.2
  • c. 72
  • d. 75

Solution:

Original cost = 70 tk.

Step 1: Increase by 20%

Increase amount = 20% of 70 = \(0.20 \times 70 = 14\) tk.

Price after increase = 70 + 14 = 84 tk.

Step 2: Decrease by 20% (from the new price)

Decrease amount = 20% of 84 = \(0.20 \times 84 = 16.8\) tk.

Current price = Price after increase - Decrease amount = 84 - 16.8 = 67.2 tk.

Alternatively, use multipliers:

Increase by 20% means multiplying by \(1 + 0.20 = 1.20\).

Decrease by 20% means multiplying by \(1 - 0.20 = 0.80\).

Current price = Original price \(\times 1.20 \times 0.80\)

Current price = \(70 \times 1.20 \times 0.80 = 70 \times 0.96 = 67.2\) tk.

Answer: b. 67.2

12. If Arif's salary is 20% more than Shafin's salary, then Shafin's salary is how much less than Arfi's salary? [যদি আরিফের বেতন শাফিনের বেতনের থেকে ২০% বেশি হয়, তাহলে শাফিনের বেতন আরিফের বেতনের চেয়ে কত কম?]

Options:

  • a. 80%
  • b. 83.33%
  • c. 86.67%
  • d. 90%

Solution:

Let Shafin's salary be \(S\).

Arif's salary (A) is 20% more than Shafin's:

\[ A = S + 0.20 S = 1.20 S \]

We want to find how much less Shafin's salary is than Arif's, expressed as a percentage of Arif's salary.

Difference = \(A - S = 1.20 S - S = 0.20 S\)

Percentage less = \(\frac{\text{Difference}}{\text{Arif's Salary}} \times 100\%\)

\[ \text{Percentage less} = \frac{0.20 S}{1.20 S} \times 100\% \]

\[ = \frac{0.20}{1.20} \times 100\% = \frac{2}{12} \times 100\% = \frac{1}{6} \times 100\% \]

\[ = 16.666... \% \approx 16.67\% \]

\[ \frac{S}{A} = \frac{S}{1.20 S} = \frac{1}{1.20} = \frac{1}{6/5} = \frac{5}{6} \]

13. The cost price of 20 articles is the same as the selling price of x articles. If the profit is 25%, then the value of x is: [২০টি নিবন্ধের ক্রয় মূল্য xটি নিবন্ধের বিক্রয় মূল্যের সমান। যদি লাভ 25% হয়, তাহলে x এর মান কত?]

Options:

  • a. 15%
  • b. 16%
  • c. 18%
  • d. 25%

Solution:

Let CP be the cost price of one article and SP be the selling price of one article.

Given: Cost Price of 20 articles = Selling Price of x articles

\[ 20 \times \text{CP} = x \times \text{SP} \]

\[ \frac{\text{SP}}{\text{CP}} = \frac{20}{x} \]

The profit percentage is given by:

\[ \text{Profit \%} = \frac{\text{SP} - \text{CP}}{\text{CP}} \times 100\% \]

\[ \text{Profit \%} = \left( \frac{\text{SP}}{\text{CP}} - 1 \right) \times 100\% \]

Given Profit = 25%:

\[ 25 = \left( \frac{\text{SP}}{\text{CP}} - 1 \right) \times 100 \]

\[ \frac{20}{x} = \frac{5}{4} \]

Cross-multiply:

14. By increasing each side of a square by 50%, what will be the ratio of the new area to the original area of the square? [বর্গের প্রতিবাহুর দৈর্ঘ্য ৫০% বৃদ্ধি করাতে বর্তমান ক্ষেত্রফল ও পূর্বের ক্ষেত্রফলের অনুপাত কত হবে?]

Options:

  • a. 5 : 4
  • b. 9 : 4
  • c. 4 : 5
  • d. 4 : 9

Solution:

Let the original side of the square be \(s\).

Original Area \(A_{orig} = s^2\).

The side is increased by 50%. The increase is \(0.50 s = 0.5 s\).

New side \(s_{new} = s + 0.5 s = 1.5 s\).

New Area \(A_{new} = (s_{new})^2 = (1.5 s)^2 = 2.25 s^2\).

Ratio of the new area to the original area:

\[ \frac{A_{new}}{A_{orig}} = \frac{2.25 s^2}{s^2} = 2.25 \]

Convert 2.25 to a fraction:

\[ 2.25 = \frac{225}{100} = \frac{9 \times 25}{4 \times 25} = \frac{9}{4} \]

The ratio is 9 : 4.

Answer: b. 9 : 4

15. The sides of a rectangular field are in the ratio 3:4, and the area of the field is 7500 square meters. What is the cost of fencing the field at a rate of 25 paisha per meter? [একটি আয়তাকার মাঠের বাহুদ্বয়ের অনুপাত হলো ৩:৪ এবং ক্ষেত্রফল ৭৫০০ বর্গমিটার। প্রতিমিটারে ২৫ পয়সা হারে মাঠটিতে বেড়া দিতে খরচ কত পড়বে?]

Options:

  • a. 87.5 Tk
  • b. 77.5 Tk
  • c. 67.5 Tk
  • d. 57.5 Tk

Solution:

Let the sides of the rectangle be \(3k\) meters and \(4k\) meters.

Area = Length × Width

\[ \text{Area} = (3k) \times (4k) = 12k^2 \]

Given Area = 7500 m\(^2\).

\[ 12k^2 = 7500 \]

\[ k^2 = \frac{7500}{12} = \frac{2500}{4} = 625 \]

\[ k = \sqrt{625} = 25 \]

The sides of the rectangle are:

Length = \(4k = 4 \times 25 = 100\) meters.

Width = \(3k = 3 \times 25 = 75\) meters.

Fencing involves finding the perimeter.

Perimeter = \(2 \times (\text{Length} + \text{Width})\)

Perimeter = \(2 \times (100 + 75) = 2 \times 175 = 350\) meters.

Cost of fencing per meter = 25 paisha = 0.25 Tk.

Total Cost = Perimeter × Cost per meter

Total Cost = \(350 \times 0.25\) Tk.

Total Cost = \(350 \times \frac{1}{4} = \frac{350}{4} = \frac{175}{2} = 87.5\) Tk.

Answer: a. 87.5 Tk

01. Evaluate \(\int 4\cos x \, dx\)

  • b. \(\cos x + c\)
  • c. \(\sin x + c\)
  • d. \(4\sin x + c\)

The constant factor 4 can be taken outside the integral:

\[ \int 4\cos x \, dx = 4 \int \cos x \, dx \]

The integral of \(\cos x\) is \(\sin x\). Don't forget the constant of integration, c.

\[ 4 \int \cos x \, dx = 4 \sin x + c \]

02. What is the value of \(\frac{d}{dx} (\sin(\ln x))\)? [\(\frac{d}{dx} (\sin(\ln x))\) এর মান নিচের কোনটি?]

Options:

  • a. \(\frac{\cos(\ln x)}{\ln x}\)
  • b. \(e^x\)
  • c. \(\frac{\cos(\ln x)}{x}\)
  • d. 0

Solution:

Use the chain rule. Let \(y = \sin u\) and \(u = \ln x\).

\(\frac{dy}{du} = \cos u = \cos(\ln x)\)

\(\frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{x}\)

\[ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \cdot \frac{du}{dx} = \cos(\ln x) \cdot \frac{1}{x} = \frac{\cos(\ln x)}{x} \]

Answer: c. \(\frac{\cos(\ln x)}{x}\)

03. What is the value of \(\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x^2 + 3x}{2x^2 - 5}\)? [\(\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x^2 + 3x}{2x^2 - 5}\) এর মান নিচের কোনটি?]

Options:

  • a. ½
  • b. 1/3
  • c. 4
  • d. 2

Solution:

To evaluate the limit as \(x \to \infty\), divide both the numerator and the denominator by the highest power of x in the denominator, which is \(x^2\).

\[ \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{\frac{x^2}{x^2} + \frac{3x}{x^2}}{\frac{2x^2}{x^2} - \frac{5}{x^2}} = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1 + \frac{3}{x}}{2 - \frac{5}{x^2}} \]

As \(x \to \infty\), the terms \(\frac{3}{x}\) and \(\frac{5}{x^2}\) approach 0.

\[ \text{Limit} = \frac{1 + 0}{2 - 0} = \frac{1}{2} \]

Answer: a. ½

08. If a car is traveling at 50 km/h and then speeds up to 100 km/h, how does its kinetic energy change? [যদি একটি গাড়ি 50 কিমি/ঘণ্টা বেগে যাত্রা করে এবং তারপর 100 কিমি/ঘণ্টা বেগে যায়, তাহলে তার গতিশক্তি কিভাবে পরিবর্তিত হয়?]

Options:

  • a. It stays the same
  • b. It decreases
  • c. It increases by a factor of 2
  • d. It increases by a factor of 4

Solution:

Kinetic Energy (KE) is given by the formula \(KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2\), where m is mass and v is velocity.

Let the initial velocity be \(v_1 = 50\) km/h.

Initial KE: \(KE_1 = \frac{1}{2}m(v_1)^2 = \frac{1}{2}m(50)^2 = \frac{1}{2}m(2500)\).

Let the final velocity be \(v_2 = 100\) km/h. Note that \(v_2 = 2 v_1\).

Final KE: \(KE_2 = \frac{1}{2}m(v_2)^2 = \frac{1}{2}m(100)^2 = \frac{1}{2}m(10000)\).

Compare the final KE to the initial KE:

\[ \frac{KE_2}{KE_1} = \frac{\frac{1}{2}m(10000)}{\frac{1}{2}m(2500)} = \frac{10000}{2500} = 4 \]

So, \(KE_2 = 4 \times KE_1\). The kinetic energy increases by a factor of 4.

Answer: d. It increases by a factor of 4

09. A car is moving with a velocity of 30 m/s and it comes to rest in 10 seconds. What is the average acceleration of the car? [একটি গাড়ি 30 মি/সেকেন্ড বেগে চলছে এবং এটি 10 সেকেন্ডের মধ্যে বিশ্রামে আসে। গাড়ির গড় ত্বরণ কত?]

Options:

  • a. 3 m/s\(^2\)
  • b. -3 m/s\(^2\)
  • c. 0.3 m/s\(^2\)
  • d. -0.3 m/s\(^2\)

Solution:

Average acceleration \(a_{avg}\) is the change in velocity (\(\Delta v\)) divided by the time interval (\(\Delta t\)).

\[ a_{avg} = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} = \frac{v_{final} - v_{initial}}{\Delta t} \]

Given:

  • Initial velocity \(v_{initial} = 30\) m/s
  • Final velocity \(v_{final} = 0\) m/s (comes to rest)
  • Time interval \(\Delta t = 10\) s

\[ a_{avg} = \frac{0 - 30}{10} = \frac{-30}{10} = -3 \, \text{m/s}^2 \]

The negative sign indicates deceleration (acceleration opposite to the direction of motion).

Answer: b. -3 m/s\(^2\)

10. A cyclist turns around a curve at 15 miles/hour. If he turns at double the speed, the tendency to overturn is? [একজন সাইকেল আরোহী 15 মাইল/ঘণ্টা বেগে একটি বক্ররেখার চারপাশে ঘুরে। সে দ্বিগুণ গতিতে ঘুরলে উল্টে যাওয়ার প্রবণতা হয়-]

Options:

  • a. Doubled
  • b. Quadrupled
  • c. Halved
  • d. Unchanged

Solution:

The tendency to overturn when turning a curve is related to the centripetal force required to maintain the circular motion, compared to the counteracting forces (like friction and the component of gravity if the road is banked). The centripetal force needed is given by \(F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r}\), where m is mass, v is speed, and r is the radius of the curve.

The "tendency to overturn" increases as the required centripetal force increases (or as the speed increases for a given radius).

Let the initial speed be \(v_1 = 15\) mph. The initial tendency is proportional to \(v_1^2\).

The new speed is \(v_2 = 2 \times v_1\).

The new tendency is proportional to \(v_2^2 = (2v_1)^2 = 4v_1^2\).

Comparing the new tendency to the initial tendency:

\[ \frac{\text{New Tendency}}{\text{Initial Tendency}} = \frac{4v_1^2}{v_1^2} = 4 \]

The tendency to overturn is quadrupled when the speed is doubled.

Answer: b. Quadrupled

10. Which is a non-redox reaction? [কোনটি নন-রিডক্স বিক্রিয়া?]

Options:

  • A. MnCl\(_{3}\) → MnCl\(_{2}\) + 1/2 Cl\(_{2}\)
  • B. O\(_{2}\) + 2H\(_{2}\) → 2H\(_{2}\)O
  • C. Na + H\(_{2}\)O → NaOH + 1/2H\(_{2}\)
  • D. CaCO\(_{3}\) → CaO + CO\(_{2}\)

Solution:

A non-redox reaction is one where the oxidation states of all elements remain unchanged throughout the reaction.

  • A. MnCl\(_{3}\) → MnCl\(_{2}\) + 1/2 Cl\(_{2}\): Mn goes from +3 to +2 (reduction). Cl goes from -1 to 0 in Cl\(_{2}\) (oxidation). This is a redox reaction.
  • B. O\(_{2}\) + 2H\(_{2}\) → 2H\(_{2}\)O: O goes from 0 to -2 (reduction). H goes from 0 to +1 (oxidation). This is a redox reaction.
  • C. 2Na + 2H\(_{2}\)O → 2NaOH + H\(_{2}\) (Correct balancing): Na goes from 0 to +1 (oxidation). H goes from +1 in H\(_{2}\)O to 0 in H\(_{2}\) (reduction). This is a redox reaction.
  • D. CaCO\(_{3}\) → CaO + CO\(_{2}\): In CaCO\(_{3}\): Ca is +2, C is +4, O is -2. In CaO: Ca is +2, O is -2. In CO\(_{2}\): C is +4, O is -2. The oxidation states of Ca, C, and O do not change. This is a decomposition reaction, but it is non-redox.

Answer: D. CaCO\(_{3}\) → CaO + CO\(_{2}\)
