Section A – [English Grammer]

Fill in the blanks:

Chronobiology might sound a little 1. futuristic – like something from a science fiction novel, 2. perhaps – but it’s actually a field of 3. study that 4. concerns one of the oldest 5. processes life on this planet 6. has ever known: short-term 7. rhythms of time and their effect on 8. flora and fauna. This can take many forms. Marine life, for example, is 9. influenced by tidal patterns. Animals 10. tend to be active or inactive depending on the position of the sun or moon.

1. Chronobiology might sound a little ______

Options:

  • A. back dated
  • B. ancient
  • C. weird
  • D. futuristic

Solution: The comparison to a science fiction novel suggests the subject might sound advanced or related to the future.

Answer: D. futuristic

2. like something from a science fiction novel, ______ –

Options:

  • A. certainly
  • B. though
  • C. perhaps
  • D. so

Solution: "Perhaps" fits well here as a parenthetical remark softening the comparison.

Answer: C. perhaps

3. but it’s actually a field of ______

Options:

  • A. study
  • B. species
  • C. subjects
  • D. inventions

Solution: Chronobiology is presented as an academic or scientific discipline, making "field of study" the appropriate phrase.

Answer: A. study

4. that ______ one of the oldest

Options:

  • A. was
  • B. has been
  • C. concerns
  • D. related

Solution: The field of study (singular subject) deals with or relates to certain processes. "Concerns" fits grammatically and contextually.

Answer: C. concerns

5. one of the oldest ______ life on this planet

Options:

  • A. species
  • B. kinds
  • C. types
  • D. processes

Answer: D. processes

6. processes life on this planet ______ ever known

  • A. is
  • B. has
  • C. was
  • D. has been

Solution: "Life" (singular subject) requires the singular verb "has" in the present perfect tense ("has ever known").

Answer: B. has

7. short-term ______ of time

  • A. period

8. their effect on ______ and fauna

  • C. us
  • D. flora

Answer: D. flora

9. Marine life, for example, is ______ by tidal patterns

  • C. tends

Section C – [General Math]

1. If the size of a tile is 9" by 9", how many tiles are required to cover a 12 ft. wide and 18 ft. long floor? [যদি একটি টাইলসের সাইজ ৯" বাই ৯" তাহলে ১২ ফুট চওড়া ও ১৮ ফুট দীর্ঘ মেঝের জন্য কয়টি টাইলসের প্রয়োজন হবে?]

Options:

  • a. 400
  • b. 384
  • c. 484
  • d. 24

Solution:

First, convert the floor dimensions to inches (since tile dimensions are in inches). 1 foot = 12 inches.

Floor Width = 12 ft = \(12 \times 12 = 144\) inches.

Floor Length = 18 ft = \(18 \times 12 = 216\) inches.

Area of the floor = Width × Length = \(144 \times 216\) square inches.

Area of one tile = \(9 \times 9 = 81\) square inches.

Number of tiles required = \(\frac{\text{Area of the floor}}{\text{Area of one tile}}\)

Number of tiles = \(\frac{144 \times 216}{81}\)

We know \(144 = 9 \times 16\) and \(81 = 9 \times 9\). Also \(216 = 9 \times 24\).

Number of tiles = \(\frac{(9 \times 16) \times (9 \times 24)}{9 \times 9} = 16 \times 24\)

Number of tiles = \(16 \times (20 + 4) = 320 + 64 = 384\).

Answer: b. 384

2. The area of a rectangular plot is increased by 30% and its width remains as it was before. What will be the ratio between the area of the new rectangle and the original rectangle? [একটি আয়তাকার জমির ক্ষেত্রফল ৩০% বৃদ্ধি করা হলো যেখানে প্রস্থ আগে যা ছিল সেটাই থাকল। জমির পূর্বের ও বর্তমানের ক্ষেত্রফলের অনুপাত কত?]

Options:

  • a. 10 : 2
  • b. 13 : 10
  • c. 7 : 3
  • d. 14 : 15

Solution:

Let the original length be L and original width be W.

Original Area \(A_{orig} = L \times W\).

The area is increased by 30%.

New Area \(A_{new} = A_{orig} + 0.30 \times A_{orig} = 1.30 \times A_{orig}\).

The question asks for the ratio between the new area and the original area.

Ratio = \(\frac{A_{new}}{A_{orig}} = \frac{1.30 \times A_{orig}}{A_{orig}} = 1.30\).

Convert 1.30 to a fraction: \(1.30 = \frac{130}{100} = \frac{13}{10}\).

The ratio of the new area to the original area is 13 : 10.

Answer: b. 13 : 10

3. If selling price is doubled, the profit triples. Find the profit percent. [যদি বিক্রয়মূল্য দ্বিগুণ করা হয়, তাহলে লাভ তিনগুণ হয়। লাভের শতকরা হার কত?]

Options:

  • a. 100%
  • b. 120%
  • c. 125%
  • d. 130%

Solution:

Let CP = Cost Price, SP = Original Selling Price, P = Original Profit.

Original Profit: \(P = SP - CP\).

New Selling Price SP' = 2 × SP.

New Profit P' = SP' - CP = 2 × SP - CP.

Given that the new profit triples: \(P' = 3P\).

Substitute the expressions for P' and P:

\[ 2 \times SP - CP = 3 \times (SP - CP) \]

\[ 2 SP - CP = 3 SP - 3 CP \]

\[ 3 CP - CP = 3 SP - 2 SP \]

\[ 2 CP = SP \]

The selling price is twice the cost price.

Now find the original profit percent:

Profit Percent = \(\frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{CP}} \times 100\% = \frac{SP - CP}{CP} \times 100\%\)

Substitute SP = 2 CP:

Profit Percent = \(\frac{2 CP - CP}{CP} \times 100\% = \frac{CP}{CP} \times 100\% = 1 \times 100\% = 100\%\)

Answer: a. 100%

4. In a certain store, the profit is 320% of the cost. If the cost increases by 25% but the selling price remains constant, approximately what percentage of the selling price is the profit? [একটি নির্দিষ্ট দোকানে, লাভ হলো ক্রয়মূল্যের ৩২০%। যদি ক্রয়মূল্য ২৫% বৃদ্ধি পায় কিন্তু বিক্রয়মূল্য স্থির থাকে, তাহলে আনুমানিক বিক্রয়মূল্যের কত শতাংশ লাভ হবে?]

Options:

  • a. 30%
  • b. 50%
  • c. 70%
  • d. 80%

Solution:

Let the original Cost Price (CP) be C.

Original Profit (P) = 320% of C = 3.20 C.

Original Selling Price (SP) = CP + P = C + 3.20 C = 4.20 C.

Now, the cost increases by 25%.

New Cost Price (CP') = C + 0.25 C = 1.25 C.

The Selling Price (SP) remains constant: SP = 4.20 C.

New Profit (P') = SP - CP' = 4.20 C - 1.25 C = 2.95 C.

We need to find the new profit as a percentage of the selling price.

Percentage = \(\frac{\text{New Profit}}{\text{Selling Price}} \times 100\% = \frac{P'}{SP} \times 100\%\)

Percentage = \(\frac{2.95 C}{4.20 C} \times 100\% = \frac{2.95}{4.20} \times 100\%\)

Percentage \(\approx 0.70238 \times 100\% \approx 70.24\%\).

Approximately, the profit is 70% of the selling price.

Answer: c. 70%

5. You can buy 5 more bananas for 75tk due to the price reduction of 20%. Calculate the current price of one dozen bananas? [২০% মূল্যহ্রাসের কারণে তুমি এখন ৭৫ টাকায় ৫টি বেশি কলা কিনতে পারবে। এক ডজন কলার বর্তমান মূল্য নির্ণয় করো।]

Options:

  • a. 24
  • b. 30
  • c. 32
  • d. 36

Solution:

Let the original price be P tk per banana.

Original number of bananas for 75 tk = \(75 / P\).

The price reduces by 20%, so the new price is \(P - 0.20 P = 0.80 P\) tk per banana.

New number of bananas for 75 tk = \(75 / (0.80 P)\).

The difference in the number of bananas is 5:

\[ \frac{75}{0.80 P} - \frac{75}{P} = 5 \]

Factor out 75/P:

\[ \frac{75}{P} \left( \frac{1}{0.80} - 1 \right) = 5 \]

\[ \frac{75}{P} \left( \frac{1}{4/5} - 1 \right) = 5 \]

\[ \frac{75}{P} \left( \frac{5}{4} - 1 \right) = 5 \]

\[ \frac{75}{P} \left( \frac{1}{4} \right) = 5 \]

\[ \frac{75}{4P} = 5 \]

\[ 75 = 20 P \]

\[ P = \frac{75}{20} = \frac{15}{4} = 3.75 \, \text{tk per banana (original price)} \]

Current price per banana = \(0.80 P = 0.80 \times 3.75 = \frac{4}{5} \times \frac{15}{4} = 3\) tk per banana.

Current price of one dozen (12) bananas = \(12 \times 3 = 36\) tk.

Answer: d. 36

6. An athlete runs 200 meters race in 24 seconds. His speed in km/h is [একজন এথলেট ২৪ সেকেন্ডে ২০০ মিটার দৌড় শেষ করে। তার গতিবেগ কিমি/ঘণ্টা এককে নির্ণয় করো।]

Options:

  • a. 20
  • b. 24
  • c. 28.5
  • d. 30

Solution:

Distance = 200 meters.

Time = 24 seconds.

Speed = Distance / Time = \(200 / 24\) meters per second.

Simplify the speed in m/s: \( \frac{200}{24} = \frac{100}{12} = \frac{25}{3} \) m/s.

To convert m/s to km/h, multiply by \(\frac{3600}{1000} = \frac{18}{5}\).

Speed in km/h = \(\frac{25}{3} \times \frac{18}{5}\)

Speed in km/h = \((25 \div 5) \times (18 \div 3) = 5 \times 6 = 30\) km/h.

Answer: d. 30

7. If Srabon travels to his school from his house at the speed of 3 km/h, then he reaches the school 5 minutes late. If he travels at the speed of 4 km/h, he reaches the school 5 minutes earlier than school time. The distance of his school from his house is: [যদি শ্রাবণ বাড়ি থেকে স্কুলে ৩ কিমি/ঘণ্টা বেগে যায়, তাহলে সে স্কুলে ৫ মিনিট দেরিতে পৌঁছায়। যদি সে ৪ কিমি/ঘণ্টা বেগে যায়, তাহলে সে স্কুলে ৫ মিনিট আগে পৌঁছায়। তার বাসা থেকে স্কুলের দূরত্ব-]

Options:

  • a. 1 km
  • b. 2 km
  • c. 3 km
  • d. 4 km

Solution:

Let D be the distance in km.

Let T be the scheduled time in hours.

Convert minutes to hours: 5 minutes = 5/60 = 1/12 hour.

Case 1: Speed = 3 km/h. Time taken \(T_1 = D/3\). Reaches 5 min late. \(T_1 = T + 1/12 \implies D/3 = T + 1/12\) (Equation 1)

Case 2: Speed = 4 km/h. Time taken \(T_2 = D/4\). Reaches 5 min early. \(T_2 = T - 1/12 \implies D/4 = T - 1/12\) (Equation 2)

Subtract Equation 2 from Equation 1:

\[ (D/3) - (D/4) = (T + 1/12) - (T - 1/12) \]

\[ D \left( \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{4} \right) = T + \frac{1}{12} - T + \frac{1}{12} \]

\[ D \left( \frac{4 - 3}{12} \right) = \frac{2}{12} \]

\[ D \left( \frac{1}{12} \right) = \frac{1}{6} \]

\[ D = \frac{1}{6} \times 12 = 2 \, \text{km} \]

Answer: b. 2 km

Section C – [General Math]

08. In a family of 10 members, 7 of them like tea or coffee, 4 of them like tea and 5 of them like coffee. How many of them like only coffee? [১০ জন সদস্যের একটি পরিবারে ৭ জন চা অথবা কফি, ৪ জন চা এবং ৫ জন কফি পছন্দ করে। তাদের কতজন কেবলমাত্র কফি পছন্দ করে?]

Options:

  • a. 2
  • b. 3
  • c. 4
  • d. 5

Solution:

Let T be the set of members who like tea, and C be the set of members who like coffee.

Total members = 10.

Number who like tea or coffee, \(|T \cup C| = 7\).

Number who like tea, \(|T| = 4\).

Number who like coffee, \(|C| = 5\).

We use the formula: \(|T \cup C| = |T| + |C| - |T \cap C|\)

\[ 7 = 4 + 5 - |T \cap C| \]

\[ 7 = 9 - |T \cap C| \]

\[ |T \cap C| = 9 - 7 = 2 \]

This means 2 members like both tea and coffee.

Number who like only coffee = (Number who like coffee) - (Number who like both)

Only Coffee = \(|C| - |T \cap C| = 5 - 2 = 3\)

Answer: b. 3

09. Two pipes, P and Q can fill a cistern in 12 and 15 minutes respectively. Both are opened together, but at the end of 3 minutes, P is turned off. In how many more minutes will Q fill the cistern? [দুটি পাইপ P ও Q একটি জলাধারকে যথাক্রমে ১২ ও ১৫ মিনিটে পূর্ণ করতে পারে। দুটিকে একত্রে খুলে দেওয়া হলো কিন্তু ৩ মিনিট পর P বন্ধ করে দেওয়া হয়। আর কত সময়ে Q দ্বারা জলাধারটি পূর্ণ হবে?]

Options:

  • a. 7 minutes
  • b. 7.5 minutes
  • c. 8 minutes
  • d. 8 ¼ minutes

Solution:

Rate of pipe P = 1/12 of cistern per minute.

Rate of pipe Q = 1/15 of cistern per minute.

Combined rate of P and Q = \( \frac{1}{12} + \frac{1}{15} \)

LCM of 12 and 15 is 60.

Combined rate = \( \frac{5}{60} + \frac{4}{60} = \frac{9}{60} = \frac{3}{20} \) of cistern per minute.

In the first 3 minutes, work done by P and Q together = Rate × Time = \( \frac{3}{20} \times 3 = \frac{9}{20} \) of the cistern.

Remaining work = \( 1 - \frac{9}{20} = \frac{11}{20} \) of the cistern.

Now, pipe P is turned off, and only Q works.

Time taken by Q to fill the remaining work = \(\frac{\text{Remaining Work}}{\text{Rate of Q}}\)

Time = \( \frac{11/20}{1/15} = \frac{11}{20} \times 15 = \frac{11 \times 3}{4} = \frac{33}{4} \) minutes.

\[ \frac{33}{4} = 8 \frac{1}{4} \, \text{minutes} \]

Answer: d. 8 ¼ minutes

10. A cistern can be filled by two pipes in 20 and 30 minutes respectively. Both pipes being opened, when the first pipe must be turned off so that the cistern may be filled in 10 minutes more. [একটি জলাধার দুইটি পাইপ দিয়ে যথাক্রমে ২০ ও ৩০ মিনিটে পূর্ণ করা যায়। দুইটি পাইপই একত্রে খুলে দেওয়া হলো। প্রথম পাইপ কখন বন্ধ করলে জলাধারটি আর ১০ মিনিটে পূর্ণ হবে?]

Options:

  • a. After 10 minutes
  • b. After 12 minutes
  • c. After 20 minutes
  • d. After 8 minutes

Solution:

Let pipe 1 fill in 20 min (Rate = 1/20 per min).

Let pipe 2 fill in 30 min (Rate = 1/30 per min).

Let the first pipe be turned off after 't' minutes.

The cistern must be filled in 't + 10' minutes total time.

In the first 't' minutes, both pipes are open.

Work done in first 't' minutes = \( t \times (\frac{1}{20} + \frac{1}{30}) = t \times (\frac{3+2}{60}) = \frac{5t}{60} = \frac{t}{12} \).

For the remaining 10 minutes, only the second pipe is open.

Work done in the last 10 minutes = \( 10 \times \frac{1}{30} = \frac{10}{30} = \frac{1}{3} \).

Total work = Work in first 't' mins + Work in last 10 mins = 1 (full cistern)

\[ \frac{t}{12} + \frac{1}{3} = 1 \]

\[ \frac{t}{12} = 1 - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{2}{3} \]

\[ t = \frac{2}{3} \times 12 = 2 \times 4 = 8 \]

The first pipe must be turned off after 8 minutes.

Answer: d. After 8 minutes

11. The ratio of water and milk in a 30-liter mixture is 7 : 3. Find the quantity of water to be added to the mixture to make this ratio 6 : 1. [৩০ লিটারের একটি মিশ্রণে পানি ও দুধের অনুপাত ৭:৩। আর কি পরিমাণ পানি মিশালে এই অনুপাত ৬:১ হবে?]

Options:

  • a. 30
  • b. 32
  • c. 33
  • d. 35

Solution:

Total mixture = 30 liters.

Ratio of Water (W) to Milk (M) = 7 : 3.

Sum of ratio parts = 7 + 3 = 10.

Quantity of Water = \( \frac{7}{10} \times 30 = 21 \) liters.

Quantity of Milk = \( \frac{3}{10} \times 30 = 9 \) liters.

Let 'x' liters of water be added.

New quantity of Water = \( 21 + x \).

Quantity of Milk remains the same = 9 liters.

The new ratio of Water to Milk is 6 : 1.

\[ \frac{21 + x}{9} = \frac{6}{1} \]

\[ 21 + x = 6 \times 9 = 54 \]

\[ x = 54 - 21 = 33 \]

33 liters of water must be added.

Answer: c. 33

12. The incomes of A and B are in the ratio 3 : 2 and their expenditure are in ratio 5 : 3. If each saves Tk. 1000, then, A's income can be: [A ও B এর আয়ের অনুপাত ৩:২ এবং তাদের ব্যয়ের অনুপাত ৫:৩। যদি প্রত্যেকে ১০০০ টাকা সঞ্চয় করে তাহলে A এর আয় হতে পারে-]

Options:

  • a. Tk. 3000
  • b. Tk. 4000
  • c. Tk. 6000
  • d. Tk. 9000

07. Which one is the correct order sequence of the increasing covalent bonds? [বন্ধন সমযোজী বন্ধনের সঠিক ক্রম কোনটি?]

  • B. BeCl2 < NaCl < LiCl
  • C. LiCl < NaCl < BeCl2
  • D. BeCl2 < LiCl < NaCl

Solution:

Covalent character increases as the difference in electronegativity between the bonded atoms decreases. It also increases with increasing charge density (higher charge, smaller size) of the cation, which leads to greater polarization of the anion's electron cloud (Fajans' rules).

Comparing NaCl, LiCl, and BeCl\(_{2}\):

  • Cations: Na\(^+\), Li\(^+\), Be\(^{2+}\). Anion: Cl\(^-\).
  • Electronegativity difference (Pauling scale approx): Cl (3.16), Na (0.93) -> Diff ≈ 2.23 (Highly ionic) Cl (3.16), Li (0.98) -> Diff ≈ 2.18 (Highly ionic, slightly less than NaCl) Cl (3.16), Be (1.57) -> Diff ≈ 1.59 (Polar covalent)
  • Fajans' Rules: Be\(^{2+}\) has a higher charge (+2 vs +1) and smaller ionic radius than Li\(^+\) and Na\(^+\). Li\(^+\) is smaller than Na\(^+\). Therefore, Be\(^{2+}\) polarizes the Cl\(^-\) ion most, leading to the highest covalent character. Li\(^+\) polarizes Cl\(^-\) more than Na\(^+\) does.

Based on both electronegativity difference and Fajans' rules, the covalent character increases in the order: NaCl < LiCl < BeCl\(_{2}\).

Answer: A. NaCl < LiCl < BeCl2

"Relative conductivity" is not a standard term with a distinct unit; it usually implies a ratio compared to a standard, making it dimensionless. The options provided seem to be units for *specific conductivity* (also known as conductivity, symbol κ or σ).

Conductivity (κ) is the reciprocal of resistivity (ρ). Resistivity has units of ohm·cm (or ohm·m).

Therefore, the unit of conductivity (κ) is (ohm·cm)\(^{-1}\) = ohm\(^{-1}\)·cm\(^{-1}\). The unit ohm\(^{-1}\) is also called Siemens (S).

So, the unit is S·cm\(^{-1}\) or ohm\(^{-1}\)·cm\(^{-1}\).

Assuming the question meant "specific conductivity":

Answer: B. ohm\(^{-1}\) cm\(^{-1}\)
