1. Science and technology ______ parts

Options:

  • A. prior
  • B. essential
  • C. Escapable
  • D. Secondary

  • B. causes

  • D. none

Section C – [General Math]

1. The CP of 25 articles is equal to SP of 20 articles. Find the loss or gain percent. [২৫টি আর্টিকেলের ক্রয়মূল্য ২০ টি আর্টিকেলের বিক্রয়মূল্যের সমান। লাভ/ক্ষতির শতকরা হার নির্ণয় করো।]

Options:

  • a. 50
  • b. 25% loss
  • c. 40
  • d. 25% gain

Solution:

Let CP be the cost price of one article and SP be the selling price of one article.

Given: \( 25 \times CP = 20 \times SP \)

Rearrange to find the ratio SP/CP:

\[ \frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{25}{20} = \frac{5}{4} = 1.25 \]

Since SP/CP > 1, there is a gain.

Gain Percent = \( \left( \frac{SP}{CP} - 1 \right) \times 100\% \)

Gain Percent = \( (1.25 - 1) \times 100\% = 0.25 \times 100\% = 25\% \).

Answer: d. 25% gain

2. A shopkeeper increased the sell price of an object by 10%. After realizing the increase of demand he increased the sell price by another 10%. What is his final gain? [একজন বিক্রেতা একটি পণ্যের বিক্রয়মূল্য ১০% বৃদ্ধি করে। পণ্যের চাহিদা বৃদ্ধি পাওয়ায় সে আবার পণ্যের বিক্রয়মূল্য ১০% বাড়িয়ে দেয়। অবশেষে তার লাভের শতকরা হার কত?]

Options:

  • a. 20
  • b. 21
  • c. 22
  • d. 24

Solution:

Let the original price (assume it's the cost price, CP) be 100 units.

First increase: 10% of 100 = 10. New price = 100 + 10 = 110.

Second increase: 10% of the new price (110) = 0.10 × 110 = 11.

Final Selling Price (SP) = 110 + 11 = 121.

Assuming original price was CP, the total gain = SP - CP = 121 - 100 = 21 units.

Final Gain Percent = \( \frac{\text{Total Gain}}{\text{Original CP}} \times 100\% = \frac{21}{100} \times 100\% = 21\% \).

Answer: b. 21

3. If the price of milk is increased by 20%, how much should be the consumption reduced so that there is no increase in expenditure? [যদি দুধের বিক্রয়মূল্য ২০% বৃদ্ধি হয়, তাহলে দুধের ব্যবহার কতটুকু হ্রাস করা উচিত যেন খরচ বৃদ্ধি না পায়?]

Options:

  • a. 83.33%
  • b. 16.67%
  • c. 20%
  • d. 8.33%

Solution:

Let original Price = P, original Consumption = C.

Original Expenditure = E = P × C.

New Price P' = P + 0.20 P = 1.20 P.

Let new Consumption = C'.

New Expenditure E' = P' × C' = (1.20 P) × C'.

We want the expenditure to remain the same: E' = E.

\[ (1.20 P) \times C' = P \times C \]

\[ C' = \frac{P \times C}{1.20 P} = \frac{C}{1.20} = \frac{C}{6/5} = \frac{5}{6} C \]

The new consumption is 5/6 of the original consumption.

Reduction in consumption = C - C' = C - (5/6)C = (1/6)C.

Percentage Reduction = \( \frac{\text{Reduction}}{\text{Original Consumption}} \times 100\% = \frac{(1/6)C}{C} \times 100\% \)

Percentage Reduction = \( \frac{1}{6} \times 100\% = 16.666... \% \approx 16.67\% \).

Answer: b. 16.67%

4. Two trains started at the same time, one from A to B and other from B to A. If they arrived at B and A respectively in 4 hours and 9 hours after they passed each other, the ratio of the speeds of the two trains was? [দুটি ট্রেন একই সময়ে যাত্রা শুরু করে, একটি A থেকে B এবং অপরটি B থেকে A তে। যদি তারা পরস্পরকে অতিক্রম করার যথাক্রমে ৪ ঘণ্টা ও ৯ ঘণ্টা পর গন্তব্যে পৌঁছায়, ট্রেন দুইটির গতিবেগের অনুপাত কত?]

Options:

  • a. 2 : 1
  • b. 3 : 2
  • c. 4 : 3
  • d. 5 : 4

Solution:

Let the speeds of the two trains starting from A and B be \(S_A\) and \(S_B\) respectively.

Let them meet after time 't'.

After meeting, the train from A takes \(t_A = 4\) hours to reach B.

After meeting, the train from B takes \(t_B = 9\) hours to reach A.

There is a standard formula relating the speeds and these times: \(\frac{S_A}{S_B} = \sqrt{\frac{t_B}{t_A}}\).

\[ \frac{S_A}{S_B} = \sqrt{\frac{9}{4}} = \frac{3}{2} \]

The ratio of their speeds (Speed of train from A : Speed of train from B) is 3 : 2.

Answer: b. 3 : 2

5. Two trains of equal length, running in opposite directions, pass a pole in 18 and 12 seconds. The trains will cross each other in: [সমান দৈর্ঘ্যের দুটি ট্রেন, বিপরীত দিক থেকে চলমান অবস্থায়, একটি খুঁটিকে ১৮ ও ১২ সেকেন্ডে অতিক্রম করে। ট্রেন দুটি পরস্পরকে অতিক্রম করতে সময় লাগবে:]

Options:

  • a. 14.4 seconds
  • b. 15.5 seconds
  • c. 18.8 seconds
  • d. 20.2 seconds

Solution:

Let the length of each train be L meters.

Train 1 passes a pole in 18 s. Speed \(S_1 = \frac{\text{Length}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{L}{18}\) m/s.

Train 2 passes a pole in 12 s. Speed \(S_2 = \frac{L}{12}\) m/s.

They are running in opposite directions.

Relative speed when crossing each other = \(S_1 + S_2 = \frac{L}{18} + \frac{L}{12}\).

LCM of 18 and 12 is 36.

Relative speed = \( \frac{2L}{36} + \frac{3L}{36} = \frac{5L}{36} \) m/s.

Total distance to be covered when crossing each other = Sum of their lengths = L + L = 2L meters.

Time to cross each other = \(\frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Relative Speed}} = \frac{2L}{5L/36}\)

Time = \( 2L \times \frac{36}{5L} = \frac{2 \times 36}{5} = \frac{72}{5} = 14.4 \) seconds.

Answer: a. 14.4 seconds

6. Zeroes of \(p(x) = x^2 - 27\) are:

Options:

  • a. \(\pm 9\sqrt{3}\)
  • b. \(\pm 3\sqrt{3}\)
  • c. \(\pm 7\sqrt{3}\)
  • d. 5/3.3

Solution:

To find the zeroes, set \(p(x) = 0\).

\[ x^2 - 27 = 0 \]

\[ x^2 = 27 \]

\[ x = \pm \sqrt{27} \]

Simplify \(\sqrt{27}\): \(\sqrt{27} = \sqrt{9 \times 3} = \sqrt{9} \times \sqrt{3} = 3\sqrt{3}\).

So, the zeroes are \(x = \pm 3\sqrt{3}\).

Answer: b. \(\pm 3\sqrt{3}\)

7. A man and a boy received Tk. 800 as wages for 5 days for the work they did together. The man's efficiency in the work was thrice times that of the boy. What are the daily wages of the boy? [একজন লোক ও এক বালক 5 দিন ধরে একত্রে একটি কাজ করায় 800 টাকা পারিশ্রমিক পায়। লোকটির কর্মদক্ষতা বালকটির কর্মদক্ষতার দ্বিগুণ। বালকটির দৈনিক পারিশ্রমিক কত?]

Options:

  • a. Tk. 76
  • b. Tk. 56
  • c. Tk. 44
  • d. Tk. 40

Solution:

Total wages for 5 days = 800 Tk.

Total wages per day = 800 / 5 = 160 Tk.

Let the boy's efficiency be E. The man's efficiency is 3E.

The daily wages should be shared in the ratio of their efficiencies (assuming wages are proportional to work done/efficiency).

Ratio of Man's wage : Boy's wage = 3E : E = 3 : 1.

Sum of ratio parts = 3 + 1 = 4.

Boy's share of the daily wage = \( \left( \frac{\text{Boy's ratio part}}{\text{Sum of parts}} \right) \times \text{Total daily wage} \)

Boy's daily wage = \( \frac{1}{4} \times 160 = 40 \) Tk.

Answer: d. Tk. 40

8. Srabon can copy 50 pages in 10 hours; Srabon and Subha together can copy 300 pages in 40 hours. In how much time can Subha copy 30 pages? [শ্রাবণ ১০ ঘণ্টায় ৫০ পৃষ্ঠা কপি করতে পারে; শ্রাবণ ও শুভ একত্রে ৪০ ঘণ্টায় ৩০০ পৃষ্ঠা কপি করতে পারে। শুভ ৩০ পৃষ্ঠা কপি করতে কত সময় নেবে?]

Options:

  • a. 13 hour
  • b. 12 hour
  • c. 11 hour
  • d. 9 hour

Solution:

Srabon's rate (R_S) = 50 pages / 10 hours = 5 pages/hour.

Combined rate of Srabon and Subha (R_{S+Su}) = 300 pages / 40 hours = 30/4 = 7.5 pages/hour.

Subha's rate (R_Su) = Combined rate - Srabon's rate

R_Su = 7.5 - 5 = 2.5 pages/hour.

Time for Subha to copy 30 pages = \(\frac{\text{Pages}}{\text{Subha's Rate}} = \frac{30}{2.5}\) hours.

Time = \( \frac{30}{5/2} = 30 \times \frac{2}{5} = 6 \times 2 = 12 \) hours.

Answer: b. 12 hour

9. If the ratio of the ages of Maya and Chhaya is 6 : 5 at present, and fifteen years from now, the ratio will get changed to 9 : 8, then find Maya's present age. [যদি মায়া ও ছায়ার বর্তমান বয়সের অনুপাত ৬:৫ এবং পনেরো বছর পর তাদের বয়সের অনুপাত হবে ৯:৮। মায়ার বর্তমান বয়স কত?]

Options:

  • a. 24 years
  • b. 30 years
  • c. 18 years
  • d. 33 years

Solution:

Let Maya's present age = 6x and Chhaya's present age = 5x.

After 15 years:

Maya's age = 6x + 15

Chhaya's age = 5x + 15

The new ratio is 9 : 8.

\[ \frac{6x + 15}{5x + 15} = \frac{9}{8} \]

Cross-multiply:

\[ 8(6x + 15) = 9(5x + 15) \]

\[ 48x + 120 = 45x + 135 \]

\[ 48x - 45x = 135 - 120 \]

\[ 3x = 15 \]

\[ x = 5 \]

Maya's present age = 6x = 6 × 5 = 30 years.

Answer: b. 30 years

10. A vessel of capacity 2 Liter has 25% alcohol and another vessel of capacity 6 Liter had 40% alcohol. The total liquid of 8 Liter was poured out in a vessel of capacity 10 Liter and thus the rest part of the vessel was filled with the water. What is the new concentration of mixture? [২ লিটার ধারণক্ষমতার একটি পাত্রে ২৫% এ্যালকোহল এবং ৬ লিটার ধারণক্ষমতার পাত্রে ৪০% এ্যালকোহল। মোট ৮ লিটারের দ্রবণটি ১০ লিটার ধারণক্ষমতার একটি পাত্রে ঢালা হলো এবং পাত্রের বাকি অংশ পানি দিয়ে পূর্ণ করা হলো। নতুন মিশ্রণে এ্যালকোহলের ঘনত্ব কত?]

Options:

  • a. 29%
  • b. 49%
  • c. 31%
  • d. 71%

Solution:

Alcohol from first vessel = 25% of 2 L = 0.25 × 2 = 0.5 L.

Alcohol from second vessel = 40% of 6 L = 0.40 × 6 = 2.4 L.

Total liquid poured = 2 L + 6 L = 8 L.

Total alcohol in the 8 L mixture = 0.5 L + 2.4 L = 2.9 L.

This 8 L mixture is poured into a 10 L vessel. The remaining volume is filled with water.

Volume of water added = 10 L - 8 L = 2 L.

The final mixture has a total volume of 10 L.

The amount of alcohol in the final 10 L mixture is still 2.9 L.

New concentration of alcohol = \( \frac{\text{Volume of Alcohol}}{\text{Total Volume}} \times 100\% \)

Concentration = \( \frac{2.9 \, L}{10 \, L} \times 100\% = 0.29 \times 100\% = 29\% \).

Answer: a. 29%

11. If \(\cos(\alpha + \beta) = 0\), then \(\sin(\alpha - \beta)\) can be reduced to

Options:

  • a. \(\cos \beta\)
  • b. \(\cos 2\beta\)
  • c. \(\sin \alpha\)
  • d. \(\sin 2\alpha\)

Solution:

\(\cos(\alpha + \beta) = 0\).

This implies \(\alpha + \beta = 90^\circ + 180^\circ n\) or \(\alpha + \beta = \frac{\pi}{2} + n\pi\) for some integer n.

Let's assume the principal value range, so \(\alpha + \beta = 90^\circ\). This means \(\alpha = 90^\circ - \beta\).

Now consider \(\sin(\alpha - \beta)\). Substitute \(\alpha = 90^\circ - \beta\):

\[ \sin(\alpha - \beta) = \sin((90^\circ - \beta) - \beta) = \sin(90^\circ - 2\beta) \]

Using the co-function identity \(\sin(90^\circ - A) = \cos A\):

\[ \sin(90^\circ - 2\beta) = \cos(2\beta) \]

Answer: b. \(\cos 2\beta\)

12. The shadow of a tower is equal to its height at 10-45 a.m. The sun's altitude is: [সকাল ১০:৪৫ এ একটি টাওয়ারের ছায়া তার উচ্চতার সমান। ভূমির সাথে সূর্যের অবনমন কত কোণে?]

Options:

  • a. 30°
  • b. 45°
  • c. 60°
  • d. 90°
  • e. None

Solution:

Let the height of the tower be H.

Let the length of the shadow be S.

Given: Shadow length = Height, so S = H.

Let the sun's altitude (angle of elevation) be θ.

From trigonometry, \(\tan \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}} = \frac{\text{Height}}{\text{Shadow Length}} = \frac{H}{S}\).

Since H = S, \(\tan \theta = \frac{H}{H} = 1\).

The angle θ for which \(\tan \theta = 1\) is 45°.

The sun's altitude is 45°.

Answer: b. 45°

14. In a rhombus, one of the four angles measures 90 degrees. If each side of the rhombus measures 20 cm, what is the length of the longer diagonal? [একটি রম্বসের চারটি কোণের একটির পরিমাপ ৯০ ডিগ্রী। যদি রম্বসের প্রতিটি বাহুর দৈর্ঘ্য ২০ সেমি হয়, তাহলে এর বৃহত্তর কর্ণের দৈর্ঘ্য কত?]

Options:

  • a. 20 \(\sqrt{2}\) cm
  • b. 25 \(\sqrt{2}\) cm
  • c. 30 \(\sqrt{2}\) cm
  • d. 23 \(\sqrt{2}\) cm

Solution:

A rhombus is a quadrilateral with all four sides equal. If one angle is 90 degrees, then all angles must be 90 degrees (since opposite angles are equal and adjacent angles are supplementary). A rhombus with 90-degree angles is a square.

So, we have a square with side length \(s = 20\) cm.

In a square, both diagonals are equal in length.

Using the Pythagorean theorem for a triangle formed by two sides and a diagonal (d):

03. What is the value of \(\lim_{x \to \infty} (2^x \sin \frac{b}{2^x})\)? [ \(\lim_{x \to \infty} (2^x \sin \frac{b}{2^x})\) এর মান কোনটি?]

  • 1
  • -1
  • -b
  • b

Solution:

Let \(y = \frac{1}{2^x}\). As \(x \to \infty\), \(2^x \to \infty\), so \(y \to 0\).

Also, \(2^x = \frac{1}{y}\).

\[ \lim_{x \to \infty} (2^x \sin \frac{b}{2^x}) = \lim_{y \to 0} \left( \frac{1}{y} \sin(by) \right) \] \[ = \lim_{y \to 0} \frac{\sin(by)}{y} \]

Let \( \theta = by \). As \(y \to 0\), \(\theta \to 0\).

\[ = b \cdot \lim_{\theta \to 0} \frac{\sin \theta}{\theta} = b \cdot 1 = b \]

04. What will be the equation of the parabola with focus F(0, -5) and directrix y = 5? [F(0, -5) ফোকাস ও y = 5 নিয়ামক রেখা বিশিষ্ট পরাবৃত্তের সমীকরণ কোনটি?]

  • \(x^2 = -20y\)
  • \(y^2 = -20x\)

Solution:

The focus is \(F(0, -5)\) and the directrix is the line \(y = 5\).

The vertex of the parabola is the midpoint between the focus and the directrix. The point on the directrix closest to the focus is (0, 5).

The vertex V is the midpoint of (0, -5) and (0, 5), which is \(V = \left( \frac{0+0}{2}, \frac{-5+5}{2} \right) = (0, 0)\).

07. A 0.5 kg object moves with a velocity of 4 m/s towards a stationary 0.3 kg object. After the collision, the 0.5 kg object moves at velocity of 2 m/s. [একটি 0.5 কেজি বস্তু 4 মি/সেকেন্ড বেগে স্থির থাকা 0.3 কেজি বস্তুর দিকে যায়। সংঘর্ষের পরে, 0.5 কেজি বস্তুটি 2 মি/সেকেন্ড বেগে চলে যায়। সংঘর্ষের পর 0.3 কেজি বস্তুর বেগ কত?]

  • 0.5 m/s
  • 1 m/s
  • 3.33 m/s
  • 2 m/s

Solution:

Let \(m_1 = 0.5\) kg and \(m_2 = 0.3\) kg.

Initial velocity of \(m_1\) is \(u_1 = 4\) m/s.

Initial velocity of \(m_2\) is \(u_2 = 0\) m/s (stationary).

Final velocity of \(m_1\) is \(v_1 = 2\) m/s (assuming it continues in the same direction).

Let the final velocity of \(m_2\) be \(v_2\).

By the principle of conservation of linear momentum:

\[ m_1 u_1 + m_2 u_2 = m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 \] \[ (0.5 \text{ kg})(4 \text{ m/s}) + (0.3 \text{ kg})(0 \text{ m/s}) = (0.5 \text{ kg})(2 \text{ m/s}) + (0.3 \text{ kg}) v_2 \] \[ 2 \text{ kg m/s} + 0 = 1 \text{ kg m/s} + 0.3 v_2 \] \[ 2 = 1 + 0.3 v_2 \] \[ 1 = 0.3 v_2 \] \[ v_2 = \frac{1}{0.3} = \frac{10}{3} \text{ m/s} \] \[ v_2 \approx 3.33 \text{ m/s} \]

08. A person walks a distance of 5 km towards the north in 1 hour and then a distance of 4 km towards the east in 1 hour. What is the average velocity of the person? [একজন ব্যক্তি 1 ঘন্টায় উত্তর দিকে 5 কিমি এবং তারপর 1 ঘন্টায় পূর্ব দিকে 4 কিমি দূরত্ব যায়। ব্যক্তির গড় বেগ কত?]

Options:

  • 3.4 km/h towards the north-east
  • 6.4 km/h towards the north-west
  • 6.2 km/h towards the north-east
  • 3.2 km/h towards the north-west

Solution:

Average velocity is defined as total displacement divided by total time.

Displacement is a vector quantity.

First displacement (North): \(\vec{d_1} = (0, 5)\) km. Time \(t_1 = 1\) hour.

Second displacement (East): \(\vec{d_2} = (4, 0)\) km. Time \(t_2 = 1\) hour.

Total displacement: \(\vec{D} = \vec{d_1} + \vec{d_2} = (0+4, 5+0) = (4, 5)\) km.

Total time: \(T = t_1 + t_2 = 1 + 1 = 2\) hours.

Average velocity: \(\vec{v}_{avg} = \frac{\vec{D}}{T} = \frac{(4, 5) \text{ km}}{2 \text{ h}} = (2, 2.5)\) km/h.

The magnitude of the average velocity is:

\[ |\vec{v}_{avg}| = \sqrt{2^2 + (2.5)^2} = \sqrt{4 + 6.25} = \sqrt{10.25} \approx 3.2015 \text{ km/h} \]

The direction is given by the angle \(\theta\) with the positive x-axis (East):

\[ \tan \theta = \frac{\text{North component}}{\text{East component}} = \frac{2.5}{2} = 1.25 \] \[ \theta = \arctan(1.25) \approx 51.3^\circ \]

This angle is measured North of East, which is in the North-East direction.

So the average velocity is approximately 3.2 km/h towards the North-East.

Comparing with the options: Option a: Magnitude (3.4) is slightly off, direction (north-east) is correct. Option d: Magnitude (3.2) is correct, direction (north-west) is incorrect. Given the choices, option d has the correct magnitude but incorrect direction. Option a has the correct direction but slightly off magnitude. There might be a typo in the options. However, 3.2 km/h is the precise calculated magnitude. We choose the option with the closest magnitude, noting the directional discrepancy.

Answer: d. 3.2 km/h towards the north-west (Note: Calculated direction is North-East)

10. If we throw an object from the earth's surface with a velocity of 20 km/s then what will be the trajectory of the object? [পৃথিবী পৃষ্ঠ থেকে আমরা যদি কোনো একটি বস্তুকে 20 km/s বেগে খাড়া উপরের দিকে নিক্ষেপ করি তাহলে বস্তুটির গতিপথ কেমন হবে?]

Options:

  • circle
  • parabola
  • ellipse
  • Hyperbolic

Solution:

The escape velocity from the Earth's surface is approximately \(v_e \approx 11.2\) km/s.

The velocity given is \(v = 20\) km/s.

Since the launch velocity \(v > v_e\), the object has enough energy to escape Earth's gravitational pull completely and will not return.

The trajectory of an object launched with a velocity greater than the escape velocity follows a hyperbolic path relative to the gravitational source (Earth).

10. What is the unit of rate constant of reaction? [বিক্রিয়ার হার ধ্রুবকের একক কোনটি?]

  • mol/L
  • L/mol/S
  • mol/L/S

Solution:

The unit of the rate constant (k) depends on the overall order of the reaction.

Rate = k [Concentration]order

Units of Rate are typically Concentration/Time (e.g., mol L-1 s-1).

  • Zero order: Rate = k. Units of k = Units of Rate = mol L-1 s-1.
  • First order: Rate = k[A]. Units of k = Rate/[A] = (mol L-1 s-1) / (mol L-1) = s-1.
  • Second order: Rate = k[A]2. Units of k = Rate/[A]2 = (mol L-1 s-1) / (mol L-1)2 = L mol-1 s-1.

Let's examine the options assuming S stands for seconds:

  • a. mol/L/S = mol L-1 s-1 (Unit for zero-order k)
  • b. mol/L = mol L-1 (Unit for concentration)
  • c. L/mol/S = L mol-1 s-1 (Unit for second-order k)
  • d. mol/L/S = mol L-1 s-1 (Unit for zero-order k)

Since the question asks for "the unit" without specifying the order, and options 'a' and 'd' are identical and represent a valid unit for a zero-order reaction rate constant (and also the unit of reaction rate itself), this might be the intended answer. Answer: d. mol/L/S (represents units for zero-order rate constant)
