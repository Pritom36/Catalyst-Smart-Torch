Section C – [General Math]

1. If \(x + 1/x = 1\), then \(2/(x^2 - x + 2)\) = ?

Options:

  • \(1/2\)
  • 2
  • 1
  • 4

Solution: Given \(x + 1/x = 1\). Multiplying by \(x\), we get \(x^2 + 1 = x\), which implies \(x^2 - x + 1 = 0\).

We need to find the value of \(2/(x^2 - x + 2)\).

We can rewrite the denominator as \(x^2 - x + 2 = (x^2 - x + 1) + 1\).

Since \(x^2 - x + 1 = 0\), we have \(x^2 - x + 2 = 0 + 1 = 1\).

Therefore, \(2/(x^2 - x + 2) = 2/1 = 2\).

Answer: b. 2

2. The angles of a triangle are \((x + 5)^\circ\), \((2x - 3)^\circ\) and \((3x + 4)^\circ\). Then the value of x is?

Options:

  • 30
  • 31
  • 29
  • 28

Solution: The sum of the angles in a triangle is \(180^\circ\).

So, \((x + 5) + (2x - 3) + (3x + 4) = 180\).

\(x + 2x + 3x + 5 - 3 + 4 = 180\).

\(6x + 6 = 180\).

\(6x = 180 - 6\).

\(6x = 174\).

\(x = \frac{174}{6} = 29\).

Answer: c. 29

3. When the length of shadow of a vertical pole is equal to \(\sqrt{3}\) times of its height, the angle of elevation of the Sun's altitude is? [একটি স্তম্ভের ছায়ার দৈর্ঘ্য তার উচ্চতার \(\sqrt{3}\) গুণ হলে, সূর্যের উন্নতি কোণের পরিমাপ কত?]

Options:

  • 30°
  • 45°
  • 60°
  • 15°

Solution: Let the height of the vertical pole be \(h\) and the length of its shadow be \(s\).

Given, \(s = \sqrt{3} h\).

Let \(\theta\) be the angle of elevation of the Sun's altitude.

We have \(\tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{height}}{\text{shadow}} = \frac{h}{s} = \frac{h}{\sqrt{3} h} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\).

The angle \(\theta\) for which \(\tan(\theta) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\) is \(30^\circ\).

Answer: a. 30°

  • 16
  • 18
  • 24
  • 25

Solution: The ratio of boys to girls is 3 : 2. This means the number of boys can be represented as \(3k\) and the number of girls as \(2k\), where \(k\) is a positive integer.

The total number of members in the club is the sum of the number of boys and girls, which is \(3k + 2k = 5k\).

So, the total number of members must be a multiple of 5.

Checking the options:

  • 16 is not a multiple of 5.
  • 18 is not a multiple of 5.
  • 24 is not a multiple of 5.
  • 25 is a multiple of 5 (\(25 = 5 \times 5\)).

5. Which of the following represents ab = 64?

Options:

  • a : a = 8 : 8
  • a : a = 6 : 4
  • a : b = 8 : 8
  • 32 : a = b : 2

Solution: We need to find which option implies \(ab = 64\).

  • a. \(a : a = 8 : 8\) implies \(a/a = 8/8\), which is \(1 = 1\). This doesn't give information about \(ab\).
  • b. \(a : a = 6 : 4\) implies \(a/a = 6/4\), which is \(1 = 3/2\). This is false.
  • c. \(a : b = 8 : 8\) implies \(a/b = 8/8\), which is \(a/b = 1\) or \(a = b\). This doesn't necessarily mean \(ab = 64\).
  • d. \(32 : a = b : 2\) implies \(\frac{32}{a} = \frac{b}{2}\). Cross-multiplying gives \(ab = 32 \times 2 = 64\).

Answer: d. 32 : a = b : 2

6. A single reservoir supplies the petrol to the whole city, while the reservoir is fed by a single pipeline filling the reservoir with the stream of uniform volume. When the reservoir is full and if 40, 000 Liters of petrol is used daily, the supply fails in 90 days. If 32, 000 Liters of petrol is used daily, the supply fails in 60 days. How much petrol is supplied by the pipeline daily without ever failing? [একটি রিজার্ভার থেকে পুরো শহরে পেট্রোল সরবরাহ করা হয়। যখন রিজার্ভারটি পূর্ণ থাকে যদি দৈনিক ৪০,০০০ লিটার পেট্রোল ব্যবহার করা হয়, তবে ৯০ দিনে সরবরাহ শেষ হয়ে যায়। যদি দৈনিক ৩২,০০০ লিটার পেট্রোল ব্যবহার করা হয়, তবে ৬০ দিনে সরবরাহ শেষ হয়ে যায়। পাইপলাইনটি দৈনিক কত লিটার পেট্রোল সরবরাহ করে যাতে সরবরাহ কখনই ব্যর্থ না হয়?]

Options:

  • 64000 Liters
  • 56000 Liters
  • 60000 Liters
  • 78000 Liters

Solution: Let \(C\) be the capacity of the reservoir in liters, and let \(S\) be the daily supply rate of petrol by the pipeline in liters per day.

In the first case, when 40,000 liters are used daily, the reservoir empties in 90 days. So, the total amount used is \(40000 \times 90 = 3600000\) liters. During these 90 days, the pipeline also supplies petrol. Therefore, \(C + 90S = 3600000\) (Equation 1).

In the second case, when 32,000 liters are used daily, the reservoir empties in 60 days. So, the total amount used is \(32000 \times 60 = 1920000\) liters. During these 60 days, the pipeline also supplies petrol. Therefore, \(C + 60S = 1920000\) (Equation 2).

Subtract Equation 2 from Equation 1:

\((C + 90S) - (C + 60S) = 3600000 - 1920000\).

\(30S = 1680000\).

\(S = \frac{1680000}{30} = 56000\) liters per day.

The daily supply rate of petrol by the pipeline is 56,000 liters.

Answer: b. 56000 Liters

7. In a group of 100 students, 10 know Hindi, 50 know English and 25 know both. Each of the student knows either Hindi or English. How many students are there in the group? [একটি ১০০ জন শিক্ষার্থীর একটি দলে, ১০ জন হিন্দি জানে, ৫০ জন ইংরেজি জানে এবং ২৫ জন দুইটিই জানে। প্রত্যেক শিক্ষার্থী হিন্দি অথবা ইংরেজি জানে। তাহলে ঐ দলে মোট কতজন শিক্ষার্থী আছে?]

Options:

  • 75
  • 100
  • 125
  • 175

Solution: Let H be the set of students who know Hindi, and E be the set of students who know English.

Given:

Number of students in the group = 100

Number of students who know Hindi, \(|H| = 10\)

Number of students who know English, \(|E| = 50\)

Number of students who know both Hindi and English, \(|H \cap E| = 25\)

Number of students who know either Hindi or English is given by the formula: \(|H \cup E| = |H| + |E| - |H \cap E|\).

\(|H \cup E| = 10 + 50 - 25 = 60 - 25 = 35\).

The question states that each student knows either Hindi or English. This implies that the total number of students in the group should be equal to the number of students who know either Hindi or English.

However, the given total number of students is 100, which contradicts the information about the number of students knowing Hindi, English, and both.

If we assume the question is asking for the number of students who know either Hindi or English based on the given numbers, then the answer is 35.

There seems to be an inconsistency in the problem statement. If we consider the statement "Each of the student knows either Hindi or English" to be the defining condition for the group, then the total number of students should be \(|H \cup E| = 35\).

If we trust the initial statement of 100 students, then there might be an error in the numbers provided for those who know Hindi, English, and both, as it leads to a contradiction.

Assuming the question implicitly asks for the size of the union of the two sets based on the provided numbers for Hindi, English, and both:

Number of students who know only Hindi = \(|H| - |H \cap E| = 10 - 25 = -15\). This is not possible.

There is definitely an error in the problem statement or the given numbers.

Answer: There is an inconsistency in the problem statement.

8. A candle of 6 cm burns at the rate of 5 cm in 4 h and another candle 8 cm long burns at the rate of 6 cm in 4h. What is the time required to each candle to remain of equal lengths after burning for some hours, when they starts to burn simultaneously with uniform rate of burning? [6 সেমি দৈর্ঘ্যের একটি মোমবাতি ৪ ঘন্টায় ৫ সেমি হারে এবং অন্য একটি ৮ সেমি লম্বা মোমবাতি ৪ ঘন্টায় ৬ সেমি হারে জ্বলে। কত ঘন্টা পর মোমবাতি দুটির দৈর্ঘ্য সমান হবে যখন তারা একই সাথে সমান হারে জ্বলতে শুরু করে?]

Options:

  • 8 cm
  • 6 cm
  • 4 cm
  • 2 cm

Solution: Let the burning rate of the first candle be \(r_1\) and the second candle be \(r_2\).

First candle: Length = 6 cm, burns 5 cm in 4 hours. So, \(r_1 = \frac{5}{4}\) cm/hour.

Second candle: Length = 8 cm, burns 6 cm in 4 hours. So, \(r_2 = \frac{6}{4} = \frac{3}{2}\) cm/hour.

Let \(t\) be the time in hours after which the lengths of the two candles are equal.

Length of the first candle after \(t\) hours = \(6 - r_1 t = 6 - \frac{5}{4} t\).

Length of the second candle after \(t\) hours = \(8 - r_2 t = 8 - \frac{3}{2} t\).

We want to find \(t\) when the lengths are equal:

\(6 - \frac{5}{4} t = 8 - \frac{3}{2} t\).

\(\frac{3}{2} t - \frac{5}{4} t = 8 - 6\).

\(\frac{6t - 5t}{4} = 2\).

\(\frac{t}{4} = 2\).

\(t = 8\) hours.

The time required for each candle to remain of equal lengths is 8 hours.

Answer: The question asks for the time, which is 8 hours. The options seem to be for length. The length of the first candle after 8 hours = \(6 - \frac{5}{4} \times 8 = 6 - 10 = -4\) cm (not possible). There must be an error in the question or the options.

10. Srabon, Subha and Nisat start together from the same place to walk round a circular path of length 12 km. Srabon walks at the rate of 4 km/h, Subha 3 km/h and Nisat 3/2 km/h. They will meet together at the starting place at the end of?

Options:

  • 10 hours
  • 12 hours
  • 15 hours
  • 24 hours

Solution: Time taken by Srabon to complete one round = Distance / Speed = 12 km / 4 km/h = 3 hours.

Time taken by Subha to complete one round = Distance / Speed = 12 km / 3 km/h = 4 hours.

Time taken by Nisat to complete one round = Distance / Speed = 12 km / (3/2) km/h = 12 * (2/3) = 8 hours.

They will meet together at the starting place after a time which is the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of their individual times.

LCM(3, 4, 8):

Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24, ...

Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, ...

Multiples of 8: 8, 16, 24, ...

The LCM of 3, 4, and 8 is 24.

So, they will meet together at the starting place at the end of 24 hours.

Answer: d. 24 hours

15. The area of a rectangle is the same as that of a square. The square has a side length of 6 cm, and the smaller side of the rectangle is half the length of the square's side. What would be the length of the other side of the rectangle? [একটি আয়তক্ষেত্রের ও বর্গক্ষেত্রের ক্ষেত্রফল সমান। বর্গক্ষেত্রের এক বাহুর দৈর্ঘ্য ৬ সেমি, এবং আয়তক্ষেত্রের ক্ষুদ্রতর বাহুর দৈর্ঘ্য বর্গক্ষেত্রের বাহুর দৈর্ঘ্যের অর্ধেক। আয়তক্ষেত্রের বৃহত্তর বাহুর দৈর্ঘ্য কত হবে?]

Options:

  • 5 cm
  • 8 cm
  • 10 cm
  • 12 cm

Solution: Area of the square = \((\text{side length})^2 = 6^2 = 36\) sq cm.

The area of the rectangle is the same as the area of the square, so the area of the rectangle is 36 sq cm.

The smaller side of the rectangle is half the length of the square's side = \(6 / 2 = 3\) cm.

Let the length of the other side of the rectangle be \(l\).

Area of the rectangle = length × width = \(l \times 3 = 36\).

\(l = \frac{36}{3} = 12\) cm.

Answer: d. 12 cm

Section D – [Higher Math & Physics]

01. Evaluate \(\int \frac{\ln x}{x} \, dx\)

Options:

  • \((\ln x)^2 + c\)
  • \(\ln x + c\)
  • \(\frac{1}{2} (\ln x)^2 + c\)
  • \(\frac{1}{x} (\ln x)^2 + c\)

Solution: Let \(u = \ln x\). Then, \(\frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{x}\), so \(du = \frac{1}{x} dx\).

Substituting into the integral, we get:

\(\int \frac{\ln x}{x} \, dx = \int u \, du = \frac{u^2}{2} + c = \frac{(\ln x)^2}{2} + c\).

Answer: c. \(\frac{1}{2} (\ln x)^2 + c\)

02. What is the value of \(\frac{d}{dx} (\ln |\ln x|)\)? [\( \frac{d}{dx} (\ln |\ln x|)\)-এর মান কোনটি?]

Options:

  • \(\frac{1}{x \ln x}\)
  • \(\frac{1}{\ln x}\)
  • \(\frac{\ln x}{x}\)
  • \(x \ln x\)

Solution: Using the chain rule, let \(u = \ln |x|\). Then \(\frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{x}\).

Now, we need to find the derivative of \(\ln |u|\) with respect to \(x\).

\(\frac{d}{dx} (\ln |\ln x|) = \frac{d}{du} (\ln |u|) \cdot \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{u} \cdot \frac{1}{x} = \frac{1}{\ln |x|} \cdot \frac{1}{x} = \frac{1}{x \ln |x|}\).

Assuming \(x > 0\), we have \(\frac{1}{x \ln x}\).

Answer: a. \(\frac{1}{x \ln x}\)

03. What is the value of \(\lim_{x \to 0} (1 - \cos 2x) / x^2\)? [\( \lim_{x \to 0} (1 - \cos 2x) / x^2\)-এর মান কোনটি?]

Options:

  • 2
  • 1
  • 0
  • All

Solution: We can use the trigonometric identity \(1 - \cos 2x = 2 \sin^2 x\).

\(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos 2x}{x^2} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{2 \sin^2 x}{x^2} = 2 \lim_{x \to 0} \left( \frac{\sin x}{x} \right)^2\).

We know that \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1\).

So, the limit is \(2 (1)^2 = 2\).

Answer: a. 2

04. The equation \(4x^2 + 9y^2 = 36\) represents a: [\(4x^2 + 9y^2 = 36\) সমীকরণটি নির্দেশ করে:]

Options:

  • Parabola
  • Ellipse
  • Hyperbola
  • Circle

Solution: Divide the equation by 36: \(\frac{4x^2}{36} + \frac{9y^2}{36} = 1\), which simplifies to \(\frac{x^2}{9} + \frac{y^2}{4} = 1\).

This is the standard form of the equation of an ellipse: \(\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1\), where \(a^2 = 9\) and \(b^2 = 4\).

Answer: b. Ellipse

07. The scalar product of \(5i + j - 3k\) and \(3i - 4j + 7k\) is: [\(5i + j - 3k\) এবং \(3i - 4j + 7k\) এর স্কেলার গুণফল হল?]

Options:

  • 15
  • -15
  • 10
  • -10

Solution: The scalar product (dot product) of two vectors \(\vec{a} = a_1 i + a_2 j + a_3 k\) and \(\vec{b} = b_1 i + b_2 j + b_3 k\) is given by \(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_1 b_1 + a_2 b_2 + a_3 b_3\).

Here, \(\vec{a} = 5i + j - 3k\) and \(\vec{b} = 3i - 4j + 7k\).

\(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = (5)(3) + (1)(-4) + (-3)(7) = 15 - 4 - 21 = 11 - 21 = -10\).

Answer: d. -10

09. A disk has a radius of 0.5 m and rotates at 200 rpm. What is the linear velocity of a point on the edge of the disk? [একটি চাকতির ব্যাসার্ধ 0.5 মিটার এবং এটি 200 rpm এ ঘোরে। ডিস্কের প্রান্তে অবস্থিত একটি বিন্দুর রৈখিক বেগ কত?]

Options:

  • 5.24 m/s
  • 2.8 m/s
  • 10.5 m/s
  • 104.8 m/s

Solution: Radius \(r = 0.5\) m.

Angular velocity \(\omega = 200 \, \text{rpm} = 200 \times \frac{2\pi}{60} \, \text{rad/s} = \frac{20\pi}{3} \, \text{rad/s}\).

Linear velocity \(v = r\omega = 0.5 \times \frac{20\pi}{3} = \frac{10\pi}{3} \, \text{m/s}\).

Using \(\pi \approx 3.14\), \(v \approx \frac{10 \times 3.14}{3} = \frac{31.4}{3} \approx 10.47 \, \text{m/s}\).

This is approximately 10.5 m/s.

Answer: c. 10.5 m/s
