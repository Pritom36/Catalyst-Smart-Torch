Section C – [General Math]

1. If the length of a rectangle is increased by 60%, what percentage decrease in width is required to keep the area of the rectangle unchanged? [যদি একটি আয়তক্ষেত্রের দৈর্ঘ্য 60% বৃদ্ধি করা হয়, তাহলে আয়তক্ষেত্রের ক্ষেত্রফল অপরিবর্তিত রাখতে প্রস্থ কত শতাংশ কমাতে হবে?]

  • 37.5%
  • 60%
  • 75%
  • 120%

Solution:

Let original Length = L, original Width = W.

Original Area = L × W.

Length is increased by 60%.

New Length (L') = L + 0.60 L = 1.60 L = (8/5) L.

Let the new Width be W'.

New Area = L' × W' = (1.60 L) × W'.

We want the area to remain unchanged: New Area = Original Area.

(1.60 L) × W' = L × W

Divide by L: 1.60 W' = W

W' = W / 1.60 = W / (8/5) = (5/8) W = 0.625 W.

The new width must be 0.625 times the original width.

Decrease in width = Original Width - New Width = W - 0.625 W = 0.375 W.

Percentage Decrease = \( \frac{\text{Decrease in width}}{\text{Original Width}} \times 100 \)

Percentage Decrease = \( \frac{0.375 W}{W} \times 100 = 0.375 \times 100 = 37.5\% \)

Answer: a. 37.5%

2. The breadth of a rectangle is related to its perimeter in a ratio of 2:10. The area of the rectangle is 429 square centimeters. What is the length of the rectangle? [যদি একটি আয়তক্ষেত্রের প্রস্থ এবং তার পরিসীমার মধ্যে 2:10 অনুপাত সম্পর্কযুক্ত থাকে, এবং আয়তক্ষেত্রের ক্ষেত্রফল 429 বর্গসেমি হয়, আয়তক্ষেত্রের দৈর্ঘ্য কত হবে?]

  • 25.4 cm
  • 30.4 cm
  • 40.4 cm
  • 45.4 cm

Solution:

Let Length = L, Breadth = B, Perimeter = P, Area = A.

Perimeter P = 2(L + B).

Area A = L × B = 429.

Given ratio Breadth : Perimeter = 2 : 10 (or 1 : 5).

\( \frac{B}{P} = \frac{2}{10} = \frac{1}{5} \)

\( \frac{B}{2(L + B)} = \frac{1}{5} \)

Cross-multiply: \( 5B = 2(L + B) = 2L + 2B \)

\( 5B - 2B = 2L \)

\( 3B = 2L \implies B = \frac{2}{3} L \).

Substitute this into the area equation:

\( A = L \times B = L \times (\frac{2}{3} L) = \frac{2}{3} L^2 \).

We know A = 429.

\( 429 = \frac{2}{3} L^2 \)

\( L^2 = 429 \times \frac{3}{2} = \frac{1287}{2} = 643.5 \)

\( L = \sqrt{643.5} \approx 25.367 \) cm.

This is closest to option a. Let's check if there's an easier way or a potential typo.

Let B=2x, P=10x. P=2(L+B) => 10x=2(L+2x) => 5x=L+2x => L=3x.

Area = L*B = (3x)(2x) = 6x^2.

Area = 429. So, 6x^2 = 429.

x^2 = 429 / 6 = 143 / 2 = 71.5.

x = sqrt(71.5) ≈ 8.455.

Length L = 3x = 3 * sqrt(71.5) ≈ 3 * 8.455 ≈ 25.367 cm.

The calculation consistently gives ~25.4 cm.

What if the ratio was 3:10? B=3x, P=10x. 10x=2(L+3x) => 5x=L+3x => L=2x. Area=L*B=(2x)(3x)=6x^2=429. Same result.

What if the ratio was 2:7? B=2x, P=7x. 7x=2(L+2x)=2L+4x => 3x=2L => L=1.5x. Area=L*B=(1.5x)(2x)=3x^2=429. x^2=143. x=sqrt(143). L=1.5*sqrt(143) ≈ 17.9 cm.

What if Area = 441? L^2 = 441*3/2 = 661.5. L=25.7. What if Area = 484? L^2 = 484*3/2 = 726. L=26.9.

Let's assume L=21 (factors of 429 are 3, 11, 13 => 33*13, 39*11, 21*...). If L=21, B=429/21 ≈ 20.4. Ratio B:P = 20.4 : 2(21+20.4) = 20.4 : 82.8. Not 2:10. If L=33, B=13. P=2(33+13)=92. Ratio B:P = 13:92. Not 2:10.

The result 25.367 is closest to 25.4.

Answer: a. 25.4 cm

3. What percent of 3x is 6y if x = 4y?

  • 10%
  • 20%
  • 30%
  • 50%

Solution:

We want to find what percentage 'P' satisfies: P% of (3x) = 6y.

\( \frac{P}{100} \times (3x) = 6y \)

We are given x = 4y. Substitute this into the equation:

\( \frac{P}{100} \times (3 \times 4y) = 6y \)

\( \frac{P}{100} \times (12y) = 6y \)

Divide both sides by 6y (assuming y ≠ 0):

\( \frac{P}{100} \times 2 = 1 \)

\( \frac{2P}{100} = 1 \)

\( 2P = 100 \)

\( P = 50 \)

So, 6y is 50% of 3x.

Answer: d. 50%

4. If the radius of a circle is increased by 20%. What will be the percentage increase of new area? [যদি একটি বৃত্তের ব্যাসার্ধ 20% বৃদ্ধি করা হয়, তাহলে তার ক্ষেত্রফল কত শতাংশ বৃদ্ধি পাবে?]

  • 44
  • 22
  • 25
  • 30

Solution:

Let original radius = r.

Original Area (A) = πr2.

New radius (r') = r + 20% of r = r + 0.20r = 1.20r.

New Area (A') = π(r')2 = π(1.20r)2 = π(1.44 r2) = 1.44 (πr2) = 1.44 A.

Increase in Area = A' - A = 1.44 A - A = 0.44 A.

Percentage Increase = \( \frac{\text{Increase in Area}}{\text{Original Area}} \times 100 \)

Percentage Increase = \( \frac{0.44 A}{A} \times 100 = 0.44 \times 100 = 44\% \).

Answer: a. 44

7. The speed of Srabon and Subha are in the ratio 3 : 4. Srabon takes 20 minutes more than Subha to reach a destination. In time which Srabon reach the destination? [শ্রাবণ ও শুভার গতিবেগের অনুপাত 3 : 4। একটি গন্তব্যে পৌঁছাতে শ্রাবণ শুভার চেয়ে 20 মিনিট বেশি সময় নেয়। শ্রাবণ কত সময়ে গন্তব্যে পৌঁছাবে?]

  • 1 ⅓ hours
  • 2 hours
  • 2 ⅔ hours
  • 1 ⅔ hours

Solution:

Ratio of speeds \(S_A : S_B = 3 : 4\).

Since Time = Distance / Speed, for a fixed distance, time is inversely proportional to speed.

Ratio of times \(T_A : T_B = S_B : S_A = 4 : 3\).

Let \(T_A = 4x\) and \(T_B = 3x\).

Srabon (A) takes more time than Subha (B). Difference = \(T_A - T_B\).

Given difference = 20 minutes.

\( 4x - 3x = 20 \) minutes.

\( x = 20 \) minutes.

Time taken by Srabon (\(T_A\)) = 4x = 4 × 20 minutes = 80 minutes.

Convert 80 minutes to hours:

80 minutes = 1 hour and 20 minutes = 1 and 20/60 hours = 1 and 1/3 hours.

1 1/3 hours.

Answer: a. 1 ⅓ hours

8. Let U = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}, A = {1,4} and B = {2,3,5}. Find B'. [ ধরা যাক U = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}, A = {1,4} এবং B = {2,3,5}। B' নির্ণয় কর।]

  • {2,3,5,6}
  • {1,2,3}
  • {1,4,6}
  • {1,2,3,4,5,6}

Solution:

B' (complement of B) is the set of all elements in the universal set U that are *not* in set B.

U = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}

B = {2, 3, 5}

Elements in U but not in B are 1, 4, 6.

B' = {1, 4, 6}.

Answer: c. {1,4,6}
