2. A car moving on a circular track of a radius of 2000 meters completes one revolution in 15 minutes. What is the average speed of the car (approx.)? [2000 মিটার ব্যাসার্ধের একটি বৃত্তাকার পথে চলমান একটি গাড়ি 15 মিনিটে একটি চক্র সম্পন্ন করে। গাড়ির গড় গতি কত (প্রায়)?]

  • 344.56 m/min
  • 837.76 m/min
  • 857.76 m/min
  • 987.57 m/min

Solution:

Average speed = Total Distance / Total Time.

Distance = Circumference of the track = \( 2 \pi r \).

Radius (r) = 2000 meters.

Distance = \( 2 \pi (2000) = 4000 \pi \) meters.

Time = 15 minutes.

Average Speed = \( \frac{4000 \pi \text{ meters}}{15 \text{ minutes}} = \frac{800 \pi}{3} \) m/min.

Using \(\pi \approx 3.14159\):

Average Speed \(\approx \frac{800 \times 3.14159}{3} \approx \frac{2513.27}{3} \approx 837.76 \) m/min.

Answer: b. 837.76 m/min

[একটি ক্লাসে 25 জন মেয়ের গড় ওজন 47 কেজি এবং বাকি 10 জন মেয়ের গড় ওজন 45 কেজি। ক্লাসের সমস্ত মেয়েদের গড় ওজন নির্ণয় করুন।] (Question implies 25 + 10 = 35 girls total)

  • 45.55 kg
  • 46.56 kg
  • 46.42 kg
  • 49.25kg

Group 1: Number N1 = 25 girls, Average Weight W1 = 47 kg.

Total weight of Group 1 = N1 × W1 = 25 × 47 = 1175 kg.

Total weight of Group 2 = N2 × W2 = 10 × 45 = 450 kg.

6. The ratio of milk to water in 80 liters of a mixture is 7:3. The water to be added to it to make the ratio 2:1 is [80 লিটার মিশ্রণে দুধ ও পানির অনুপাত 7:3। অনুপাতটি 2:1 করতে হলে যে পরিমাণ পানি মেশাতে হবে তা হল:]

  • 5
  • 6

Initial ratio Milk : Water = 7 : 3.

8. Four prime numbers are arranged in ascending order. The product of first three is 385 and that of last three is 1001. The largest prime number is- [ চারটি মৌলিক সংখ্যা আরোহী ক্রমে সাজানো আছে। প্রথম তিনটির গুণফল 385 এবং শেষ তিনটির গুণফল 1001। বৃহত্তম মৌলিক সংখ্যাটি হল-]

  • 9
  • 11
  • 13
  • 17

Solution:

Let the four prime numbers in ascending order be p1 < p2 < p3 < p4.

Given: p1 × p2 × p3 = 385

Given: p2 × p3 × p4 = 1001

Find the prime factors of 385:

Ends in 5, so divisible by 5. 385 / 5 = 77.

77 = 7 × 11.

So, p1, p2, p3 are 5, 7, 11 in ascending order.

p1 = 5, p2 = 7, p3 = 11.

Find the prime factors of 1001:

Try dividing by small primes. Not divisible by 2, 3, 5. Try 7.

1001 / 7 = 143.

Now factor 143. Try 11. 143 / 11 = 13.

13 is prime.

So, p2, p3, p4 are 7, 11, 13 in ascending order.

p2 = 7, p3 = 11, p4 = 13.

The four primes are 5, 7, 11, 13.

The largest prime number is p4 = 13.

Answer: c. 13

9. A can do a job in 15 days, and B can do the same job in 10 days. They start working together, but after 3 days, A leaves. In how many days will B alone complete the remaining work? [A একটি কাজ 15 দিনে করতে পারে, এবং B একই কাজ 10 দিনে করতে পারে। তারা একসাথে কাজ শুরু করে, কিন্তু 3 দিন পর, A চলে যায়। B একা বাকি কাজটি কত দিনে সম্পন্ন করবে?]

  • 5 days
  • 6 days
  • 7 days
  • 8 days

Solution:

Rate of A (\(R_A\)) = 1/15 job/day.

Rate of B (\(R_B\)) = 1/10 job/day.

Combined rate (\(R_{A+B}\)) = \(R_A + R_B = \frac{1}{15} + \frac{1}{10}\).

LCM of 15 and 10 is 30.

Combined rate = \( \frac{2}{30} + \frac{3}{30} = \frac{5}{30} = \frac{1}{6} \) job/day.

Work done together in 3 days = Combined rate × 3 days = \( \frac{1}{6} \times 3 = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2} \) job.

Remaining work = 1 - 1/2 = 1/2 job.

A leaves. B completes the remaining work alone at rate \(R_B = 1/10\) job/day.

Time taken by B = Remaining work / Rate of B

Time = \( \frac{1/2}{1/10} = \frac{1}{2} \times 10 = 5 \) days.

Answer: a. 5 days

10. If A = {1,2,3,4,5,7,8,9} and B = {2,4,6,7,9}, then find the number of proper subsets (উপসেট সংখ্যা) of A∩B?

  • 16
  • 15
  • 32
  • 31

Solution:

First, find the intersection of sets A and B, \(A \cap B\).

The intersection contains elements that are in *both* A and B.

A = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9}

B = {2, 4, 6, 7, 9}

\(A \cap B\) = {2, 4, 7, 9}.

The number of elements in the intersection set is n = 4.

The total number of subsets of a set with n elements is 2n.

Total number of subsets of \(A \cap B\) = 24 = 16.

Proper subsets are all subsets *except* the set itself.

Number of proper subsets = Total number of subsets - 1.

Number of proper subsets = 16 - 1 = 15.

14. 8, 19, 30, 41, 52, ... Given the sequence above, what is the sum of the 10th and 20th terms ? [উপরের অনুক্রমটি দেওয়া হল, 10ম এবং 20তম পদের যোগফল কত?]

15. There is a football playground whose length = 36 m and breadth = 21 m. How many flag staff will be required to be fixed on an all along field at a distance of 3 m part? [একটি ফুটবল খেলার মাঠ রয়েছে যার দৈর্ঘ্য = 36 মি এবং প্রস্থ = 21 মি। 3 মিটার দূরত্বে মাঠের চারপাশে লাগাতে কতগুলি ফ্ল্যাগ স্টাফ প্রয়োজন হবে?]

  • 39
  • 38
  • 37
  • 36

Solution:

The flags are fixed along the perimeter of the field.

Length (L) = 36 m.

Breadth (B) = 21 m.

Perimeter = 2 × (L + B) = 2 × (36 + 21) = 2 × 57 = 114 meters.

Flags are fixed at a distance of 3 m apart along the perimeter.

Number of flags = Perimeter / Distance between flags

Number of flags = 114 / 3 = 38.

Section D – [Higher Math & Physics]

01. If \( f(x) = \ln(ax) \) then dy/dx = ? (Assuming y=f(x))

  • 1 + ln(ax)
  • ln(ax)
  • 1 + 1/x
  • 1

Solution:

Method 1: Using logarithm properties first.

\( f(x) = \ln(ax) = \ln a + \ln x \). (Assuming a > 0, x > 0).

Since ln a is a constant:

03. The ratio of roots of the equation ax2 + bx + c = 0 is 3 : 4. Then which one of the following is true? [ ax2 + bx + c = 0 সমীকরণের মূলের অনুপাত 3 : 4। তাহলে নিচের কোনটি সত্য?]

  • b. 12b2 = 49ca
  • c. 12c2 = 49ab
  • d. c2 = 49ab

Solution:

Let the roots be \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \).

Given the ratio of roots is 3 : 4. Let \( \alpha = 3k \) and \( \beta = 4k \) for some constant k.

From Vieta's formulas for the quadratic equation \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\):

Sum of roots: \( \alpha + \beta = -b/a \).

\( 3k + 4k = -b/a \implies 7k = -b/a \implies k = -b / (7a) \).

Product of roots: \( \alpha \beta = c/a \).

\( (3k)(4k) = c/a \implies 12k^2 = c/a \).

Now substitute the expression for k from the sum equation into the product equation:

\( 12 \left( \frac{-b}{7a} \right)^2 = \frac{c}{a} \)

\( 12 \left( \frac{b^2}{49a^2} \right) = \frac{c}{a} \)

\( \frac{12b^2}{49a^2} = \frac{c}{a} \)

Multiply both sides by \( 49a^2 \) (assuming a ≠ 0):

\( 12b^2 = \frac{c}{a} \times 49a^2 \)

04. What is the vertex of the parabola which equation Is x2 + 4x + 2y = 0? [ x2 + 4x + 2y = 0 সমীকরণের পরাবৃত্তের শীর্ষবিন্দু কোনটি?]

  • (3, -3)
  • (1, -2)
  • (2, -2)
  • d. (-2, 2)

Solution:

To find the vertex, rewrite the equation in the standard form of a parabola by completing the square for the x terms.

\( x^2 + 4x + 2y = 0 \)

Isolate the x terms and complete the square:

\( (x^2 + 4x) = -2y \)

Take half of the coefficient of x (which is 4/2 = 2) and square it (22 = 4). Add and subtract 4 inside a parenthesis (or add 4 to both sides).

\( (x^2 + 4x + 4) - 4 = -2y \)

\( (x + 2)^2 - 4 = -2y \)

\( (x + 2)^2 = -2y + 4 \)

Factor out the coefficient of y on the right side:

\( (x + 2)^2 = -2(y - 2) \).

This is the standard form \( (x - h)^2 = 4p(y - k) \), where (h, k) is the vertex.

Comparing forms: \( x - h = x + 2 \implies h = -2 \).

\( y - k = y - 2 \implies k = 2 \).

The vertex is (h, k) = (-2, 2).

(Here, \( 4p = -2 \implies p = -1/2 \). Since p is negative, the parabola opens downwards).

Answer: d. (-2, 2)

05. Under the condition [ f : N -> N which one of the following is one-one function? [f : N -> N শর্তের অধীনে নিচের কোনটি এক-এক ফাংশন?]

  • f(x) = x2
  • f(x) = x3
  • a & b
  • None

Solution:

A function f is one-one (injective) if different inputs always produce different outputs. That is, if \( f(x_1) = f(x_2) \), then \( x_1 = x_2 \).

The domain and codomain are N (the set of natural numbers: {1, 2, 3, ...}).

a. \( f(x) = x^2 \): If \( f(x_1) = f(x_2) \), then \( x_1^2 = x_2^2 \). Since the domain is N (positive numbers), taking the square root gives \( x_1 = x_2 \). So, \( f(x) = x^2 \) is one-one for \( x \in N \).

b. \( f(x) = x^3 \): If \( f(x_1) = f(x_2) \), then \( x_1^3 = x_2^3 \). Taking the cube root gives \( x_1 = x_2 \) (this holds for all real numbers, including natural numbers). So, \( f(x) = x^3 \) is one-one for \( x \in N \).

Since both f(x) = x2 and f(x) = x3 are one-one functions when the domain is restricted to N, option c is correct.

Answer: c. a & b

06. Value of maximum gravitational potential Is— [ সর্বোচ্চ মহাকর্ষীয় বিভবের মান হল-]

  • 0
  • ∞ V
  • 1 V
  • 6.67 x 10-11 V

Solution:

Gravitational potential (V) at a distance r from a point mass M is given by \( V = -\frac{GM}{r} \).

By convention, gravitational potential is defined to be zero at an infinite distance from the mass (\( r \to \infty \)).

Since G, M, and r are positive, the potential \( V = -\frac{GM}{r} \) is always negative or zero.

The potential increases as r increases (becomes less negative).

The maximum value of the gravitational potential occurs as \( r \to \infty \), where \( V \to 0 \).

Therefore, the maximum value of gravitational potential is 0.

Answer: a. 0

07. A bullet can cross a wooden block. if the speed of the bullet is increased to 4 times then how many blocks will be able to cross? [একটি বুলেট একটি কাঠের ব্লক ভেদ করতে পারে। বুলেটের গতি 4 গুণ বাড়ানো হলে কয়টি ব্লক ভেদ করতে পারবে?]

  • 4
  • 10
  • 16
  • 32

Solution:

Assume the wooden block offers a constant resistive force (F) to the bullet over the distance (d) it penetrates (the thickness of one block).

The work done by the resistive force to stop the bullet is equal to the initial kinetic energy of the bullet (by the work-energy theorem).

Work done = Force × Distance = F × d (for one block).

Initial Kinetic Energy (KE) = \( \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \).

For one block: \( F \times d = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \).

Now, the speed is increased to 4 times the original speed: New speed \( v' = 4v \).

New Kinetic Energy (KE') = \( \frac{1}{2} m (v')^2 = \frac{1}{2} m (4v)^2 = \frac{1}{2} m (16v^2) = 16 \times (\frac{1}{2} m v^2) = 16 \times KE \).

Let N be the number of blocks the bullet can now penetrate.

The total work done by the resistive force over N blocks is \( F \times (N \times d) \).

This work must equal the new kinetic energy:

\( F \times N \times d = KE' = 16 \times KE \)

Substitute \( KE = F \times d \):

\( F \times N \times d = 16 \times (F \times d) \)

Divide by \( F \times d \) (assuming F and d are non-zero):

\( N = 16 \).

The bullet will be able to cross 16 blocks.

Answer: c. 16

08. What will be the difference in temperature of peak and bottom point of a 100m waterfall? [100 মিটার জলপ্রপাতের শীর্ষ এবং নিম্ন বিন্দুর তাপমাত্রার পার্থক্য কত হবে?]

  • 0.434°C
  • 0.234°C
  • 0.234F
  • 0.546°C

Solution:

Assume the potential energy of the water at the top is completely converted into internal energy (heat) at the bottom.

Potential Energy (PE) = mgh.

Heat gained (Q) = mc\(\Delta\)T, where c is the specific heat capacity of water and \(\Delta\)T is the temperature change.

Set PE = Q:

mgh = mc\(\Delta\)T

Divide by m:

gh = c\(\Delta\)T

\(\Delta\)T = gh / c

Height (h) = 100 m.

Gravitational acceleration (g) ≈ 9.8 m/s2.

Specific heat capacity of water (c) ≈ 4186 J/(kg·°C).

\(\Delta\)T = (9.8 m/s2 × 100 m) / (4186 J/(kg·°C))

\(\Delta\)T = 980 / 4186 °C ≈ 0.234 °C.

Answer: b. 0.234°C

10. What will be the required force to throw an object of mass 10kg with an acceleration of 1.2 m/s2 vertically upward? [10kg ভরের একটি বস্তুকে 1.2 m/s2 ত্বরণে উল্লম্বভাবে উপরের দিকে নিক্ষেপ করতে প্রয়োজনীয় বল কত হবে?]

  • 100 N
  • 200 N
  • 300 N
  • 110 N

Solution:

To throw the object upwards with acceleration 'a', the applied force (F_applied) must overcome gravity (Weight=mg) and provide the net force needed for the acceleration (ma).

Net Force (F_net) = F_applied - Weight

ma = F_applied - mg

F_applied = ma + mg = m(a + g)
