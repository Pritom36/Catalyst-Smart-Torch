1. A swimming pool 9 m wide and 12 m long ls 1 m deep on the shallow side and 4 m deep on the deeper side. Find the volume of the swimming pool? [একটি সুইমিং পুল ৯ মিটার চওড়া এবং ১২ মিটার দীর্ঘ। পুলটির অগভীর অংশের দিকে ১ মিটার গভীর এবং গভীর অংশের দিকে ৪ মিটার গভীর। সুইমিং পুলের আয়তন কত?]

  • 260 m3
  • 262 m3
  • 270 m3
  • 280 m3

Solution:

The pool has a constant width (W = 9 m) and length (L = 12 m).

The depth varies linearly from the shallow end (d1 = 1 m) to the deep end (d2 = 4 m).

The cross-section along the length is a trapezoid.

The area of this trapezoidal cross-section = \( \frac{1}{2} \times (\text{sum of parallel sides}) \times (\text{distance between them}) \)

Area of cross-section = \( \frac{1}{2} \times (d1 + d2) \times L = \frac{1}{2} \times (1 + 4) \times 12 = \frac{1}{2} \times 5 \times 12 = 30 \) m2.

Wait, the cross section is along the length. The area of the side view (trapezoid) has height = Length = 12m and parallel sides = depths = 1m and 4m. Area = \( \frac{1}{2}(1+4) \times 12 = 30 \) m2.

Volume = Area of cross-section × Width

Volume = 30 m2 × 9 m = 270 m3.

Alternatively, view the volume as the average depth multiplied by the surface area.

Average depth = (1 m + 4 m) / 2 = 2.5 m.

Surface Area = Length × Width = 12 m × 9 m = 108 m2.

Volume = Surface Area × Average Depth = 108 m2 × 2.5 m = 270 m3.

Answer: c. 270 m3

2. The cost of the paint is Tk 36.50 per kg. If 1 kg of paint covers 16 square feet, how much will it cost to paint outside of a cube having 8 feet each side? [পেইন্টের দাম প্রতি কেজি ৩৬.৫০ টাকা। যদি ১ কেজি পেইন্ট ১৬ বর্গফুট পেইন্ট করতে পারে, তাহলে ৮ ফুট ধার বিশিষ্ট একটি ঘনকের বাইরের অংশ রঙ করতে কত খরচ হবে?]

  • 850 Tk
  • 860 Tk
  • 876 Tk
  • 886 Tk

Solution:

The cube has sides of 8 feet.

Area of one face of the cube = side × side = 8 ft × 8 ft = 64 sq ft.

A cube has 6 faces. Total surface area (outside) = 6 × Area of one face = 6 × 64 sq ft = 384 sq ft.

Coverage rate = 16 sq ft per kg of paint.

Total paint required (in kg) = Total Area / Coverage rate

Paint required = 384 sq ft / 16 sq ft/kg = 24 kg.

Cost per kg = Tk 36.50.

Total cost = Paint required (kg) × Cost per kg

Total cost = 24 kg × 36.50 Tk/kg

Total cost = 24 × (36 + 0.50) = (24 × 36) + (24 × 0.50) = 864 + 12 = 876 Tk.

Answer: c. 876 Tk

3. A retailer buys a radio for Rs 225. His overhead expenses are Rs 15. He sells the radio for Rs 300. The profit percent of the retailer is? [একজন খুচরা বিক্রেতা 225 টাকায় একটি রেডিও কেনেন। তার ওভারহেড খরচ 15 টাকা। তিনি রেডিওটি 300 টাকায় বিক্রি করেন। খুচরা বিক্রেতার লাভের শতাংশ কত?]

  • 25%
  • 24%
  • 16%
  • 15%

Solution:

Purchase Price = Rs 225.

Overhead Expenses = Rs 15.

Total Cost Price (CP) = Purchase Price + Overhead Expenses = 225 + 15 = Rs 240.

Selling Price (SP) = Rs 300.

Profit = SP - CP = 300 - 240 = Rs 60.

Profit Percent = \( \frac{\text{Profit}}{CP} \times 100 \)

Profit Percent = \( \frac{60}{240} \times 100 = \frac{1}{4} \times 100 = 25\% \)

Answer: a. 25%

5. By how much percent a motorist increase his speed in order to reduce by 20%, the time taken to cover a certain distance? [একটি নির্দিষ্ট দূরত্ব অতিক্রম করতে সময় 20% কমাতে একজন মোটরচালককে তার গতি কত শতাংশ বৃদ্ধি করতে হবে?]

  • 20
  • 25
  • 30
  • 35

Solution:

Let the original time be T and original speed be S. Distance D = S × T.

The time needs to be reduced by 20%.

New time (T') = T - 0.20 T = 0.80 T.

Let the new speed be S'. The distance remains the same.

D = S' × T' = S' × (0.80 T).

Since D = S × T, we have:

S × T = S' × (0.80 T)

Divide by T (assuming T is not zero):

S = S' × 0.80

S' = S / 0.80 = S / (4/5) = (5/4) S = 1.25 S.

The new speed is 1.25 times the original speed.

Increase in speed = S' - S = 1.25 S - S = 0.25 S.

Percentage increase in speed = \( \frac{\text{Increase in speed}}{\text{Original speed}} \times 100 \)

Percentage increase = \( \frac{0.25 S}{S} \times 100 = 0.25 \times 100 = 25\% \)

Answer: b. 25

6. Srabon and Subha run a 100 meter race, where Srabon beats Subha by 10 meters. To do a favour to Subha, Srabon starts 10 meter behind the starting line in a second 100 meter race. They both run at their earlier speeds. Which of the following is true in connection with the race? [শ্রাবণ ও সুভাষ 100 মিটার দৌড়ায়, যেখানে শ্রাবণ সুভাষকে 10 মিটারে পরাজিত করে। সুভাষের সুবিধা করার জন্য, শ্রাবণ দ্বিতীয় 100 মিটার দৌড়ে স্টার্টিং লাইনের 10 মিটার পেছন থেকে শুরু করে। তারা দুজনই তাদের আগের গতিতে দৌড়ায়। নিম্নলিখিত কোনটি দৌড়ের সাথে সম্পর্কিত সত্য?]

  • Srabon and Subha reach the finishing line simultaneously
  • Subha beats Srabon by 1 meter
  • Subha beats Srabon by 11 meter
  • Srabon beats Subha by 1 meter

Solution:

Race 1: Distance = 100 m.

When Srabon finishes (covers 100 m), Subha has covered 100 - 10 = 90 m.

Let the time taken by Srabon be t. Speed of Srabon (Vs) = 100/t.

Speed of Subha (Vb) = 90/t.

The ratio of speeds Vs : Vb = (100/t) : (90/t) = 100 : 90 = 10 : 9.

Race 2: Srabon needs to run 100 + 10 = 110 m.

Subha needs to run 100 m.

Time taken by Srabon in Race 2 (Ts2) = Distance / Speed = 110 / Vs = 110 / (100/t) = 1.1 t.

Time taken by Subha in Race 2 (Tb2) = Distance / Speed = 100 / Vb = 100 / (90/t) = (100/90) t = (10/9) t ≈ 1.111 t.

Since Ts2 (1.1t) < Tb2 (1.111t), Srabon finishes first.

When Srabon finishes Race 2 (at time 1.1t), how far has Subha run?

Distance covered by Subha in time 1.1t = Speed × Time = Vb × 1.1t = (90/t) × 1.1t = 90 × 1.1 = 99 m.

Srabon finishes the 110m race when Subha has run 99m of her 100m race.

Srabon beats Subha by 100 - 99 = 1 meter.

Answer: d. Srabon beats Subha by 1 meter

7. An ant moved for several seconds and covered 3 mm in the first second and 4 mm more in each successive second than its predecessor. If the ant had covered 1 mm in the first second and 8 mm more in each successive second, then the difference between the path it would cover during the same time and the actual path would be more than 6 mm but less than 30 mm. find the time for which the ant moved (in seconds). [একটি পিঁপড়া কয়েক সেকেন্ড ধরে চলে এবং প্রথম সেকেন্ডে 3 মিমি এবং প্রতিটি পরবর্তী সেকেন্ডে তার পূর্বসূরীর চেয়ে 4 মিমি বেশি অতিক্রম করে। যদি পিঁপড়াটি প্রথম সেকেন্ডে 1 মিমি এবং প্রতিটি পরবর্তী সেকেন্ডে 8 মিমি বেশি অতিক্রম করত, তাহলে একই সময়ে তার অতিক্রম করা পথ এবং প্রকৃত পথের মধ্যে পার্থক্য 6 মিমি থেকে বেশি কিন্তু 30 মিমি থেকে কম হত। পিঁপড়াটি কত সময় ধরে চলেছিল (সেকেন্ডে)?]

  • 5 sec
  • 4 sec
  • 6 sec
  • 2 sec

Solution:

Case 1 (Actual Path): Arithmetic Progression (AP)

First term \(a_1 = 3\). Common difference \(d_1 = 4\).

Sum of distances after n seconds \(S_1(n) = \frac{n}{2}[2a_1 + (n-1)d_1] = \frac{n}{2}[2(3) + (n-1)4] = \frac{n}{2}[6 + 4n - 4] = \frac{n}{2}[4n + 2] = n(2n + 1) = 2n^2 + n\).

Case 2 (Hypothetical Path): Arithmetic Progression (AP)

First term \(a_2 = 1\). Common difference \(d_2 = 8\).

Sum of distances after n seconds \(S_2(n) = \frac{n}{2}[2a_2 + (n-1)d_2] = \frac{n}{2}[2(1) + (n-1)8] = \frac{n}{2}[2 + 8n - 8] = \frac{n}{2}[8n - 6] = n(4n - 3) = 4n^2 - 3n\).

Difference between paths = \(|S_2(n) - S_1(n)|\) Difference = \(|(4n^2 - 3n) - (2n^2 + n)| = |4n^2 - 3n - 2n^2 - n| = |2n^2 - 4n|\).

We are given \(6 < |2n^2 - 4n| < 30\).

Since \(n\) (time) must be positive, let's check values of n from the options:

  • If n=2: Difference = \(|2(2^2) - 4(2)| = |2(4) - 8| = |8 - 8| = 0\). Does not satisfy \(6 < 0 < 30\).
  • If n=4: Difference = \(|2(4^2) - 4(4)| = |2(16) - 16| = |32 - 16| = 16\). Satisfies \(6 < 16 < 30\).
  • If n=5: Difference = \(|2(5^2) - 4(5)| = |2(25) - 20| = |50 - 20| = 30\). Does not satisfy \(< 30\).
  • If n=6: Difference = \(|2(6^2) - 4(6)| = |2(36) - 24| = |72 - 24| = 48\). Does not satisfy \(< 30\).

The only value that satisfies the condition is n=4 seconds.

Answer: b. 4 sec

8. A group of men decided to do a job in 4 days. But since 20 men dropped out every day, the job completed at the end of the 7th day. How many men were there at the beginning? [একদল লোক ৪ দিনে একটি কাজ করার সিদ্ধান্ত নেয়। কিন্তু প্রতিদিন ২০ জন লোক চলে যাওয়ায় কাজটি ৭ম দিনের শেষে সম্পন্ন হয়। শুরুতে কতজন লোক ছিল?]

  • 240
  • 140
  • 280
  • 150

Solution:

Let the initial number of men be M.

Let the work done by one man in one day be 1 unit.

Total work planned = Initial Men × Planned Days = M × 4 = 4M units.

Actual work done:

Day 1: M men work => Work = M

Day 2: (M-20) men work => Work = M-20

Day 3: (M-40) men work => Work = M-40

Day 4: (M-60) men work => Work = M-60

Day 5: (M-80) men work => Work = M-80

Day 6: (M-100) men work => Work = M-100

Day 7: (M-120) men work => Work = M-120

Total actual work = Sum of work done over 7 days.

Total actual work = M + (M-20) + (M-40) + (M-60) + (M-80) + (M-100) + (M-120)

Total actual work = 7M - (20+40+60+80+100+120)

Sum of AP (20...120) = \(\frac{n}{2}(a+l) = \frac{6}{2}(20+120) = 3(140) = 420\).

Correct sum: 20+40+60+80+100+120 = 420. (Sum of first 6 multiples of 20).

Total actual work = 7M - 420.

The total actual work must equal the total planned work.

7M - 420 = 4M

3M = 420

M = 420 / 3 = 140.

The initial number of men was 140.

Answer: b. 140

9. If 40% of a number is equal to two-third of another number, what is the ratio of first number to the second number? [যদি একটি সংখ্যার ৪০% অন্য একটি সংখ্যার দুই-তৃতীয়াংশের সমান হয়, তাহলে প্রথম সংখ্যা এবং দ্বিতীয় সংখ্যার অনুপাত কত?]

  • 1:2
  • 1:3
  • 5:3
  • 4:3

Solution:

40% of the first number = 0.40 x = (4/10) x = (2/5) x.

Two-third of the second number = (2/3) y.

Given: 40% of x = (2/3) of y

\( \frac{2}{5} x = \frac{2}{3} y \)

Divide both sides by y (assuming y≠0):

\( \frac{2}{5} \frac{x}{y} = \frac{2}{3} \)

Multiply both sides by 5/2:

\( \frac{x}{y} = \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{5}{2} = \frac{5}{3} \)

The ratio x : y is 5 : 3.

11. A linear equation in two variables is of the form ax + by + c = 0, where [দুই চলকের একটি রৈখিক সমীকরণ ax + by + c = 0, যেখানে-]

  • a = 0, c = 0
  • a ≠ 0, b = 0
  • a = 0, b ≠ 0
  • a ≠ 0, b ≠ 0

Solution:

For the equation ax + by + c = 0 to represent a linear equation *in two variables* (x and y), at least one of the coefficients of the variables (a or b) must be non-zero. If both a and b were zero, the equation would become c = 0, which is either true or false and doesn't involve x or y.

The standard condition is that a and b are not *both* zero simultaneously. Options b and c are specific cases where it's still a linear equation, but potentially only in one variable effectively if the other coefficient is zero (e.g., ax+c=0 if b=0). Option d implies *both* must be non-zero, which isn't strictly necessary for it to be linear, but ensures it involves *both* variables. However, the most accurate condition encompassing all linear equations is that not both a and b are zero.

Comparing options: Options b and c allow it to be linear. Option a makes it trivial. Option d ensures it explicitly involves both x and y. Often, in the context of "linear equation in two variables", option d is implied, meaning both variables are present. But mathematically, the condition is \(a^2+b^2 \neq 0\).

Let's assume the question implies the equation must involve both variables explicitly.

Answer: d. a ≠ 0, b ≠ 0 (with the caveat that the actual condition is 'a and b are not both zero')

15. An individual is cycling at a speed of 25 km per hour. He catches his predecessor who had started earlier in two hours. What is the speed of his predecessor who had started 3 hours earlier?   • 15 kmph
  • 12 kmph
  • 10 kmph
  • 8 kmph

Solution:

Let the speed of the individual (cyclist) be \(S_C = 25\) kmph.

Let the speed of the predecessor be \(S_P\) kmph.

The predecessor started 3 hours earlier.

The cyclist catches the predecessor in 2 hours (after the cyclist starts).

In these 2 hours, the cyclist travels a distance \(D_C = S_C \times 2 = 25 \times 2 = 50\) km.

The predecessor has been traveling for a total of 3 hours (head start) + 2 hours (while cyclist traveled) = 5 hours.

In these 5 hours, the predecessor travels a distance \(D_P = S_P \times 5\).

When the cyclist catches the predecessor, they have both traveled the same distance from the cyclist's starting point (assuming they start from the same place).

Wait, the predecessor starts *earlier*, so they are at the same location when caught.

Let the starting point be 0. After 3 hours, the predecessor is at distance \(3 \times S_P\).

Now the cyclist starts. Let the time from when the cyclist starts be t. Cyclist's position: \(25t\). Predecessor's position: \( (3 \times S_P) + S_P t \). They meet when positions are equal, and this happens at t=2 hours.

\( 25 \times 2 = (3 \times S_P) + S_P \times 2 \)

\( 50 = 3 S_P + 2 S_P \)

\( 50 = 5 S_P \)

\( S_P = 50 / 5 = 10 \) kmph.

Answer: c. 10 kmph

10. For diatomic gasses γ— [ দ্বি-পারমাণবিক গ্যাসের জন্য γ— ]

  • 1.33
  • 1.41
  • 1.66
  • 1/273

Solution:

\(\gamma\) (gamma), the adiabatic index or heat capacity ratio, is given by \(\gamma = C_P / C_V\), where \(C_P\) is the molar specific heat at constant pressure and \(C_V\) is the molar specific heat at constant volume.

For an ideal diatomic gas (like N2, O2 at moderate temperatures, considering translational and rotational degrees of freedom but not vibrational), the degrees of freedom (f) is 5 (3 translational + 2 rotational).

\( C_V = \frac{f}{2} R = \frac{5}{2} R \)

\( C_P = C_V + R = \frac{5}{2} R + R = \frac{7}{2} R \)

\( \gamma = \frac{C_P}{C_V} = \frac{(7/2) R}{(5/2) R} = \frac{7}{5} = 1.4 \)

1.33 is approximately 4/3 (typical for polyatomic molecules with more degrees of freedom).

1.66 is approximately 5/3 (typical for monatomic gases like He, Ar, with f=3).

1/273 relates to temperature scales (Celsius to Kelvin offset).

Answer: b. 1.41 (approximately 1.4)
