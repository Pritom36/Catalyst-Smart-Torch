Section C – [General Math]

1. For a room with dimensions of 7 meters in length, 8 meters in width, and 9 meters in height, what is the combined area of the walls? [একটি কক্ষের দৈর্ঘ্য ৭ মিটার, প্রস্থ ৮ মিটার ও উচ্চতা ৯ মিটার হলে দেয়ালগুলির ক্ষেত্রফল কত?]

  • 270 m2
  • 260 m2
  • 265 m2
  • 255 m2

Solution:

The area of the four walls of a room (lateral surface area of a cuboid) is given by the formula: Area = 2 × (length + width) × height.

Length (l) = 7 m

Width (w) = 8 m

Height (h) = 9 m

Area = 2 × (l + w) × h

Area = 2 × (7 + 8) × 9

Area = 2 × (15) × 9

Area = 30 × 9 = 270 m2.

Answer: a. 270 m2

2. In a hall with dimensions of 40 meters, 25 meters, and 20 meters, if each person requires 200 cubic meters of space, how many people can be accommodated in the hall? [একটি হল যার দৈর্ঘ্য ৪০ মিটার, প্রস্থ ২৫ মিটার, ও উচ্চতা ২০ মিটার হলে প্রত্যেক মানুষের জন্য ২০০ ঘনমিটার স্থান লাগলে কতজন মানুষ থাকতে পারবে?]

  • 150
  • 140
  • 120
  • 100

Solution:

First, calculate the total volume of the hall.

Volume = Length × Width × Height

Volume = 40 m × 25 m × 20 m

Volume = 1000 × 20 = 20000 m3.

Space required per person = 200 m3.

Number of people = Total Volume / Space per person

Number of people = 20000 m3 / 200 m3/person

Number of people = 100.

Answer: d. 100

3. An uneducated retailer marks all his goods at 50% above the cost price and thinking that he will still make 25% profit, offers a discount of 25% on the marked price. What is his actual profit on the sales? [একজন নিরক্ষর বিক্রেতা তার সব পণ্য ক্রয়মূল্যের ৫০% বেশি মূল্য মার্ক করে রাখে এবং মার্ককৃত মূল্যের উপর ২৫% ছাড় দেয়। তার প্রকৃত লাভ কত?]

  • 11%
  • 11.5%
  • 10%
  • 12.5%

Solution:

Let the Cost Price (CP) be 100.

Marked Price (MP) is 50% above CP.

MP = CP + 0.50 × CP = 1.50 × CP = 1.50 × 100 = 150.

Discount = 25% on MP.

Discount amount = 0.25 × MP = 0.25 × 150 = 37.5.

Selling Price (SP) = MP - Discount amount = 150 - 37.5 = 112.5.

Actual Profit = SP - CP = 112.5 - 100 = 12.5.

Actual Profit Percentage = \(\frac{\text{Actual Profit}}{CP} \times 100 = \frac{12.5}{100} \times 100 = 12.5\%\)

Answer: d. 12.5%

4. If a merchant offers a discount of 40% on the marked price of his goods and thus ends up selling at cost price, what was the % mark up? [একজন বিক্রেতা মার্ককৃত মূল্যের উপর ৪০% ছাড় দেওয়ায় বিক্রয়মূল্য ক্রয়মূল্যের সমান দাঁড়ায়। মার্ককৃত মূল্য ক্রয়মূল্য থেকে কত % বেশি ছিল?]

  • 28.57%
  • 40%
  • 66.66%
  • 58.33%

Solution:

Let the Cost Price (CP) be C and Marked Price (MP) be M.

Selling Price (SP) = Cost Price (C).

Discount = 40% on MP = 0.40 M.

SP = MP - Discount

C = M - 0.40 M

C = 0.60 M

M = \(\frac{C}{0.60} = \frac{10}{6} C = \frac{5}{3} C\).

Mark up amount = MP - CP = M - C = \(\frac{5}{3} C - C = \frac{2}{3} C\).

Percentage Mark up = \(\frac{\text{Mark up amount}}{CP} \times 100\)

Percentage Mark up = \(\frac{\frac{2}{3} C}{C} \times 100 = \frac{2}{3} \times 100 = 66.66...\%\)

Answer: c. 66.66%

5. In an election between two candidates, one got 60% of the total valid votes. 10% of the votes were invalid. If the total number of votes was 8000. How many votes did the other candidate get? [দুই প্রার্থীর মধ্যে নির্বাচনে একজন বৈধ ভোটের ৬০% পান। ১০% ভোট অবৈধ ছিল। যদি মোট ভোটের সংখ্যা ৮০০০ হয়, তাহলে অন্যজন বৈধ ভোট কত পান?]

  • 2220
  • 2500
  • 2650
  • 2880

Solution:

Total votes = 8000.

Invalid votes = 10% of 8000 = 0.10 × 8000 = 800.

Valid votes = Total votes - Invalid votes = 8000 - 800 = 7200.

Candidate 1 got 60% of valid votes.

Votes for Candidate 1 = 0.60 × 7200 = 4320.

The other candidate (Candidate 2) got the remaining valid votes.

Percentage of valid votes for Candidate 2 = 100% - 60% = 40%.

Votes for Candidate 2 = 40% of valid votes = 0.40 × 7200.

Votes for Candidate 2 = 720 × 4 = 2880.

Alternatively: Votes for Candidate 2 = Valid votes - Votes for Candidate 1 = 7200 - 4320 = 2880.

Answer: d. 2880

6. A train passes two bridges of length 800 m and 400 m in 100 seconds and 60 seconds respectively. The length of the train is: [একটি ট্রেন ৮০০ মিটার ও ৪০০ মিটার দীর্ঘ দুটি ব্রিজ যথাক্রমে ১০০ সেকেন্ড ও ৬০ সেকেন্ডে পার করে যায়। ট্রেনের দৈর্ঘ্য কত?]

  • 80 m
  • 90 m
  • 200 m
  • 150 m

Solution:

Let the length of the train be L meters and its speed be S m/s.

When passing a bridge, the total distance covered is (Length of train + Length of bridge).

Case 1 (Bridge 1): Distance = L + 800 m. Time = 100 s.

Speed \(S = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{L + 800}{100}\)

Case 2 (Bridge 2): Distance = L + 400 m. Time = 60 s.

Speed \(S = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{L + 400}{60}\)

Since the speed is the same in both cases, equate the expressions for S:

\( \frac{L + 800}{100} = \frac{L + 400}{60} \)

Multiply both sides by 300 (LCM of 100 and 60):

\( 3(L + 800) = 5(L + 400) \)

\( 3L + 2400 = 5L + 2000 \)

\( 2L = 2400 - 2000 = 400 \)

\( L = 200 \) meters.

Answer: c. 200 m

7. A man goes downstream with a boat to some destination and returns upstream to his original place in 5 hours. If the speed of the boat in still water and the stream are 10 kmph and 4 kmph respectively, the distance of the destination from the starting place is: [একজন লোক স্রোতের অনুকূলে একটি গন্তব্যে যায় এবং ৫ ঘন্টায় স্রোতের প্রতিকূলে তার পূর্বের স্থানে ফিরে আসে। যদি স্থির পানিতে নৌকা এবং স্রোতের বেগ যথাক্রমে ১০ কিমি/ঘন্টা ও ৪ কিমি/ঘন্টা হয়, তাহলে শুরুর স্থান থেকে গন্তব্যের দূরত্ব কত?]

  • 16 km
  • 18 km
  • 21 km
  • 25 km

Solution:

Speed of boat in still water (Sb) = 10 kmph.

Speed of stream (Ss) = 4 kmph.

Speed downstream (Sd) = Sb + Ss = 10 + 4 = 14 kmph.

Speed upstream (Su) = Sb - Ss = 10 - 4 = 6 kmph.

Let the distance to the destination be D km.

Time taken downstream (Td) = Distance / Speed = D / 14 hours.

Time taken upstream (Tu) = Distance / Speed = D / 6 hours.

Total time = Td + Tu = 5 hours.

\( \frac{D}{14} + \frac{D}{6} = 5 \)

Find a common denominator (LCM of 14 and 6 is 42).

\( \frac{3D}{42} + \frac{7D}{42} = 5 \)

\( \frac{10D}{42} = 5 \)

\( 10D = 5 \times 42 = 210 \)

\( D = \frac{210}{10} = 21 \) km.

Answer: c. 21 km

8. There is a group of 5 boys and 2 girls. The two groups working together can do four times as much work as a boy and a girl. Ratio of working capacities of a boy and a girl is? [৫ জন ছেলে ও ২ জন মেয়ের একটি গ্রুপ আছে। এই গ্রুপটি একত্রে একজন ছেলে ও একজন মেয়ের ৪ গুণ কাজ করতে পারে। ছেলে ও মেয়ের কর্মদক্ষতার অনুপাত?]

  • 2:1
  • 2:3
  • 1:3
  • 1:2

Solution:

Let the working capacity (rate) of a boy be B units/time.

Let the working capacity (rate) of a girl be G units/time.

Capacity of the group (5 boys, 2 girls) = 5B + 2G.

Capacity of one boy and one girl = B + G.

Given that the group's capacity is four times the capacity of one boy and one girl:

\( 5B + 2G = 4 \times (B + G) \)

\( 5B + 2G = 4B + 4G \)

Rearrange to find the ratio B:G:

\( 5B - 4B = 4G - 2G \)

\( B = 2G \)

This means the capacity of one boy is twice the capacity of one girl.

The ratio of working capacities (Boy : Girl) is B : G = 2G : G = 2 : 1.

Answer: a. 2:1

9. Srabon can do a piece of work in 20 days. He started the work and left after some days, when 25% work was done. After it Subha joined and completed it working for 10 days. In how many days Srabon and Subha can do the complete work, working together? [শ্রাবণ একটি কাজ ২০ দিনে করতে পারে। সে কাজটি শুরু করে এবং ২৫% হওয়ার পর কাজটি ছেড়ে যায়। এরপর শুভ কাজটি শুরু করে এবং ১০ দিন কাজ করে শেষ করে। শ্রাবণ ও শুভ কতদিনে সম্পূর্ণ কাজটি একত্রে শেষ করতে পারবে?]

  • 6
  • 8
  • 10
  • 12

Solution:

Srabon's rate = 1/20 job/day.

Srabon completed 25% (or 1/4) of the work.

Time Srabon worked = Work done / Rate = (1/4) / (1/20) = 20/4 = 5 days.

Work remaining = 1 - 1/4 = 3/4 job.

Subha completed the remaining 3/4 job in 10 days.

Subha's rate = Work done / Time = (3/4) / 10 = 3/40 job/day.

Combined rate of Srabon and Subha = Srabon's rate + Subha's rate

Combined rate = 1/20 + 3/40 = 2/40 + 3/40 = 5/40 = 1/8 job/day.

Time to complete the work together = Work / Combined rate = 1 / (1/8) = 8 days.

Answer: b. 8

10. A track covers a distance of 550 meters in 1 minute whereas a bus covers a distance of 33 kms in 45 minutes. The ratio of their speeds is: [একটি ট্র্যাক ১ মিনিটে ৫৫০ মিটার যায় যেখানে একটি বাস ৪৫ মিনিটে ৩৩ কিমি যায়। তাদের বেগের অনুপাত?]

  • 1:2
  • 1:3
  • 3:4
  • 2:1

Solution:

Calculate speed of the track.

Distance = 550 m. Time = 1 min = 60 s.

Speed of Track (St) = Distance / Time = 550 m / 60 s = 55/6 m/s.

Calculate speed of the bus.

Distance = 33 km = 33000 m. Time = 45 min = 45 × 60 s = 2700 s.

Speed of Bus (Sb) = Distance / Time = 33000 m / 2700 s = 330 / 27 = 110 / 9 m/s.

Ratio of speeds (Track : Bus) = St : Sb = \(\frac{55}{6} : \frac{110}{9}\)

To simplify the ratio, multiply both sides by the LCM of 6 and 9, which is 18.

Ratio = \( (\frac{55}{6} \times 18) : (\frac{110}{9} \times 18) \)

Ratio = \( (55 \times 3) : (110 \times 2) \)

Ratio = \( 165 : 220 \)

Divide both sides by their greatest common divisor (55).

Ratio = \( \frac{165}{55} : \frac{220}{55} = 3 : 4 \)

Answer: c. 3:4

11. Total number of men, women and children working in a factory is 18. They earn Tk. 4000 in a day. If the sum of the wages of all men, all women and all children is in ratio of 18 : 10 : 12 and if the wages of an individual man, woman and child is in ratio 6 : 5 : 3, then how much a woman earn in a day? [একটি ফ্যাক্টরিতে পুরুষ, মহিলা ও শিশুর সংখ্যা ১৮। তারা একদিনে ৪০০০ টাকা আয় করে। যদি সকল পুরুষ, মহিলা ও শিশুর পারিশ্রমিকের অনুপাত ১৮:১০:১২ হয় এবং একজন পুরুষ, একজন মহিলা ও একজন শিশুর পারিশ্রমিকের অনুপাত ৬:৫:৩ হয়, তাহলে একজন মহিলার একদিনের পারিশ্রমিক কত?]

  • Tk. 120
  • Tk. 150
  • Tk. 250
  • Tk. 400

Solution:

Let M, W, C be the number of men, women, children. M + W + C = 18.

Total wages = 4000.

Ratio of total wages (Men : Women : Children) = 18 : 10 : 12.

Sum of ratio parts = 18 + 10 + 12 = 40.

Total wages of all women = \( \frac{10}{40} \times 4000 = \frac{1}{4} \times 4000 = 1000 \).

Let the individual wage ratio be \(6k, 5k, 3k\) for a man, woman, child respectively.

Total wages for men = M × (6k).

Total wages for women = W × (5k).

Total wages for children = C × (3k).

Ratio of total wages = \( (M \times 6k) : (W \times 5k) : (C \times 3k) = 6M : 5W : 3C \).

This ratio is given as 18 : 10 : 12.

So, \( 6M : 5W : 3C = 18 : 10 : 12 \).

From the ratios: \( 6M = 18 \times R \implies M = 3R \) \( 5W = 10 \times R \implies W = 2R \) \( 3C = 12 \times R \implies C = 4R \) (where R is some proportionality constant)

Total number of people: \( M + W + C = 3R + 2R + 4R = 9R \).

We know M + W + C = 18. So, \( 9R = 18 \implies R = 2 \).

Number of women W = 2R = 2 × 2 = 4.

Total wages earned by all women = 1000.

Wage of one woman = Total wages of women / Number of women

Wage of one woman = 1000 / 4 = 250.

Answer: c. Tk. 250

12. If sin A = 1/2, then the value of cot A is

  • √3
  • 1/√3
  • √3/2
  • 1

Solution:

Given \(\sin A = 1/2\). Assume A is in the first quadrant (standard for basic trig values).

We know \(\sin^2 A + \cos^2 A = 1\).

\( \cos^2 A = 1 - \sin^2 A = 1 - (1/2)^2 = 1 - 1/4 = 3/4 \).

\( \cos A = \sqrt{3/4} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \) (since A is in the first quadrant, cos A is positive).

\( \cot A = \frac{\cos A}{\sin A} = \frac{\sqrt{3}/2}{1/2} = \sqrt{3} \).

Alternatively, if \(\sin A = 1/2\), then \(A = 30^\circ\). \(\cot 30^\circ = \sqrt{3}\).

Answer: a. √3

Using the fundamental identity \(\cos^2 A + \sin^2 A = 1\).

Therefore, the value of the expression is 1.

01. Evaluate \(\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} dx\)

In this case, \(a^2 = 1\), so \(a = 1\).

02. Find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) where y = cos (sinx)

  • cos (sinx) cosx
  • sin (cosx) sinx
  • cos sinx sinx
  • -sin (sinx) cosx

Solution:

We need to differentiate \(y = \cos(\sin x)\) using the chain rule.

Let \(u = \sin x\). Then \(y = \cos u\).

\( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \times \frac{du}{dx} \)

\( \frac{dy}{du} = \frac{d}{du}(\cos u) = -\sin u \)

\( \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx}(\sin x) = \cos x \)

\( \frac{dy}{dx} = (-\sin u) \times (\cos x) \)

Substitute back \(u = \sin x\):

\( \frac{dy}{dx} = -\sin(\sin x) \cos x \)

Answer: d. -sin (sinx) cosx

03. What is the value of \(\lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{1 + \sin x}{\cos x}\right)^{1/x}\)?

  • 1
  • e
  • -1
  • 10

Solution:

This limit is of the indeterminate form \(1^\infty\) as \(x \to 0\), since \(\frac{1+\sin 0}{\cos 0} = \frac{1+0}{1} = 1\) and the exponent \(1/x \to \infty\).

Let \(L = \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{1 + \sin x}{\cos x}\right)^{1/x}\). Take the natural logarithm:

\( \ln L = \lim_{x \to 0} \ln \left[ \left(\frac{1 + \sin x}{\cos x}\right)^{1/x} \right] \)

\( \ln L = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1}{x} \ln \left(\frac{1 + \sin x}{\cos x}\right) = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\ln(1 + \sin x) - \ln(\cos x)}{x} \)

This is of the form 0/0, so we can use L'Hopital's Rule.

Derivative of numerator: \( \frac{\cos x}{1 + \sin x} - \frac{-\sin x}{\cos x} = \frac{\cos x}{1 + \sin x} + \frac{\sin x}{\cos x} \)

Derivative of denominator: \( 1 \)

\( \ln L = \lim_{x \to 0} \left( \frac{\cos x}{1 + \sin x} + \frac{\sin x}{\cos x} \right) \)

Substitute \(x=0\):

\( \ln L = \frac{\cos 0}{1 + \sin 0} + \frac{\sin 0}{\cos 0} = \frac{1}{1+0} + \frac{0}{1} = 1 + 0 = 1 \)

Since \(\ln L = 1\), then \(L = e^1 = e\).

Answer: b. e

04. The focal distance of a point on the parabola y2 = 8x is 10. What will be its coordinates? [y2 = 8x পরাবৃত্তের উপর কোনো বিন্দুর ফোকাস দূরত্ব 10. বিন্দুটির স্থানাঙ্ক কত?]

  • (10, ±10)
  • (7, ±7)
  • (5, ±5)
  • (8, ±8)

Solution:

The parabola is \(y^2 = 8x\). This is of the form \(y^2 = 4ax\).

Comparing, \(4a = 8 \implies a = 2\).

The focus F is at \((a, 0) = (2, 0)\).

The directrix is the line \(x = -a \implies x = -2\).

For any point P(x, y) on the parabola, the focal distance (distance from P to the focus F) is equal to the distance from P to the directrix.

Distance from P(x, y) to directrix \(x = -2\) is \(|x - (-2)| = |x+2|\).

Since for \(y^2=8x\), \(x \ge 0\), the distance is \(x+2\).

Focal distance = \(x+2\).

We are given the focal distance is 10.

\( x + 2 = 10 \implies x = 8 \).

Now find the y-coordinate(s) using the parabola equation: \(y^2 = 8x\).

\( y^2 = 8(8) = 64 \)

\( y = \pm \sqrt{64} = \pm 8 \).

The coordinates are (8, 8) and (8, -8).

Answer: d. (8, ±8)

  • Electron pass from lower to higher potential

08. What is the escape velocity of Mars? (The diameter of Mars is 6600 km and gravitational acceleration is 3.6 m/s2) [মঙ্গল গ্রহের মুক্তিবেগ কত?]

  • 48.75 ms-1
  • 487.4 ms-1
  • 4874 ms-1
  • 48740 ms-1

Solution:

Escape velocity \(v_e\) can be calculated using \(v_e = \sqrt{2gR}\), where g is the surface gravitational acceleration and R is the radius of the planet.

Diameter = 6600 km, so Radius \(R = 6600 / 2 = 3300\) km = \(3300 \times 10^3\) m = \(3.3 \times 10^6\) m.

Gravitational acceleration \(g = 3.6\) m/s2.

\( v_e = \sqrt{2 \times (3.6 \text{ m/s}^2) \times (3.3 \times 10^6 \text{ m})} \)

\( v_e = \sqrt{7.2 \times 3.3 \times 10^6} \)

\( v_e = \sqrt{23.76 \times 10^6} \)
