01. The area of a trapezium with parallel sides measuring 6 cm and 10 cm is 32 square centimeters. What is the distance between these parallel sides? [একটি ট্রাপিজিয়ামের ক্ষেত্রফল ৩২ বর্গসেমি যেখানে সমান্তরাল বাহুদ্বয়ের দৈর্ঘ্য ৬ সেমি ও ১০ সেমি। সমান্তরাল বাহুদ্বয়ের মধ্যবর্তী দূরত্ব কত?]

Options:

  • 2 cm
  • 4 cm
  • 5 cm
  • 8 cm

Solution:

The formula for the area of a trapezium is:

\[ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (\text{sum of parallel sides}) \times \text{height} \]

Given:

  • Area = 32 cm\(^2\)
  • Parallel side a = 6 cm
  • Parallel side b = 10 cm
  • Height (distance between parallel sides) h = ?

Substitute the values into the formula:

\[ 32 = \frac{1}{2} \times (6 + 10) \times h \]

\[ 32 = \frac{1}{2} \times 16 \times h \]

\[ 32 = 8 \times h \]

\[ h = \frac{32}{8} = 4 \, \text{cm} \]

Answer: b. 4 cm

02. The rhombus has side lengths of 10 cm, and one of its diagonals measures 16 cm. What is the area of the rhombus? [একটি রম্বসের এক বাহুর দৈর্ঘ্য ১০ সেমি এবং একটি কর্ণের দৈর্ঘ্য ১৬ সেমি। রম্বসের ক্ষেত্রফল কত?]

Options:

  • 96 cm\(^2\)
  • 95 cm\(^2\)
  • 94 cm\(^2\)
  • 93 cm\(^2\)

Solution:

Let the side length be \(s = 10\) cm and the diagonals be \(d_1\) and \(d_2\). We are given \(d_1 = 16\) cm.

The diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other at right angles. This forms four congruent right-angled triangles. The hypotenuse of each triangle is the side \(s\), and the legs are half of each diagonal (\(d_1/2\) and \(d_2/2\)).

We have \(d_1/2 = 16/2 = 8\) cm.

Using the Pythagorean theorem:

\[ s^2 = \left(\frac{d_1}{2}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{d_2}{2}\right)^2 \]

\[ 10^2 = 8^2 + \left(\frac{d_2}{2}\right)^2 \]

\[ 100 = 64 + \left(\frac{d_2}{2}\right)^2 \]

\[ \left(\frac{d_2}{2}\right)^2 = 100 - 64 = 36 \]

\[ \frac{d_2}{2} = \sqrt{36} = 6 \, \text{cm} \]

So, the second diagonal \(d_2 = 2 \times 6 = 12\) cm.

The area of the rhombus is:

\[ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times d_1 \times d_2 \]

\[ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 16 \times 12 = 8 \times 12 = 96 \, \text{cm}^2 \]

Answer: a. 96 cm\(^2\)

03. Karim bought 6 bananas for 10tk and sold them at 4 for 6tk. What is his gain or loss? [করিম ৬টি কলা কিনল ১০ টাকায় এবং সেগুলোর ৪টি বিক্রয় করল ৬ টাকায়। তার লাভ/ক্ষতি কত?]

Options:

  • A.5%
  • b.6.67%
  • c.8.5%
  • d.10%

Solution:

Calculate the cost price (CP) per banana: CP = 10 tk / 6 bananas = 5/3 tk/banana.

Calculate the selling price (SP) per banana: SP = 6 tk / 4 bananas = 3/2 tk/banana.

Compare SP and CP: SP = 3/2 = 1.5 tk CP = 5/3 ≈ 1.67 tk

Since SP < CP, Karim incurred a loss.

Calculate the loss per banana: Loss = CP - SP = 5/3 - 3/2 = (10 - 9) / 6 = 1/6 tk/banana.

Calculate the loss percentage: Loss % = (Loss / CP) × 100% Loss % = [(1/6) / (5/3)] × 100% Loss % = (1/6) × (3/5) × 100% Loss % = (3 / 30) × 100% Loss % = (1 / 10) × 100% = 10%

Answer: d. 10%

05. A machine produce 600 units per minute. If the productivity decrease by ½%. What is the current production? [একটি যন্ত্র প্রতি মিনিটে ৬০০ ইউনিট উৎপাদন করে। যদি কর্মদক্ষতা ১/২% কমানো হয়, তাহলে বর্তমান উৎপাদন কত?]

06. Running at 5/4 of his usual speed, an athlete improves his timing by 5 minutes. The time he usually takes to run the same distance is: [নিজের স্বাভাবিক গতিবেগের ৫/৪ অংশ বেগে দৌড়ে একজন এথলেট দৌড় শেষ করে স্বাভাবিকের চেয়ে ৫ মিনিট কম সময় নিয়ে। স্বাভাবিক গতিতে ওই একই দূরত্ব অতিক্রমে প্রয়োজনীয় সময়?]

Options:

  • a.30 min.
  • b.28 min.
  • c.25 min.
  • d.23 min.

Solution:

Let the usual speed be \(S\) and the usual time be \(T\).

The distance \(D = S \times T\).

The new speed is \(S' = \frac{5}{4} S\).

The new time is \(T' = T - 5\) minutes (since timing improved).

The distance is the same, so \(D = S' \times T'\).

\[ S \times T = \left(\frac{5}{4} S\right) \times (T - 5) \]

Divide both sides by \(S\) (assuming \(S \neq 0\)):

\[ T = \frac{5}{4} (T - 5) \]

\[ T = \frac{5}{4} T - \frac{25}{4} \]

\[ \frac{25}{4} = \frac{5}{4} T - T \]

\[ \frac{25}{4} = \left(\frac{5}{4} - \frac{4}{4}\right) T \]

\[ \frac{25}{4} = \frac{1}{4} T \]

Multiply both sides by 4:

\[ T = 25 \, \text{minutes} \]

The time he usually takes is 25 minutes.

Answer: c. 25 min.

07. In a race of 1000m, Srabon can beat Subha by 100m. In a race of 400m, Subha beats Nisat by 40m. In a race of 500m, Srabon will beat Nisat by: [১০০০ মিটারের একটি দৌড় প্রতিযোগিতায় শ্রাবণ শুভকে হারাতে পারে ১০০ মিটারে। ৪০০ মিটারের একটি দৌড় প্রতিযোগিতায় শুভ নিশাতকে হারায় ৪০ মিটারে। ৫০০ মিটারের দৌড় প্রতিযোগিতায় শ্রাবণ নিশাতকে হারাবে-]

Options:

  • a. 95m
  • b. 50m
  • c. 45m
  • d. 60m

Solution:

Let \(V_S\), \(V_{Su}\), and \(V_N\) be the speeds of Srabon, Subha, and Nisat, respectively.

Race 1 (1000m): Srabon beats Subha by 100m. When Srabon runs 1000m, Subha runs 1000 - 100 = 900m in the same time. Ratio of speeds: \(V_S / V_{Su} = 1000 / 900 = 10 / 9\).

Race 2 (400m): Subha beats Nisat by 40m. When Subha runs 400m, Nisat runs 400 - 40 = 360m in the same time. Ratio of speeds: \(V_{Su} / V_N = 400 / 360 = 40 / 36 = 10 / 9\).

We need the ratio of Srabon's speed to Nisat's speed (\(V_S / V_N\)). \[ \frac{V_S}{V_N} = \frac{V_S}{V_{Su}} \times \frac{V_{Su}}{V_N} = \frac{10}{9} \times \frac{10}{9} = \frac{100}{81} \]

Race 3 (500m): Srabon vs Nisat. When Srabon runs 500m, let the distance Nisat runs in the same time be \(D_N\). \[ \frac{V_S}{V_N} = \frac{500}{D_N} \]

\[ \frac{100}{81} = \frac{500}{D_N} \]

\[ D_N = \frac{500 \times 81}{100} = 5 \times 81 = 405 \, \text{m} \]

In a 500m race, when Srabon finishes, Nisat has covered 405m.

Srabon beats Nisat by: \(500 - D_N = 500 - 405 = 95 \, \text{m}\).

Answer: a. 95m

08. Which of the following is equal to \(A \cup A'\)?

Options:

  • U
  • A
  • A'
  • U'

Solution:

In set theory, \(A'\) represents the complement of set A, which contains all elements in the universal set (U) that are not in A.

The union (\(\cup\)) of a set and its complement includes all elements that are in A, or in A', or in both. By definition, every element in the universal set U must be either in A or not in A (i.e., in A').

Therefore, \(A \cup A' = U\) (the Universal set).

Answer: a. U

09. A polynomial of degree n has: [n মাত্রার একটি বহুপদীর বৈশিষ্ট্য]

Options:

  • Only one zero
  • At least n zeroes
  • More than n zeroes
  • At most n zeroes

Solution:

The Fundamental Theorem of Algebra states that a polynomial of degree \(n\) with complex coefficients has exactly \(n\) complex roots (zeroes), counting multiplicity.

If we are considering only real roots (zeroes), a polynomial of degree \(n\) can have *at most* \(n\) real roots. Since the options don't specify complex vs. real and option d is standard terminology for real roots, we interpret it in that context.

Answer: d. At most n zeroes

10. If 10 men or 20 boys can make 260 mats in 20 days, then how many mats will be made by 8 men and 4 boys in 20 days? [যদি ১০ জন লোক ও ২০ জন বালক একত্রে ২৬০টি পাটি ২০ দিনে তৈরি করতে পারে, তাহলে ৮ জন লোক ও ৪ জন বালক একত্রে ২০ দিনে কতগুলো পাটি তৈরি করতে পারবে?]

Options:

  • 260
  • 240
  • 280
  • 520

Solution:

Let the rate of work of one man be M mats/day and one boy be B mats/day.

From the problem statement: Work = Rate × Time × Number of workers

Condition 1: 10 men make 260 mats in 20 days. \(260 = 10 \times M \times 20 \implies 200 M = 260 \implies M = \frac{260}{200} = 1.3\) mats/man-day.

Condition 2: 20 boys make 260 mats in 20 days. \(260 = 20 \times B \times 20 \implies 400 B = 260 \implies B = \frac{260}{400} = 0.65\) mats/boy-day.

(Note: This means 1 man does the work of 2 boys, as \(1.3 = 2 \times 0.65\)).

Now, find the work done by 8 men and 4 boys in 20 days:

Total Rate = (Rate of 8 men) + (Rate of 4 boys) Total Rate = \(8 \times M + 4 \times B = 8 \times 1.3 + 4 \times 0.65\) Total Rate = \(10.4 + 2.6 = 13\) mats/day.

Total Mats = Total Rate × Time = \(13 \times 20 = 260\) mats.

Answer: a. 260

11. If A:B = 2:3, B:C = 4:5, C:D = 5:9, A:D is equal to:

Options:

  • 11 : 17
  • 8 : 27
  • 5 : 9
  • 2 : 9

Solution:

We need to find the combined ratio A:B:C:D to find A:D.

1. Combine A:B and B:C. The common term is B. A:B = 2:3 B:C = 4:5 Find LCM of 3 and 4, which is 12. A:B = (2×4):(3×4) = 8:12 B:C = (4×3):(5×3) = 12:15 So, A:B:C = 8:12:15.

2. Combine A:B:C and C:D. The common term is C. A:B:C = 8:12:15 C:D = 5:9 Find LCM of 15 and 5, which is 15. A:B:C = 8:12:15 (C is already 15) C:D = (5×3):(9×3) = 15:27 So, A:B:C:D = 8:12:15:27.

3. Find A:D from the combined ratio. A:D = 8:27.

Answer: b. 8 : 27

12. In a class, the number of girls is 20% more than that of the boys. The strength of the class is 66. If 4 more girls are admitted to the class, the ratio of the number of boys to that of the girls is? [একটি ক্লাসে মেয়ের সংখ্যা ছেলের সংখ্যার চেয়ে ২০% বেশি। ক্লাসের শিক্ষার্থী সংখ্যা ৬৬ জন। যদি আরও ৪ জন মেয়ে ক্লাসটিতে ভর্তি হয়, তাহলে ক্লাসে ছেলে ও মেয়ের সংখ্যার অনুপাত কত হবে?]

Options:

  • 12:7
  • 3:4
  • 8:7
  • 4:3

Solution:

Let B be the number of boys and G be the number of girls.

Given: G is 20% more than B. \(G = B + 0.20 B = 1.20 B\)

Given: Total strength is 66. \(B + G = 66\)

Substitute G in the second equation: \(B + 1.20 B = 66\) \(2.20 B = 66\) \(B = \frac{66}{2.2} = \frac{660}{22} = 30\) boys.

Now find the initial number of girls: \(G = 1.20 \times B = 1.20 \times 30 = 36\) girls.

(Check: \(B + G = 30 + 36 = 66\)).

Now, 4 more girls are admitted. New number of girls \(G_{new} = 36 + 4 = 40\). The number of boys remains \(B = 30\).

The required ratio of boys to girls is: \(B : G_{new} = 30 : 40\)

13. If \(\cos X = \frac{2}{3}\) then \(\tan X\) is equal to:

Options:

  • 5/2
  • \(\sqrt{(5/2)}\)
  • \(\sqrt{5}/2\)
  • \(2/\sqrt{5}\)

Solution:

We know the trigonometric identity: \(\sin^2 X + \cos^2 X = 1\).

Substitute the given value of \(\cos X\): \(\sin^2 X + \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^2 = 1\) \(\sin^2 X + \frac{4}{9} = 1\) \(\sin^2 X = 1 - \frac{4}{9} = \frac{9}{9} - \frac{4}{9} = \frac{5}{9}\)

Taking the square root (assuming X is in a quadrant where sin is positive, like Q1): \(\sin X = \sqrt{\frac{5}{9}} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{3}\)

Now, find \(\tan X\): \(\tan X = \frac{\sin X}{\cos X} = \frac{\sqrt{5}/3}{2/3}\)

\(\tan X = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{3} \times \frac{3}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{2}\)

Answer: c. \(\sqrt{5}/2\)

14. Solve the following equation: \(x + 2 = 1/14\)

15. Solve the following system of equations: \(5/x + 4/y = 22\), \(3/x + 2/y = 12\)

Options:

  • (\(\frac{1}{2}\), \(\frac{2}{3}\))
  • (\(\frac{2}{3}\), \(\frac{4}{5}\))
  • (\(\frac{2}{5}\), \(\frac{3}{4}\))
  • (\(\frac{1}{2}\), \(\frac{1}{3}\))

Solution:

Let \(u = \frac{1}{x}\) and \(v = \frac{1}{y}\). The system becomes:

1) \(5u + 4v = 22\) 2) \(3u + 2v = 12\)

We can solve this system using elimination or substitution. Let's use elimination.

Multiply equation (2) by 2 to make the coefficients of v match (or opposite): \(2 \times (3u + 2v = 12) \implies 6u + 4v = 24\) (Equation 3)

Now subtract equation (1) from equation (3): \((6u + 4v) - (5u + 4v) = 24 - 22\) \(6u - 5u + 4v - 4v = 2\) \(u = 2\)

Substitute \(u = 2\) back into equation (2): \(3(2) + 2v = 12\) \(6 + 2v = 12\) \(2v = 12 - 6\) \(2v = 6\) \(v = 3\)

Now find x and y: \(u = \frac{1}{x} \implies 2 = \frac{1}{x} \implies x = \frac{1}{2}\) \(v = \frac{1}{y} \implies 3 = \frac{1}{y} \implies y = \frac{1}{3}\)

The solution is (x, y) = (\(\frac{1}{2}\), \(\frac{1}{3}\)).

Answer: d. (\(\frac{1}{2}\), \(\frac{1}{3}\))

02. What is the value of \(\frac{d}{dx} \sin\sqrt{x}\)? [\(\frac{d}{dx} \sin\sqrt{x}\) এর মান কোনটি?]

Options:

  • \(\frac{\sin\sqrt{x}}{2\sqrt{x}}\)
  • \(\frac{\sin\sqrt{x}}{\sqrt{x}}\)
  • \(\frac{\cos\sqrt{x}}{2\sqrt{x}}\)
  • \(\frac{\cos\sqrt{x}}{\sqrt{x}}\)

Solution:

Use the chain rule. Let \(y = \sin u\) and \(u = \sqrt{x} = x^{1/2}\).

\(\frac{dy}{du} = \cos u = \cos\sqrt{x}\)

\(\frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{2} x^{-1/2} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\)

\[ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \cdot \frac{du}{dx} = (\cos\sqrt{x}) \cdot \left(\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\right) = \frac{\cos\sqrt{x}}{2\sqrt{x}} \]

Answer: c. \(\frac{\cos\sqrt{x}}{2\sqrt{x}}\)

03. What is the value of \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan^{-1} x}{x}\)? [\(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan^{-1} x}{x}\) এর মান কোনটি?]

Options:

  • 0
  • 1/0
  • 1
  • -1

Solution:

As \(x \to 0\), \(\tan^{-1} x \to 0\) and \(x \to 0\). This is an indeterminate form of type \(\frac{0}{0}\), so we can use L'Hôpital's Rule.

\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\frac{d}{dx}(\tan^{-1} x)}{\frac{d}{dx}(x)} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\frac{1}{1+x^2}}{1} \]

Now, substitute \(x = 0\):

\[ \frac{\frac{1}{1+0^2}}{1} = \frac{1}{1} = 1 \]

Answer: c. 1

04. The equation \(9x^2 - 16y^2 = 144\) represents a: [\(9x^2 - 16y^2 = 144\) কিসের সমীকরণ?]

Options:

  • Parabola
  • Ellipse
  • Hyperbola
  • Circle

Solution:

Divide the equation by 144 to get the standard form:

\[ \frac{9x^2}{144} - \frac{16y^2}{144} = \frac{144}{144} \]

\[ \frac{x^2}{16} - \frac{y^2}{9} = 1 \]

This equation is in the standard form of a hyperbola: \(\frac{x^2}{a^2} - \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1\), where \(a^2 = 16\) and \(b^2 = 9\).

Answer: c. Hyperbola

05. What is the range of the function \(f(x) = \log x\)? [\(f(x) = \log x\) ফাংশনের রেঞ্জ কোনটি?]

Options:

  • All real Numbers
  • All positive Numbers
  • All negative Numbers
  • All irrational Numbers

Solution:

The function \(f(x) = \log x\) (assuming the base is \(e\) or 10) is defined for \(x > 0\). As \(x\) approaches 0 from the positive side (\(x \to 0^+\)), \(\log x\) approaches \(-\infty\). As \(x\) approaches infinity (\(x \to \infty\)), \(\log x\) approaches \(+\infty\). Therefore, the function can take any real value from \(-\infty\) to \(+\infty\).

The range is the set of all real numbers.

Answer: a. All real Numbers

06. Which of the following is the correct formula for the theory of relativity? [নিচের কোনটি আপেক্ষিক তত্ত্বের সঠিক সূত্র?]

Options:

  • \(E = mc^2\)
  • \(M = Ec^2\)
  • \(E = hf\)
  • \(E = \frac{1}{2}mc^2\)

Solution:

The famous mass-energy equivalence formula derived from Einstein's theory of special relativity is \(E = mc^2\), where E is energy, m is mass, and c is the speed of light in a vacuum.

\(E = hf\) is the Planck-Einstein relation for the energy of a photon (quantum mechanics).

\(E = \frac{1}{2}mv^2\) is the formula for kinetic energy in classical mechanics (using 'm' and 'v' instead of 'm' and 'c').

Answer: a. \(E = mc^2\)

07. The magnitude of the vector \(6\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}\) is equal to: [ভেক্টর \(6\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}\) এর মান?]

Options:

  • 5
  • 1
  • 7
  • 12

Solution:

The magnitude of a vector \(\mathbf{v} = a\mathbf{i} + b\mathbf{j} + c\mathbf{k}\) is given by \(|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}\).

For the vector \(6\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}\):

\[ \text{Magnitude} = \sqrt{6^2 + 2^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{36 + 4 + 9} = \sqrt{49} = 7 \]

08. A ball is thrown vertically upwards with an initial velocity of 30 m/s. What is the maximum height reached by the ball? (Assume \(g = 10 \, \text{m/s}^2\)) [একটি বল 30 m/s এর প্রাথমিক বেগ সহ উল্লম্বভাবে উপরের দিকে নিক্ষেপ করা হয়। বল সর্বোচ্চ কত উচ্চতায় পৌঁছছে?]

Options:

  • 45 m
  • 300 m
  • 450 m
  • 900 m

Solution:

We can use the kinematic equation: \(v^2 = u^2 + 2as\)

Here:

  • Initial velocity \(u = 30 \, \text{m/s}\)
  • Final velocity at maximum height \(v = 0 \, \text{m/s}\)
  • Acceleration due to gravity \(a = -g = -10 \, \text{m/s}^2\) (negative as it opposes upward motion)
  • Displacement (maximum height) \(s = ?\)

Substitute the values:

\[ 0^2 = (30)^2 + 2(-10)s \]

\[ 0 = 900 - 20s \]

\[ 20s = 900 \]

\[ s = \frac{900}{20} = 45 \, \text{m} \]

Answer: a. 45 m

09. A weight lifter lifts a 100 kg barbell a distance of 2 meters in 3 seconds. What is the power output of the weight lifter? [একজন ভারোত্তোলক 100 কেজি বারবেল 3 সেকেন্ডে 2 মিটার দূরত্বে উত্তোলন করে। ভারোত্তোলকের পাওয়ার আউটপুট কি?]

Options:

  • 666.67 W
  • 200 W
  • 6666.67 W
  • 20 W

Solution:

Power is the rate at which work is done: \(P = \frac{W}{t}\).

Work done against gravity is \(W = \text{Force} \times \text{Distance} = (mg) \times h\).

Given:

  • Mass \(m = 100 \, \text{kg}\)
  • Distance (height) \(h = 2 \, \text{m}\)
  • Time \(t = 3 \, \text{s}\)
  • Acceleration due to gravity \(g \approx 10 \, \text{m/s}^2\) (using the value common in such problems, which fits the options).

Calculate the work done:

\[ W = (100 \, \text{kg}) \times (10 \, \text{m/s}^2) \times (2 \, \text{m}) = 2000 \, \text{J} \]

Calculate the power:

\[ P = \frac{2000 \, \text{J}}{3 \, \text{s}} = 666.666... \, \text{W} \]

Rounding to two decimal places gives 666.67 W.

Answer: a. 666.67 W

10. During electric current flow through the conductor — [বৈদ্যুতিক প্রবাহের সময়, কন্ডাক্টরের মধ্যে যে পরিবর্তন হয়—]

Options:

  • Proton passes from higher to lower potential
  • Proton passes from lower to higher potential
  • Electrons passed from higher to lower potential
  • Electrons pass from lower to higher potential

Solution:

Electric current in metallic conductors is due to the flow of free electrons. Protons are fixed in the atomic nuclei and do not move to constitute the current.

Electrons are negatively charged particles. They are attracted towards higher (more positive) potential and repelled from lower (more negative) potential.

Therefore, electrons flow from a region of lower electric potential to a region of higher electric potential.

(Note: Conventional current direction is defined as the direction of flow of positive charge, which is opposite to the direction of electron flow, i.e., from higher to lower potential).

Answer: d. Electrons pass from lower to higher potential

04. By what process sorus is produced? [কোন প্রক্রিয়ায় সোরাস উৎপন্ন হয়?]

Options:

  • A. Sexual Reproduction
  • B. Asexual Reproduction
  • C. Vegetative Reproduction
  • D. Artificial

Solution:

A sorus (plural: sori) is a cluster of sporangia (structures producing and containing spores) found on the underside of fern fronds. Spores are typically produced through meiosis (part of the sexual life cycle), but their formation and release from the sorus are part of the asexual phase (sporophyte generation) which reproduces via spores. However, the question asks how the *sorus* itself is produced, which is part of the development of the mature sporophyte (the leafy fern plant). This plant grows from a zygote formed during sexual reproduction. If the question implies spore production *within* the sorus, it's linked to the alternation of generations involving both phases. If it refers to the *sorus structure*, it develops on the sporophyte. Often, spore production itself is considered asexual reproduction in the context of the sporophyte generation releasing spores. Given the options, 'Asexual Reproduction' (via spores produced in the sorus) is the most likely intended answer related to the function of the sorus.

Note: Fern life cycles are complex involving alternation of generations. Sori are structures on the asexual sporophyte generation which produce spores (often via meiosis, related to sexual cycle). Depending on interpretation, answers could vary, but 'Asexual Reproduction' via spores is a common association with sori function.

Answer: B. Asexual Reproduction

09. Which of the following acts as both an oxidizing agent and a reductant agent? [নিচের কোনটি জারক ও বিজারক উভয় হিসেবে কাজ করে?]

Options:

  • A. Ammonia (NH\(_{3}\))
  • B. Hydrogen peroxide (H\(_{2}\)O\(_{2}\))
  • C. Nitrous acid (HNO\(_{2}\))
  • D. Nitric Acid (HNO\(_{3}\))

Solution:

A substance can act as both an oxidizing and reducing agent if the element undergoing redox can exist in higher and lower oxidation states.

  • In H\(_{2}\)O\(_{2}\), Oxygen has an oxidation state of -1. It can be oxidized to 0 (in O\(_{2}\)) or reduced to -2 (in H\(_{2}\)O). Thus, H\(_{2}\)O\(_{2}\) can act as both.
  • In HNO\(_{2}\), Nitrogen has an oxidation state of +3. It can be oxidized to +5 (in HNO\(_{3}\)) or reduced to lower states (e.g., +2 in NO). Thus, HNO\(_{2}\) can also act as both.
  • In NH\(_{3}\), Nitrogen is -3, its lowest state, so it can only be oxidized (act as a reductant).
  • In HNO\(_{3}\), Nitrogen is +5, its highest common state, so it can usually only be reduced (act as an oxidant).

Both Hydrogen peroxide and Nitrous acid can act as both. However, Hydrogen peroxide is a more common textbook example. Without further context or specific reactions, H\(_{2}\)O\(_{2}\) is often the expected answer.

Answer: B. Hydrogen peroxide`

Note: Nitrous acid (C) also fits the description.

10. 1 Faraday =?

Options:

  • A. 96500 Coulomb
  • B. 95600 Coulomb
  • C. 96500 ampere
  • D. 9640 Coulomb

Solution:

One Faraday (F) is the magnitude of electric charge per mole of electrons. It is calculated as the product of the Avogadro constant (\(N_A\)) and the elementary charge (\(e\)).

\(F = N_A \times e \approx (6.022 \times 10^{23} \, \text{mol}^{-1}) \times (1.602 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C}) \approx 96485 \, \text{C/mol}\).

This value is commonly rounded to 96500 Coulombs per mole for calculations.

Ampere is the unit of current, not charge.

Answer: A. 96500 Coulomb
