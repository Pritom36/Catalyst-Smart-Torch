1. If Srabon takes a loan of 10000 taka, on a compounding flat Interest rate of 15% for 2 years, how much monthly payment will he need to make? [যদি শ্রাবণ 2 বছরের জন্য 15% চক্রবৃদ্ধি হারে 10000 টাকা ঋণ নেয়, তাহলে প্রতি মাসে তাকে পরিশোধ করতে হবে কত টাকা?] (Note: "compounding flat" is contradictory, assumes compound interest)

  • 422.22
  • 433.33
  • 455.55
  • 444.44

Solution:

This requires the annuity payment formula, assuming interest is compounded consistently with payments (i.e., monthly).

M = P [ i(1 + i)n ] / [ (1 + i)n – 1]

  • P = 10000
  • Annual Rate = 15% = 0.15
  • Monthly Rate (i) = 0.15 / 12 = 0.0125
  • Number of years = 2
  • Number of Payments (n) = 2 × 12 = 24 months

(1 + i) = 1.0125

(1 + i)n = (1.0125)24

Using a calculator: (1.0125)24 ≈ 1.34735

Numerator = i(1 + i)n ≈ 0.0125 × 1.34735 ≈ 0.0168419

Denominator = (1 + i)n – 1 ≈ 1.34735 - 1 = 0.34735

M ≈ 10000 × [ 0.0168419 / 0.34735 ]

M ≈ 10000 × 0.048487 ≈ 484.87 taka.

None of the options match 484.87.

Answer: (Calculation ~484.87 does not match options)

2. In a 300 meters race, Y can give a start of 20 meters to X, and Z can give a start of 30 meters to Y. The starts that Z can gives to X, in the same race is? [একটি 300 মিটার দৌড়ে, Y, X কে 20 মিটারের স্টার্ট দিতে পারে, এবং Z, Y কে 30 মিটারের স্টার্ট দিতে পারে। একই দৌড়ে Z, X কে যে স্টার্ট দিতে পারে তা হল:]

  • 74 meters
  • 57 meters
  • 64 meters
  • 48 meters

Solution:

Race distance = 300 m.

Y gives X a 20m start: When Y runs 300 m, X runs 300 - 20 = 280 m.

Ratio of speeds \( \frac{S_Y}{S_X} = \frac{300}{280} = \frac{30}{28} = \frac{15}{14} \).

Z gives Y a 30m start: When Z runs 300 m, Y runs 300 - 30 = 270 m.

Ratio of speeds \( \frac{S_Z}{S_Y} = \frac{300}{270} = \frac{30}{27} = \frac{10}{9} \).

We want the start Z gives to X. Need the ratio \(S_Z / S_X\).

\( \frac{S_Z}{S_X} = \frac{S_Z}{S_Y} \times \frac{S_Y}{S_X} = \frac{10}{9} \times \frac{15}{14} = \frac{150}{126} = \frac{25}{21} \).

This means when Z runs 25 units of distance, X runs 21 units in the same time.

When Z runs 300 m, X runs \( 300 \times \frac{S_X}{S_Z} = 300 \times \frac{21}{25} \).

Distance X runs = \( 12 \times 21 = 252 \) m.

The start Z can give to X = 300 m - 252 m = 48 meters.

Answer: d. 48 meters

3. In a family, the average age of a father and a mother is 33 years. The average age of the father, mother and their only son is 25 years. What is the age of the son? [একটি পরিবারে, বাবা ও মায়ের গড় বয়স 33 বছর। বাবা, মা এবং তাদের একমাত্র ছেলের গড় বয়স 25 বছর। ছেলের বয়স কত?]

  • 9 years
  • 7 years
  • 8 years
  • 10 years

Solution:

Let F = Father's age, M = Mother's age, S = Son's age.

Average age of Father and Mother = 33.

\( \frac{F + M}{2} = 33 \implies F + M = 33 \times 2 = 66 \).

Average age of Father, Mother, and Son = 25.

\( \frac{F + M + S}{3} = 25 \implies F + M + S = 25 \times 3 = 75 \).

Substitute (F + M) = 66 into the second equation:

\( 66 + S = 75 \)

\( S = 75 - 66 = 9 \).

The age of the son is 9 years.

Answer: a. 9 years

[একটি ব্যাগে 8টি সবুজ মার্বেল, 5টি লাল মার্বেল এবং 3টি হলুদ মার্বেল রয়েছে। যদি একটি মার্বেল এলোমেলোভাবে টানা হয়, তাহলে একটি লাল বা হলুদ মার্বেল টানার সম্ভাবনা কত?]

  • ⅜
  • ⅜
  • 7/16
  • 1/2

Solution:

Total number of marbles = 8 (green) + 5 (red) + 3 (yellow) = 16 marbles.

Number of red marbles = 5.

Number of yellow marbles = 3.

Number of red OR yellow marbles = 5 + 3 = 8.

12. If log 2 = 0.30103, the number of digits in 450 is

  • 30
  • 31
  • 100
  • 200

Solution:

The number of digits in a positive integer N is given by \( \lfloor \log_{10} N \rfloor + 1 \).

We need to find the number of digits in \( N = 4^{50} \).

Calculate \( \log_{10} N = \log_{10} (4^{50}) \).

Using \( \log(a^c) = c \log a \):

\( \log_{10} N = 50 \log_{10} 4 \).

\( \log_{10} 4 = \log_{10} (2^2) = 2 \log_{10} 2 \).

\( \log_{10} N = 50 \times (2 \log_{10} 2) = 100 \log_{10} 2 \).

Given \( \log_{10} 2 = 0.30103 \).

\( \log_{10} N = 100 \times 0.30103 = 30.103 \).

Now find the floor (greatest integer less than or equal to) of this value:

\( \lfloor 30.103 \rfloor = 30 \).

Number of digits = \( \lfloor \log_{10} N \rfloor + 1 = 30 + 1 = 31 \).

Answer: b. 31

  • 1050
  • 2550
  • 3050

New Length (L') = S + 40% of S = 10 + 0.40(10) = 10 + 4 = 14 units.

New Breadth (B') = S + 30% of S = 10 + 0.30(10) = 10 + 3 = 13 units.

New Area (Rectangle) = L' × B' = 14 × 13 = 182 sq units.

Increase in Area = New Area - Original Area = 182 - 100 = 82 sq units.

Percentage Increase = \( \frac{\text{Increase in Area}}{\text{Original Area}} \times 100 \)

Percentage Increase = \( \frac{82}{100} \times 100 = 82\% \).

Alternatively, use successive percentage change formula for area (multiplication): \( x + y + \frac{xy}{100} \).

Increase = \( 40 + 30 + \frac{40 \times 30}{100} = 70 + \frac{1200}{100} = 70 + 12 = 82\% \).

Answer: b. 82%

01. Slope of tangent line of which one of the following functions is not variable? [ নিচের কোন ফাংশনের স্পর্শক লাইনের ঢাল পরিবর্তনশীল নয়?]

  • y = xe

The slope of the tangent line is given by the derivative (dy/dx). We are looking for the function whose derivative is a constant (not variable, i.e., not dependent on x).

  • a. \( y = e^x \implies \frac{dy}{dx} = e^x \). Variable.
  • b. \( y = x \implies \frac{dy}{dx} = 1 \). Constant.
  • c. \( y = x^e \implies \frac{dy}{dx} = e x^{e-1} \). Variable (since e≠1).
  • d. \( y = e^{\ln x} \). Since \(e^{\ln x} = x\), this is the same as option b. \( y = x \implies \frac{dy}{dx} = 1 \). Constant.

03. In an ellipse if the major axis is 4 times of the minor axis then eccentricity = ? [ একটি উপবৃত্তে যদি প্রধান অক্ষ অপ্রধান অক্ষের 4 গুণ হয় তবে উৎকেন্দ্রিকতা = ?]

  • √3/2
  • √15 / 4
  • √3/4

Solution:

Let the semi-major axis be 'a' and the semi-minor axis be 'b'.

Major axis length = 2a.

Minor axis length = 2b.

Given: Major axis = 4 × Minor axis

\( 2a = 4 \times (2b) \)

\( 2a = 8b \implies a = 4b \).

Eccentricity (e) is related by \( b^2 = a^2 (1 - e^2) \).

\( e^2 = 1 - \frac{b^2}{a^2} \)

\( e^2 = 1 - \frac{b^2}{(4b)^2} = 1 - \frac{b^2}{16b^2} = 1 - \frac{1}{16} \)

\( e^2 = \frac{16 - 1}{16} = \frac{15}{16} \)

04. If the line x – py + 3 = 0 touches the parabola y2 = 4x then p = ? [ যদি x – py + 3 = 0 রেখাটি y2 = 4x পরাবৃত্তকে স্পর্শ করে তাহলে p = ?]

\( py = x + 3 \)

\( y = \frac{1}{p}x + \frac{3}{p} \).

So, slope \(m = 1/p\) and y-intercept \(c = 3/p\).

For the parabola \(y^2 = 4x\), compare with \(y^2 = 4ax\). We have \(4a = 4\), so \(a = 1\).

Apply the tangency condition \(c = a/m\):

\( \frac{3}{p} = \frac{1}{(1/p)} \)

\( \frac{3}{p} = p \)

\( 3 = p^2 \)

\( p = \pm \sqrt{3} \).

05. What is the value of \( \lim_{x\to\infty} \left(1 + \frac{2}{x}\right)^? \) (Exponent missing, assume x based on options)

06. In a river if the velocity of current is x km/h and velocity of a boat is 2x km/h then at what angle the boat should be rowed to reach directly opposite to the river? [একটি নদীতে স্রোতের বেগ x km/h এবং নৌকার বেগ 2x km/h হলে নদীটি সরাসরি বিপরীত দিকে পৌঁছাতে নৌকাটিকে কোন কোণে চালাতে হবে?]

  • 60°
  • 120°
  • 180°
  • 90°

Solution:

Let the velocity of the current be \( \vec{v}_c \) (magnitude x) along the river flow (say, positive x-direction).

Let the velocity of the boat relative to water be \( \vec{v}_{bw} \) (magnitude 2x).

Let the velocity of the boat relative to the ground be \( \vec{v}_{bg} \).

\( \vec{v}_{bg} = \vec{v}_{bw} + \vec{v}_c \).

To reach the point directly opposite, the resultant velocity \( \vec{v}_{bg} \) must be perpendicular to the current (directly across the river, say, positive y-direction).

Let the boat be rowed at an angle \(\alpha\) upstream relative to the perpendicular line across the river. The angle relative to the bank/flow direction (\(\theta\)) would be \(90^\circ + \alpha\).

We need the component of \( \vec{v}_{bw} \) along the river flow to exactly cancel \( \vec{v}_c \).

Component of \( \vec{v}_{bw} \) against the current = \( |\vec{v}_{bw}| \cos\alpha \) (where \(\alpha\) is angle relative to the upstream bank) OR \( |\vec{v}_{bw}| \sin\beta \) (where \(\beta\) is angle relative to the across-stream line). Let's use \(\theta\) as the angle relative to the *across-stream* direction, measured towards upstream.

The component of \( \vec{v}_{bw} \) along the river flow (upstream) is \( |\vec{v}_{bw}| \sin\theta \).

We need \( |\vec{v}_{bw}| \sin\theta = |\vec{v}_c| \).

\( (2x) \sin\theta = x \)

\( \sin\theta = x / (2x) = 1/2 \).

\( \theta = 30^\circ \).

This angle \(\theta=30^\circ\) is the angle upstream from the line directly across the river.

The angle relative to the *downstream* bank is \( 90^\circ + \theta = 90^\circ + 30^\circ = 120^\circ \).

The boat should be rowed at an angle of 120° with respect to the river flow (current direction).

Answer: b. 120°

07. A projectile is thrown with an angle of 60° and velocity 3 m/s then what will be the velocity at maximum height? [একটি প্রাসকে 60° কোণে এবং 3 m/s বেগে নিক্ষেপ করা হয় তাহলে সর্বোচ্চ উচ্চতায় বেগ কত হবে?]

  • 1 m/s
  • 1.5 m/s
  • 2 m/s
  • 3 m/s

Solution:

In projectile motion (neglecting air resistance), the horizontal component of velocity remains constant throughout the flight.

The vertical component of velocity changes due to gravity and becomes zero at the maximum height.

Initial velocity (\(v_0\)) = 3 m/s.

Launch angle (\(\theta\)) = 60°.

Initial horizontal velocity (\(v_{0x}\)) = \( v_0 \cos\theta = 3 \cos(60^\circ) \).

Initial vertical velocity (\(v_{0y}\)) = \( v_0 \sin\theta = 3 \sin(60^\circ) \).

At maximum height:

  • Vertical velocity (\(v_y\)) = 0.
  • Horizontal velocity (\(v_x\)) = \( v_{0x} \) (constant).

Velocity at maximum height = \(v_x = v_{0x} = 3 \cos(60^\circ) \).

Since \( \cos(60^\circ) = 1/2 \):

Velocity at maximum height = \( 3 \times (1/2) = 1.5 \) m/s.

Answer: b. 1.5 m/s

08. An iron of mass 2kg is crashed with a wall of the angle 300 and velocity 4 m/s and returns with the velocity of 4 m/s and angle 300. What will be the impulse of force? [2kg ভরের একটি লোহা 300 কোণে এবং 4 m/s বেগে একটি দেয়ালের সাথে সংঘর্ষে লিপ্ত হয় এবং 4 m/s বেগ এবং 300 কোণে ফিরে আসে। বলের ঘাত কত হবে?] (Assume angle is with the wall/normal)

  • 8 kgm/s
  • 16 kg m/s
  • 19 kgm/s
  • 18 kgm/s

Solution:

Impulse = Change in Momentum (\(\Delta p\)). Momentum \(p = mv\).

Mass (m) = 2 kg.

Initial speed = Final speed = 4 m/s.

The angle is 30°. Assume this is the angle the velocity vector makes with the normal to the wall.

Let the wall be along the y-axis. The normal is along the x-axis.

Initial velocity vector (\(\vec{v}_i\)):

  • Component normal to wall (x): \( -4 \cos(30^\circ) \) (moving towards wall)
  • Component parallel to wall (y): \( 4 \sin(30^\circ) \)

Final velocity vector (\(\vec{v}_f\)):

  • Component normal to wall (x): \( +4 \cos(30^\circ) \) (moving away from wall)
  • Component parallel to wall (y): \( 4 \sin(30^\circ) \) (assuming elastic collision parallel component doesn't change)

Initial momentum (\(\vec{p}_i = m \vec{v}_i\)):

  • x: \( 2 \times (-4 \cos 30^\circ) = -8 \cos 30^\circ \)
  • y: \( 2 \times (4 \sin 30^\circ) = 8 \sin 30^\circ \)

Final momentum (\(\vec{p}_f = m \vec{v}_f\)):

  • x: \( 2 \times (4 \cos 30^\circ) = 8 \cos 30^\circ \)
  • y: \( 2 \times (4 \sin 30^\circ) = 8 \sin 30^\circ \)

Change in momentum \(\Delta \vec{p} = \vec{p}_f - \vec{p}_i\).

\( \Delta p_x = (8 \cos 30^\circ) - (-8 \cos 30^\circ) = 16 \cos 30^\circ \).

\( \Delta p_y = (8 \sin 30^\circ) - (8 \sin 30^\circ) = 0 \).

Impulse \(\vec{J} = \Delta \vec{p} = (16 \cos 30^\circ) \hat{i} \).

Magnitude of Impulse = \( |\vec{J}| = 16 \cos 30^\circ \).

\( \cos 30^\circ = \sqrt{3}/2 \approx 0.866 \).

Impulse = \( 16 \times (\sqrt{3}/2) = 8\sqrt{3} \approx 8 \times 1.732 = 13.856 \) kg·m/s.

This doesn't match options. Let's assume the angle 30° was with the *wall* itself, not the normal.

Angle with normal = 90° - 30° = 60°.

Impulse magnitude = \( 16 \cos 60^\circ = 16 \times (1/2) = 8 \) kg·m/s.

This matches option a.

Answer: a. 8 kgm/s (Assuming angle 30° is with the wall)

10. Determine the height at which gravitational acceleration will decrease to 25% - (R=6.4 x106) [উচ্চতা নির্ধারণ করুন যেখানে মহাকর্ষীয় ত্বরণ 25% হ্রাস পাবে - (R=6.4 x106)]

  • 100 km
  • 250 km
  • 640 km
  • 6400 km

Solution:

Acceleration due to gravity at height h above the surface (\(g_h\)) is related to the surface gravity (g) and Earth's radius (R) by:

\( g_h = g \left( \frac{R}{R+h} \right)^2 \)

We want the height 'h' where the acceleration decreases *to* 25% of the surface value. This means \(g_h = 0.25 g = \frac{1}{4} g \).

(If it meant decrease *by* 25%, then \(g_h = g - 0.25g = 0.75g\)). Assuming "decrease to 25%".

\( \frac{1}{4} g = g \left( \frac{R}{R+h} \right)^2 \)

Cancel g:

\( \frac{1}{4} = \left( \frac{R}{R+h} \right)^2 \)

Take the square root of both sides:

\( \sqrt{\frac{1}{4}} = \frac{R}{R+h} \)

\( \frac{1}{2} = \frac{R}{R+h} \)

Cross-multiply:

\( R + h = 2R \)

\( h = 2R - R = R \)

The height required is equal to the Earth's radius.

Given R = 6.4 × 106 meters = 6400 km.

So, h = 6400 km.

Answer: d. 6400 km
