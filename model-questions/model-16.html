1. Length of a side of a rhombus = 30 cm. If the distance between any two parallel sides is 15m what will be the area of the rhombus? [একটি রম্বসের বাহুর দৈর্ঘ্য = ৩০ সেমি। যদি সমান্তরাল বাহু দুইটির মধ্যবর্তী দূরত্ব ১৫ মিটার হয় তাহলে এর ক্ষেত্রফল কত?] (Note: Unit mismatch cm vs m)

  • 45 m2
  • 450 m2
  • 4500 m2
  • 4.5 m2

Solution:

Area of a rhombus = Base × Height.

The "base" is the length of any side.

The "height" is the perpendicular distance between that side and the opposite parallel side.

Given: Side length (Base) = 30 cm = 0.30 m.

Distance between parallel sides (Height) = 15 m.

There seems to be a significant unit mismatch or value error. A rhombus with side 30 cm cannot have a height of 15 m (1500 cm) between parallel sides, as the height cannot be greater than the side length.

Let's assume the side is 30 m and height is 15 m.

Area = Base × Height = 30 m × 15 m = 450 m2.

Alternatively, assume the side is 30 cm and the height is 15 cm.

Area = 30 cm × 15 cm = 450 cm2. To convert to m2, divide by 10000: 450 / 10000 = 0.045 m2.

Given the options are in m2 and 450 is an option, it's most likely the side length was intended to be 30 m.

Answer: b. 450 m2 (Assuming side = 30m, height = 15m)

2. If the adjacent angles of the rhombus are in the ratio of 4 : 5 what will be the difference between the larger angle and smaller angle? [রম্বসের সন্নিহিত কোণগুলি ৪ : ৫ অনুপাতে হলে বৃহত্তর কোণ এবং ক্ষুদ্রতর কোণের মধ্যে পার্থক্য কী?]

  • 10°
  • 20°
  • 15°
  • 25°

Solution:

Adjacent angles in a rhombus (or any parallelogram) are supplementary, meaning they add up to 180°.

Let the angles be 4x and 5x.

4x + 5x = 180°

9x = 180°

x = 20°

The angles are:

Smaller angle = 4x = 4 × 20° = 80°.

Larger angle = 5x = 5 × 20° = 100°.

Difference = Larger angle - Smaller angle = 100° - 80° = 20°.

Answer: b. 20°

4. A seller sold 1 kg potato at 25 tk and made a loss of 20%. At what price he needs so sell if he want to make a profit of 20%? [একজন বিক্রেতা ১ কেজি আলু ২৫ টাকায় বিক্রি করে ২০% লোকসান করেছেন। তিনি যদি ২০% লাভ করতে চান তাহলে তাকে কী দামে বিক্রি করতে হবে?]

  • 25
  • 30
  • 35
  • 37.5

Solution:

Selling Price (SP1) = 25 tk.

Loss = 20%.

Let Cost Price = CP.

SP1 = CP - 0.20 * CP = 0.80 * CP.

25 = 0.80 * CP

CP = 25 / 0.80 = 25 / (4/5) = 25 * (5/4) = 125 / 4 = 31.25 tk.

Now, the seller wants a profit of 20%.

New Selling Price (SP2) = CP + 0.20 * CP = 1.20 * CP.

SP2 = 1.20 * 31.25 = (6/5) * (125/4) = (6 * 125) / (5 * 4) = (3 * 125) / (5 * 2) = (3 * 25) / 2 = 75 / 2 = 37.5 tk.

Answer: d. 37.5

5. The cost price of 20 articles is the same as the selling price of x articles. If the profit is 25%, then the value of x is: [২০টি নিবন্ধের মূল্য x নিবন্ধের বিক্রয় মূল্যের সমান। যদি লাভ ২৫% হয়, তাহলে x এর মান হল-]

  • 25
  • 18
  • 16
  • 15

Solution:

Let CP be the cost price of one article.

Let SP be the selling price of one article.

Cost price of 20 articles = 20 * CP.

Selling price of x articles = x * SP.

Given: 20 * CP = x * SP.

Profit = 25%. This means SP = CP + 0.25 * CP = 1.25 * CP = (5/4) * CP.

Substitute SP in the first equation:

20 * CP = x * ( (5/4) * CP )

Divide both sides by CP (assuming CP is not zero):

20 = x * (5/4)

x = 20 * (4/5)

x = 4 * 4 = 16.

6. In an organization 40% of employees are matriculates, 50% of the remaining are graduates and the remaining 180 are post-graduates. What is the number of graduate employees? [একটি প্রতিষ্ঠানে ৪০% কর্মচারী ম্যাট্রিকুলেট, অবশিষ্টদের ৫০% স্নাতক এবং অবশিষ্ট ১৮০ জন স্নাতকোত্তর। স্নাতক কর্মচারীর সংখ্যা কত?]

  • 180
  • 240
  • 300
  • 360

Solution:

Let the total number of employees be T.

Matriculates = 40% of T = 0.40 T.

Remaining employees = T - 0.40 T = 0.60 T.

Graduates = 50% of the remaining = 0.50 * (0.60 T) = 0.30 T.

Post-graduates are the remaining after matriculates and graduates are accounted for.

Remaining after matriculates = 0.60 T.

Remaining after graduates = (Remaining after matriculates) - Graduates = 0.60 T - 0.30 T = 0.30 T.

These remaining are the post-graduates. So, Post-graduates = 0.30 T.

We are given that the number of post-graduates is 180.

0.30 T = 180.

T = 180 / 0.30 = 1800 / 3 = 600.

Total employees = 600.

The question asks for the number of *graduate* employees.

Number of Graduates = 0.30 T = 0.30 * 600 = 180.

Answer: a. 180

7. Two horses started simultaneously towards each other and meet each other 3 hr 20 mins later. How much time will it take the slower horse to cover the whole distance if the first arrived at the place of departure of the second 5 hours later than the second arrived at the point of departure of the first? [দুটি ঘোড়া একযোগে একে অপরের দিকে যাত্রা শুরু করে এবং ৩ ঘণ্টা ২০ মিনিট পরে মিলিত হয়। প্রথমটি দ্বিতীয়টির প্রস্থান স্থানে পৌঁছানোর ৫ ঘণ্টা পরে যদি দ্বিতীয়টি প্রথমটির প্রস্থান স্থানে পৌঁছায় তাহলে ধীরগতির ঘোড়াটির সম্পূর্ণ দূরত্ব অতিক্রম করতে কত সময় লাগবে?]

  • 10 hours
  • 5 hours
  • 15 hours
  • 6 hours

Solution:

Let the speeds of the two horses be \(v_1\) and \(v_2\).

Let the distance between the starting points be D.

They meet after time \(t_m = 3 \text{ hr } 20 \text{ min} = 3 + 20/60 = 3 + 1/3 = 10/3\) hours.

When moving towards each other, Distance = Relative Speed × Time.

\( D = (v_1 + v_2) \times t_m = (v_1 + v_2) \times \frac{10}{3} \). (Equation 1)

Let \(t_1\) be the time taken by the first horse to cover distance D, so \(t_1 = D/v_1\).

Let \(t_2\) be the time taken by the second horse to cover distance D, so \(t_2 = D/v_2\).

We are given that the first horse arrives 5 hours later than the second: \(t_1 = t_2 + 5\). (Equation 2)

There is a formula relating meeting time (\(t_m\)) and the times taken after meeting to reach the destinations (\(t_{a1}, t_{a2}\)): \(t_m = \sqrt{t_{a1} t_{a2}}\). This isn't directly what's given.

Another formula relates total travel times \(t_1, t_2\) and meeting time \(t_m\): \(t_m^2 = t_1 t_2\) is incorrect. The correct relation uses time after meeting.

Let's use the relation \(D/v_1 = t_1\) and \(D/v_2 = t_2\). From Eq 1: \(v_1 + v_2 = \frac{3D}{10}\).

Substitute \(v_1 = D/t_1\) and \(v_2 = D/t_2\):

\( \frac{D}{t_1} + \frac{D}{t_2} = \frac{3D}{10} \)

Divide by D: \( \frac{1}{t_1} + \frac{1}{t_2} = \frac{3}{10} \)

We also know \(t_1 = t_2 + 5\). Substitute this:

\( \frac{1}{t_2 + 5} + \frac{1}{t_2} = \frac{3}{10} \)

\( \frac{t_2 + (t_2 + 5)}{t_2(t_2 + 5)} = \frac{3}{10} \)

\( \frac{2t_2 + 5}{t_2^2 + 5t_2} = \frac{3}{10} \)

\( 10(2t_2 + 5) = 3(t_2^2 + 5t_2) \)

\( 20t_2 + 50 = 3t_2^2 + 15t_2 \)

\( 3t_2^2 - 5t_2 - 50 = 0 \)

Solve the quadratic for \(t_2\). Using the formula \(t_2 = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\):

\( t_2 = \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{(-5)^2 - 4(3)(-50)}}{2(3)} = \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{25 + 600}}{6} = \frac{5 \pm \sqrt{625}}{6} = \frac{5 \pm 25}{6} \)

Since time must be positive, \(t_2 = \frac{5 + 25}{6} = \frac{30}{6} = 5\) hours.

Then \(t_1 = t_2 + 5 = 5 + 5 = 10\) hours.

The times taken are 10 hours and 5 hours. The slower horse is the one that takes more time, which is \(t_1 = 10\) hours.

Answer: a. 10 hours

8. Two boats, traveling at 5 km/h and 10 km/h, head directly towards each other. They begin at a distance of 20 km from each other. How far apart are they (In km) one minute before they collide? [দুটি নৌকা, ৫ কিমি/ঘণ্টা এবং ১০ কিমি/ঘণ্টা বেগে সরাসরি একে অপরের দিকে যাচ্ছে। তারা একে অপর থেকে ২০ কিমি দূরত্বে যাত্রা শুরু করে। সংঘর্ষের এক মিনিট আগে তারা কত দূরে (কিমি) থাকবে?]

  • 1/12
  • 1/6
  • ¼
  • ½

Solution:

Speed of boat 1 = 5 km/h.

Speed of boat 2 = 10 km/h.

They are moving towards each other, so their relative speed is the sum of their speeds.

Relative Speed = 5 km/h + 10 km/h = 15 km/h.

In the last minute before collision, the distance covered by them relative to each other is the distance they cover in one minute at their relative speed.

Time = 1 minute = 1/60 hour.

Distance covered in the last minute = Relative Speed × Time

Distance = 15 km/h × (1/60) h = 15/60 km = 1/4 km.

This distance is the separation between them one minute before they collide.

Answer: c. ¼

9. Two forest officials in their respective division were involved in the harvesting of tendu leaves. One division had an average output of 21 tons from a hectares and other division, which had 12 hectares of land less, dedicated to tendu leaves, got 25 tons of tendu from a hectare. As a result, the second division harvested 300 tons of tendu leaves more than the first. How many tons of tendu leaves did the first division harvest? [দুটি ফরেস্ট অফিস তাদের নিজ নিজ বিভাগে তেঁতুল পাতা সংগ্রহে জড়িত ছিল। একটি বিভাগের হেক্টর প্রতি গড় উৎপাদন ছিল ২১ টন এবং অন্য বিভাগ, যার তেঁতুল পাতার জন্য নিবেদিত জমির পরিমাণ ১২ হেক্টর কম ছিল, হেক্টর প্রতি ২৫ টন তেঁতুল পাতা পায়। ফলে, দ্বিতীয় বিভাগ প্রথম বিভাগের চেয়ে ৩০০ টন বেশি তেঁতুল পাতা সংগ্রহ করে। প্রথম বিভাগ কত টন তেঁতুল পাতা সংগ্রহ করেছিল?]

  • 3150
  • 3450
  • 3500
  • 3600

Solution:

Let \(A_1\) be the area (in hectares) of the first division.

Let \(A_2\) be the area (in hectares) of the second division.

Average output (yield) of first division = 21 tons/hectare.

Average output (yield) of second division = 25 tons/hectare.

Total harvest of first division (\(H_1\)) = Yield1 × Area1 = 21 \(A_1\).

Total harvest of second division (\(H_2\)) = Yield2 × Area2 = 25 \(A_2\).

We are given that the second division had 12 hectares less land: \(A_2 = A_1 - 12\).

We are given that the second division harvested 300 tons more than the first: \(H_2 = H_1 + 300\).

Substitute the expressions for H1, H2, and A2 into the last equation:

\( 25 A_2 = 21 A_1 + 300 \)

\( 25 (A_1 - 12) = 21 A_1 + 300 \)

\( 25 A_1 - 300 = 21 A_1 + 300 \)

\( 25 A_1 - 21 A_1 = 300 + 300 \)

\( 4 A_1 = 600 \)

\( A_1 = 150 \) hectares.

The question asks for the total harvest of the first division (\(H_1\)).

\( H_1 = 21 A_1 = 21 \times 150 = 3150 \) tons.

Answer: a. 3150

10. In a mixture 60 liters, the ratio of milk and water 2 : 1. If this ratio is to be 1 : 2, then the quantity of water to be further added is: [৬০ লিটার মিশ্রণে, দুধ ও পানির অনুপাত ২:১। যদি এই অনুপাত ১:২ করতে হয়, তাহলে আরও কত পরিমাণ পানি যোগ করতে হবে?]

  • 20 liters
  • 30 liters
  • 40 liters
  • 60 liters

Solution:

Total mixture = 60 liters.

Initial ratio Milk : Water = 2 : 1.

Sum of ratio parts = 2 + 1 = 3.

Initial quantity of Milk = \( (2/3) \times 60 = 40 \) liters.

Initial quantity of Water = \( (1/3) \times 60 = 20 \) liters.

Let W liters of water be added.

New quantity of Milk = 40 liters.

New quantity of Water = 20 + W liters.

New ratio Milk : Water = 1 : 2.

\( \frac{\text{New Milk}}{\text{New Water}} = \frac{40}{20 + W} = \frac{1}{2} \)

Cross-multiply:

\( 40 \times 2 = 1 \times (20 + W) \)

\( 80 = 20 + W \)

14. If x and y are both positive solutions of equation ax + by + c = 0, always lie in the: [যদি x এবং y উভয়ই ax+by+c=0 সমীকরণের ধনাত্মক সমাধান হয়, তাহলে x ও y সর্বদা কোন চতুর্ভাগে থাকে?]

  • First quadrant
  • Second quadrant
  • Third quadrant
  • Fourth quadrant

Solution:

The Cartesian coordinate system is divided into four quadrants:

  • First Quadrant: x > 0, y > 0 (both positive)
  • Second Quadrant: x < 0, y > 0
  • Third Quadrant: x < 0, y < 0
  • Fourth Quadrant: x > 0, y < 0

If x and y are both positive solutions, meaning x > 0 and y > 0, then the point (x, y) lies in the First Quadrant.

15. In what ratio should water and wine be mixed so that after selling the mixture at the cost price a profit of 33.33% is made? [কোন অনুপাতে পানি এবং ওয়াইন মিশিয়ে ক্রয় মূল্যে বিক্রি করার পর ৩৩.৩৩% লাভ হয়?]

  • 2:5
  • 1:4
  • 1:6
  • 1:3

Solution:

Let the cost price (CP) of 1 unit of wine be C.

Assume the cost of water is negligible (0).

Let the mixture contain W units of water and N units of wine.

Total quantity of mixture = W + N.

Cost price of the mixture = Cost of water + Cost of wine = W(0) + N(C) = NC.

Selling Price (SP) per unit of mixture = Cost price per unit of wine = C.

Total Selling Price of the mixture = (W + N) × C.

Profit = Total SP - Total CP = (W + N)C - NC = WC.

Profit Percentage = \( \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{Total CP}} \times 100 \)

Given Profit Percentage = 33.33...% = 100/3 % = 1/3.

\( \frac{WC}{NC} = \frac{1}{3} \)

\( \frac{W}{N} = \frac{1}{3} \)

The ratio of Water : Wine should be 1 : 3.

Answer: d. 1:3

02. What is the value of \(\frac{d}{dx} (e^{\sqrt{x}})\)?

  • \( e^{\sqrt{x}} \)
  • \( 2\sqrt{x} e^{\sqrt{x}} \)
  • \( \frac{e^{\sqrt{x}}}{2\sqrt{x}} \)
  • \( \frac{\sqrt{2}e^{\sqrt{x}}}{2} \)

Solution:

Use the chain rule. Let \(y = e^{\sqrt{x}}\). Let \(u = \sqrt{x} = x^{1/2}\).

Then \(y = e^u\).

\( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \times \frac{du}{dx} \)

\( \frac{dy}{du} = \frac{d}{du}(e^u) = e^u \)

\( \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx}(x^{1/2}) = \frac{1}{2} x^{-1/2} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} \)

\( \frac{dy}{dx} = (e^u) \times \left(\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}\right) \)

Substitute back \(u = \sqrt{x}\):

\( \frac{dy}{dx} = e^{\sqrt{x}} \times \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} = \frac{e^{\sqrt{x}}}{2\sqrt{x}} \)

Answer: c. \( \frac{e^{\sqrt{x}}}{2\sqrt{x}} \)

03. What is the value of \(\lim_{x \to 0} \left(\frac{1 - \cos x}{x}\right)\)?

  • 2
  • 1
  • 0
  • All

Solution:

This limit is of the indeterminate form 0/0 (\(\cos 0 = 1\)).

Method 1: L'Hopital's Rule

\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\frac{d}{dx}(1 - \cos x)}{\frac{d}{dx}(x)} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{-(-\sin x)}{1} = \lim_{x \to 0} \sin x \] \[ = \sin 0 = 0 \]

Method 2: Multiply by conjugate / Use identity

Recall \(1 - \cos x = 2 \sin^2(x/2)\). \[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{2 \sin^2(x/2)}{x} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{2 \sin(x/2) \sin(x/2)}{x} \] \[ = \lim_{x \to 0} \left( \frac{\sin(x/2)}{x/2} \times \sin(x/2) \right) \] \[ = \left( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(x/2)}{x/2} \right) \times \left( \lim_{x \to 0} \sin(x/2) \right) \] \[ = (1) \times (\sin 0) = 1 \times 0 = 0 \]

Answer: c. 0

  • 80 kg m / s

  • 20 kg m / s

  • Work done by the system

In thermodynamics, the symbol S represents entropy, a measure of the disorder or randomness of a system. \(\Delta S\) represents the change in entropy.

The inequality \(\Delta S \ge Q/T\) (or more commonly written for a process involving heat transfer Q at temperature T, \(\Delta S \ge \int dQ/T\)) is a statement of the second law, indicating that the entropy of an isolated system never decreases over time (\(\Delta S \ge 0\)).
