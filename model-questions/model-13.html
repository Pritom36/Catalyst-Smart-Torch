1. A students scoring 25% marks in an exam failed by 30 marks and another student scoring 50% marks gained 30 more than pass marks. What is the pass mark? [একজন ছাত্র এবাং পরীক্ষায় ২৫% মার্কস পেয়ে ৩০ মার্কস এর জন্য ফেল করে। আরেকজন ছাত্র ৫০% মার্কস পেয়ে পাস মার্কস থেকে ৩০ মার্কস বেশি পায়। পাস মার্কস কত?]

  • 50
  • 60
  • 70
  • 90

Solution:

Let \(T\) be the total marks and \(P\) be the pass mark.

Student 1: \(0.25 T = P - 30\)

Student 2: \(0.50 T = P + 30\)

Subtract the first equation from the second:

\( (0.50 T - 0.25 T) = (P + 30) - (P - 30) \)

\( 0.25 T = 60 \)

\( T = \frac{60}{0.25} = 240 \)

Now find the pass mark \(P\) using the first equation:

\( 0.25 \times 240 = P - 30 \)

\( 60 = P - 30 \)

\( P = 60 + 30 = 90 \)

Answer: d. 90

2. When a man sold an article for Tk 540, he made a loss of 10%. At what price should he sell it, so that he incurs a loss of only 5%? [একজন লোক ৫৪০ টাকায় একটি আর্টিকেল বিক্রি করে ১০% লস করে। ৫% লস করতে হলে কত মূল্যে বিক্রয় করতে হবে?]

  • 520
  • 525
  • 550
  • 570

Solution:

Let CP be the cost price.

Selling Price (SP1) = Tk 540 with 10% loss.

\( SP1 = CP - 0.10 \times CP = 0.90 \times CP \)

\( 540 = 0.90 \times CP \)

\( CP = \frac{540}{0.90} = 600 \)

Now, find the selling price (SP2) for a 5% loss.

\( SP2 = CP - 0.05 \times CP = 0.95 \times CP \)

\( SP2 = 0.95 \times 600 = 570 \)

Answer: d. 570

3. Rahim sells chocolates at a profit of 20% for Tk 60. What will be the percentage loss or gain if he reduces the price to Tk 55 due to less demand. [রহিম ২০% লাভে একটি চকলেট ৬০ টাকায় বিক্রি করে। সে যদি চাহিদা কম থাকায় ৫৫ টাকায় বিক্রি করত তাহলে কি পরিমাণ লাভ/ক্ষতির সম্মুখীন হত?]

  • +11%
  • -11%
  • +10%
  • -10%

Solution:

Let CP be the cost price.

Selling Price (SP1) = Tk 60 with 20% profit.

\( SP1 = CP + 0.20 \times CP = 1.20 \times CP \)

\( 60 = 1.20 \times CP \)

\( CP = \frac{60}{1.20} = 50 \)

New Selling Price (SP2) = Tk 55.

Profit/Loss = SP2 - CP = 55 - 50 = +5

Percentage Profit/Loss = \(\frac{\text{Profit/Loss}}{CP} \times 100\)

\( \text{Percentage Profit} = \frac{5}{50} \times 100 = \frac{1}{10} \times 100 = 10\% \)

It's a gain of 10%.

Answer: c. +10%

4. Two trains 105 meters and 90 meters long, run at the speeds of 45 kmph and 72 kmph respectively, in opposite directions on parallel tracks. The time which they take to cross each other, is: [দুইটি ট্রেন ১০৫ মিটার ও ৯০ মিটার দৈর্ঘ্যের, একে অপরের বিপরীত দিকে সমান্তরাল ট্র্যাকে চলে যথাক্রমে ৪৫ কিমি/ঘণ্টা ও ৭২ কিমি/ঘণ্টা বেগে। একে অপরকে ক্রস করতে তাদের যে সময়ের প্রয়োজন-]

  • 8 seconds
  • 6 seconds
  • 7 seconds
  • 5 seconds

Solution:

Total distance to cover = Sum of lengths of the trains = 105 m + 90 m = 195 m.

Relative speed = Sum of speeds (since moving in opposite directions).

Speed 1 = 45 kmph = \(45 \times \frac{1000}{3600} = 45 \times \frac{5}{18} = 12.5\) m/s.

Speed 2 = 72 kmph = \(72 \times \frac{5}{18} = 4 \times 5 = 20\) m/s.

Relative speed = 12.5 m/s + 20 m/s = 32.5 m/s.

Time = \(\frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Relative Speed}}\)

Time = \(\frac{195}{32.5} = \frac{1950}{325} = 6\) seconds.

Answer: b. 6 seconds

5. A train passes two persons walking in the same direction at a speed of 3 kmph and 5 kmph respectively in 10 seconds and 11 seconds respectively. The speed of the train is: [একটি ট্রেন একই দিকে হাঁটা দুজন লোককে যথাক্রমে ৩ কিমি/ঘন্টা ও ৫ কিমি/ঘন্টা বেগে চলা দুজন লোককে ১০ সেকেন্ড ও ১১ সেকেন্ডে পার করে যায়। ট্রেনটির গতিবেগ-]

  • 28 kmph
  • 27 kmph
  • 25 kmph
  • 24 kmph

Solution:

Let the length of the train be L meters and its speed be S kmph.

Convert speeds to m/s: Person 1 speed = 3 kmph = \(3 \times \frac{5}{18} = \frac{5}{6}\) m/s. Person 2 speed = 5 kmph = \(5 \times \frac{5}{18} = \frac{25}{18}\) m/s. Train speed = S kmph = \(S \times \frac{5}{18}\) m/s.

Relative speed when passing Person 1 (same direction) = \((S \times \frac{5}{18}) - \frac{5}{6}\) m/s.

Relative speed when passing Person 2 (same direction) = \((S \times \frac{5}{18}) - \frac{25}{18}\) m/s.

Distance covered in both cases is the length of the train, L.

Case 1: \(L = \text{Relative Speed}_1 \times \text{Time}_1 = \left(S \frac{5}{18} - \frac{5}{6}\right) \times 10\)

Case 2: \(L = \text{Relative Speed}_2 \times \text{Time}_2 = \left(S \frac{5}{18} - \frac{25}{18}\right) \times 11\)

Equate the expressions for L:

\( \left(\frac{5S}{18} - \frac{15}{18}\right) \times 10 = \left(\frac{5S}{18} - \frac{25}{18}\right) \times 11 \)

\( \frac{5(S-3)}{18} \times 10 = \frac{5(S-5)}{18} \times 11 \)

\( 10(S-3) = 11(S-5) \)

\( 10S - 30 = 11S - 55 \)

\( S = 55 - 30 = 25 \)

The speed of the train is 25 kmph.

Answer: c. 25 kmph

6. If one of the zeroes of cubic polynomial is x3 + ax2 + bx + c is -1, then product of other two zeroes is: [যদি ত্রিঘাত সমীকরণের একটি মূল -১ হয়, বাকি দুইটি মূলের গুণফল কত?] x3 + ax2 + bx + c

  • b-a-1
  • b-a+1
  • a-b+1
  • a-b-1

Solution:

Let the roots (zeroes) of the cubic polynomial \(P(x) = x^3 + ax^2 + bx + c\) be \(\alpha, \beta, \gamma\).

From Vieta's formulas:

Sum of roots: \(\alpha + \beta + \gamma = -a\)

Sum of roots taken two at a time: \(\alpha\beta + \beta\gamma + \gamma\alpha = b\)

Product of roots: \(\alpha\beta\gamma = -c\)

We are given that one root is -1. Let \(\alpha = -1\).

We need to find the product of the other two roots, which is \(\beta\gamma\).

Substitute \(\alpha = -1\) into the second Vieta's formula:

\( (-1)\beta + \beta\gamma + \gamma(-1) = b \)

\( -\beta - \gamma + \beta\gamma = b \)

\( \beta\gamma - (\beta + \gamma) = b \)

We need to find \((\beta + \gamma)\). Substitute \(\alpha = -1\) into the first Vieta's formula:

\( -1 + \beta + \gamma = -a \)

\( \beta + \gamma = -a + 1 = 1 - a \)

Now substitute this back into the modified second formula:

\( \beta\gamma - (1 - a) = b \)

\( \beta\gamma = b + (1 - a) = b - a + 1 \)

Answer: b. b-a+1

7. There is provision of food in fort for 1200 soldiers for 60 days. After 15 days, 200 soldiers leave the fort. Remaining food will last for how many days? [একটি দুর্গে ১২০০ সৈন্যের ৬০ দিনের খাবার সংস্থিত আছে। ১৫ দিন পর ২০০ সৈন্য দুর্গটি ছেড়ে যায়। বাকি খাবারে আর কতদিন চলবে?]

  • 56 days
  • 50 days
  • 54 days
  • 48 days

Solution:

Total food units = Number of soldiers × Number of days = 1200 × 60 = 72000 units.

Food consumed in the first 15 days = 1200 soldiers × 15 days = 18000 units.

Remaining food = 72000 - 18000 = 54000 units.

Number of soldiers remaining = 1200 - 200 = 1000 soldiers.

Let the remaining food last for \(D\) days.

Remaining food = Remaining soldiers × D

\( 54000 = 1000 \times D \)

\( D = \frac{54000}{1000} = 54 \) days.

Answer: c. 54 days

8. A and B working together completed a job in 5 days. If A works twice as efficiently as he actually did and B works — of actual efficiency, the work would have completed in 3 days. Find the for A to complete the job alone? [A ও B একত্রে একটি কাজ ৫ দিনে শেষ করে। যদি A কাজটি দ্বিগুণ কর্মদক্ষতায় এবং B প্রকৃত কর্মদক্ষতার — কাজ করত, তাহলে মোট ৩ দিনে শেষ হত। A একা কাজটি কত দিনে শেষ করবে?] (Note: Fraction for B's efficiency is missing, assume 1/3 based on similar problems)

  • 6 ¼
  • 6 ¾
  • 6 ⅓
  • 12 ½

Solution:

Let A's normal rate be \(a\) jobs/day and B's normal rate be \(b\) jobs/day.

Working together, their combined rate is \(a+b\). Time = 5 days. Work = 1 job.

Rate × Time = Work \(\implies (a+b) \times 5 = 1 \implies a+b = \frac{1}{5}\) (Equation 1)

Assume B works at 1/3 of actual efficiency.

New rates: A's rate = \(2a\), B's rate = \(\frac{1}{3}b\).

New combined rate = \(2a + \frac{1}{3}b\). Time = 3 days. Work = 1 job.

\( (2a + \frac{1}{3}b) \times 3 = 1 \implies 6a + b = 1 \) (Equation 2)

Now solve the system of equations:

Eq 2: \(b = 1 - 6a\)

Substitute into Eq 1: \(a + (1 - 6a) = \frac{1}{5}\)

\( 1 - 5a = \frac{1}{5} \)

\( 5a = 1 - \frac{1}{5} = \frac{4}{5} \)

\( a = \frac{4}{25} \) jobs/day

Time for A alone = \(\frac{\text{Work}}{\text{Rate of A}} = \frac{1}{a} = \frac{1}{4/25} = \frac{25}{4}\) days.

\( \frac{25}{4} = 6 \frac{1}{4} \) days.

Answer: a. 6 ¼

9. The number of oranges in three basket are in the ratio 3 : 4 : 5. In which ratio the no. of oranges in first two basket must be increased so that the new ratio become 5 : 4 : 3? [তিনটি ঝুড়িতে কমলার অনুপাত ৩:৪:৫। প্রথম দুইটি ঝুড়িতে কমলার পরিমাণ কোন অনুপাতে বৃদ্ধি করতে হবে তিনটি ঝুড়িতে কমলার অনুপাত দাঁড়ায়ে ৫:৪:৩?]

  • 1:2
  • 1:3
  • 7:2
  • 2:1

Solution:

Let the initial number of oranges be \(3x, 4x, 5x\).

Let the number of oranges added to the first two baskets be \(n_1\) and \(n_2\).

New numbers: \(3x + n_1, 4x + n_2, 5x\).

The new ratio is 5 : 4 : 3.

This implies the number in the third basket must have decreased or the overall proportion changed. Let's assume the numbers become proportional to 5:4:3. Let the new counts be \(5y, 4y, 3y\).

Comparing the third basket: Initial count was proportional to 5, final count is proportional to 3. This suggests a rescaling. Let the initial counts be \(3k, 4k, 5k\). Let the final counts be \(5m, 4m, 3m\).

If the third basket quantity remained constant, \(5k = 3m \implies m = \frac{5}{3}k\).

Then the final counts should be \(5(\frac{5}{3}k), 4(\frac{5}{3}k), 3(\frac{5}{3}k)\) which are \(\frac{25}{3}k, \frac{20}{3}k, 5k\).

Amount added to basket 1: \(n_1 = \frac{25}{3}k - 3k = \frac{25-9}{3}k = \frac{16}{3}k\).

Amount added to basket 2: \(n_2 = \frac{20}{3}k - 4k = \frac{20-12}{3}k = \frac{8}{3}k\).

The ratio in which the number of oranges must be increased is \(n_1 : n_2\).

\( \frac{n_1}{n_2} = \frac{\frac{16}{3}k}{\frac{8}{3}k} = \frac{16}{8} = \frac{2}{1} \).

The ratio is 2 : 1.

Answer: d. 2:1

10. At a casino in Mumbai, there are 3 tables A, B and C. The payoffs at A is 10:1, at B is 20:1 and C is 30:1. If a man bets Tk. 200 at each table and win at two of the tables, what is the maximum and minimum difference between his earnings can be? [মুম্বাইয়ের একটি ক্যাসিনোতে, তিনটি টেবিল আছে- A, B, C. A তে পেঅফ ১০:১, B তে ২০:১ এবং C তে ৩০:১. একজন লোক যদি প্রতিটি টেবিলে ২০০ টাকা করে বাজি ধরে এবং ২টিতে জিততে পারলে, তার সর্বোচ্চ ও সর্বনিম্ন উপার্জনের পার্থক্য কত?]

  • Tk. 4000
  • Tk. 4500
  • Tk. 2500
  • Tk. 2000

Solution:

Bet amount per table = Tk 200.

Payoff means (Profit : Bet Amount). A win returns (Bet + Profit).

Profit from winning at A = \(10 \times 200 = 2000\).

Profit from winning at B = \(20 \times 200 = 4000\).

Profit from winning at C = \(30 \times 200 = 6000\).

Total amount bet = 3 tables \(\times\) Tk 200/table = Tk 600.

The man wins at two tables and loses at one.

Possible winning pairs and total profit (Profit = Sum of profits from winning tables - Bet on losing table):

1. Win A & B, Lose C: Profit = (2000 + 4000) - 200 = 5800.

2. Win A & C, Lose B: Profit = (2000 + 6000) - 200 = 7800.

3. Win B & C, Lose A: Profit = (4000 + 6000) - 200 = 9800.

Maximum profit = 9800.

Minimum profit = 5800.

Difference = Maximum Profit - Minimum Profit = 9800 - 5800 = 4000.

Answer: a. Tk. 4000

11. The value of (sin 45° + cos 45°) is

  • 1/√2
  • √2
  • √3/2
  • 1

Solution:

\( \sin 45^\circ = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \)

\( \cos 45^\circ = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \)

15. The diameter of a circle is increased by 100%. What is the percentage increase in area? [একটি বৃত্তের ব্যাস ১০০% বৃদ্ধি করা হলে ক্ষেত্রফল কত বৃদ্ধি পাবে?]

  • 250%
  • 200%
  • 400%
  • 300%

Solution:

Let the original diameter be \(D\). Original radius \(r = D/2\).

Original Area \(A_1 = \pi r^2 = \pi (D/2)^2 = \frac{\pi D^2}{4}\).

The diameter is increased by 100%. New diameter \(D_{new} = D + 100\% \times D = D + D = 2D\).

03. What is the value of \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan ax}{\sin bx}\)? [\(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan ax}{\sin bx}\) এর মান কোনটি?]

  • b/a
  • a
  • b
  • a/b

Solution:

We use the standard limits \(\lim_{\theta \to 0} \frac{\tan \theta}{\theta} = 1\) and \(\lim_{\theta \to 0} \frac{\sin \theta}{\theta} = 1\).

\[ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan ax}{\sin bx} = \lim_{x \to 0} \left( \frac{\tan ax}{ax} \cdot \frac{bx}{\sin bx} \cdot \frac{ax}{bx} \right) \] \[ = \left( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\tan ax}{ax} \right) \cdot \left( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{bx}{\sin bx} \right) \cdot \left( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{ax}{bx} \right) \]

04. Which one is the length of intercept from y-axis cut off by the circle \(x^2 + y^2 + 2x + 4y - 1 = 0\)? [\(x^2 + y^2 + 2x + 4y - 1 = 0\) বৃত্ত দ্বারা y-অক্ষের ছেদাংশের মান কত?]

  • \(2\sqrt{2}\)
  • \(\sqrt{5}\)
  • \(2\sqrt{5}\)
  • \(\sqrt{2}\)

Solution:

To find the y-intercepts, set \(x=0\) in the circle's equation:

\( (0)^2 + y^2 + 2(0) + 4y - 1 = 0 \)

\( y^2 + 4y - 1 = 0 \)

Let the y-coordinates of the intersection points be \(y_1\) and \(y_2\). These are the roots of the quadratic equation.

The length of the y-intercept is the distance between these two points, which is \(|y_1 - y_2|\).

We know for a quadratic \(ay^2+by+c=0\), the roots \(y_1, y_2\) satisfy \((y_1 - y_2)^2 = (y_1 + y_2)^2 - 4y_1 y_2\). Also, \(y_1 + y_2 = -b/a\) and \(y_1 y_2 = c/a\).

In our equation \(y^2 + 4y - 1 = 0\), \(a=1, b=4, c=-1\).

\( y_1 + y_2 = -4/1 = -4 \)

\( y_1 y_2 = -1/1 = -1 \)

\( (y_1 - y_2)^2 = (-4)^2 - 4(-1) = 16 + 4 = 20 \)

Length \( = |y_1 - y_2| = \sqrt{20} = \sqrt{4 \times 5} = 2\sqrt{5} \)

Alternatively, the length of the y-intercept for \(x^2+y^2+2gx+2fy+c=0\) is \(2\sqrt{f^2-c}\). Here, \(2f=4 \implies f=2\) and \(c=-1\). Length = \(2\sqrt{2^2 - (-1)} = 2\sqrt{4+1} = 2\sqrt{5}\).

Answer: c. \(2\sqrt{5}\)

06. Heat absorbed, \(dQ\) or \(\Delta Q = 700\) J and work done, \(dW\) or \(\Delta W = 200\) J, what will be the rise in internal energy? [যদি গৃহীত তাপ 700 J এবং কৃত কাজ 200 J তাহলে কি পরিমাণ অভ্যন্তরীণ শক্তি পাবে?]

  • 900 J
  • 700 J
  • 600 J
  • 500 J

Solution:

According to the First Law of Thermodynamics:

\( \Delta Q = \Delta U + \Delta W \)

Where:

  • \(\Delta Q\) is the heat added to the system (positive if absorbed, negative if released).
  • \(\Delta U\) is the change in internal energy (positive if increased, negative if decreased).
  • \(\Delta W\) is the work done by the system (positive if done by the system, negative if done on the system).

The rise in internal energy is 500 J.

  • \(e = -\frac{d\Phi}{dt}\)
  • \(et = -\frac{d\Phi}{dt}\)
  • \(e = \frac{d\Phi}{dt}\)

Solution:

Faraday's Law of Induction states that the induced electromotive force (emf, \(e\)) in any closed circuit is equal to the negative of the time rate of change of the magnetic flux (\(\Phi\)) enclosed by the circuit: \(e = -\frac{d\Phi}{dt}\).

Lenz's Law determines the direction of the induced current (and hence the sign of the induced emf). It states that the induced current flows in a direction that opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it. The negative sign in Faraday's Law is a mathematical representation of Lenz's Law.

Answer: b. \(e = -\frac{d\Phi}{dt}\)

08. A disk has a radius of 0.5 m and rotates at 200 rpm. What is the linear velocity of a point on the edge of the disk? [একটি ডিস্কের ব্যাসার্ধ 0.5 মিটার এবং এটি 200 rpm এ ঘোরে। ডিস্কের কিনারায় একটি বিন্দুর রৈখিক বেগ কত?]

  • 5.24 m/s
  • 2.8 m/s
  • 10.5 m/s
  • 104.8 m/s

Solution:

Radius \(r = 0.5\) m.

Angular velocity \(\omega\) in revolutions per minute (rpm) = 200 rpm.

Convert \(\omega\) to radians per second (rad/s):

\( \omega = 200 \frac{\text{rev}}{\text{min}} \times \frac{2\pi \text{ rad}}{1 \text{ rev}} \times \frac{1 \text{ min}}{60 \text{ s}} \)

\( v = (0.5 \text{ m}) \times \left(\frac{20\pi}{3} \frac{\text{rad}}{\text{s}}\right) = \frac{10\pi}{3} \) m/s.

Using \(\pi \approx 3.14159\):

\( v \approx \frac{10 \times 3.14159}{3} \approx \frac{31.4159}{3} \approx 10.47 \) m/s.

This is closest to 10.5 m/s.

  • Gravitational potential energy
  • Electrical energy
