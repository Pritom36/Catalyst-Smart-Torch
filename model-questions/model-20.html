Section C – [General Math]

1. If the curved surface area of a sphere is equal to the curved surface area of a hemisphere, what is the radius of the hemisphere? [একটি গোলকের বক্র পৃষ্ঠের ক্ষেত্রফল একটি অর্ধগোলকের বক্র পৃষ্ঠের ক্ষেত্রফলের সমান হলে, গোলকের ব্যাসার্ধ কত?] (Question asks for radius of hemisphere, likely means ratio to sphere's radius)

  • Same of the sphere
  • √2times of sphere
  • √3 times of sphere
  • 2 times of sphere

Solution:

Let the radius of the sphere be \(R_s\).

Curved Surface Area (CSA) of sphere = \(4 \pi R_s^2\).

Let the radius of the hemisphere be \(R_h\).

Curved Surface Area (CSA) of hemisphere = \(2 \pi R_h^2\). (Note: Total surface area includes the base, \(2\pi R_h^2 + \pi R_h^2 = 3\pi R_h^2\), but the question specifies curved surface area).

Given: CSA of sphere = CSA of hemisphere

\( 4 \pi R_s^2 = 2 \pi R_h^2 \)

Divide by \(2\pi\):

\( 2 R_s^2 = R_h^2 \)

Take the square root of both sides:

\( R_h = \sqrt{2 R_s^2} = \sqrt{2} R_s \).

The radius of the hemisphere is \(\sqrt{2}\) times the radius of the sphere.

Answer: b. √2times of sphere

2. The radii of two cylinders are in the ratio of 2:3, and their heights are in the ratio of 5:3. What is the ratio of their volumes? [দুটি সিলিন্ডারের ব্যাসার্ধ 2:3 অনুপাতে এবং তাদের উচ্চতা 5:3 অনুপাতে। তাদের আয়তনের অনুপাত কত?]

  • 4 : 9
  • 9 : 4
  • 20 : 27
  • 20 : 25

Solution:

Let the radii be \(r_1, r_2\) and heights be \(h_1, h_2\).

Given: \( \frac{r_1}{r_2} = \frac{2}{3} \) and \( \frac{h_1}{h_2} = \frac{5}{3} \).

The volume of a cylinder is \( V = \pi r^2 h \).

Volume of first cylinder \(V_1 = \pi r_1^2 h_1\).

Volume of second cylinder \(V_2 = \pi r_2^2 h_2\).

Ratio of volumes:

\( \frac{V_1}{V_2} = \frac{\pi r_1^2 h_1}{\pi r_2^2 h_2} = \frac{r_1^2}{r_2^2} \times \frac{h_1}{h_2} = \left( \frac{r_1}{r_2} \right)^2 \times \left( \frac{h_1}{h_2} \right) \)

Substitute the given ratios:

\( \frac{V_1}{V_2} = \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^2 \times \left( \frac{5}{3} \right) = \frac{4}{9} \times \frac{5}{3} = \frac{20}{27} \).

The ratio of volumes is 20 : 27.

Answer: c. 20 : 27

3. Rajiv sold an article for Rs.56 which cost him Rs.x. If he had gained x% on his outlay, what was his cost? [রাজীব 56 টাকায় একটি আর্টিকেল বিক্রি করে যা কিনতে x টাকা লাগে। যদি সে তার ব্যয়ের x% লাভ করে থাকে, তাহলে তার খরচ কত ছিল?]

  • 25
  • 40
  • 30
  • 40

Solution:

Selling Price (SP) = 56.

Cost Price (CP) = x.

Gain Percentage = x%.

Gain Amount = x% of CP = \( \frac{x}{100} \times x = \frac{x^2}{100} \).

We know SP = CP + Gain Amount.

\( 56 = x + \frac{x^2}{100} \)

Multiply by 100 to clear the fraction:

\( 5600 = 100x + x^2 \)

\( x^2 + 100x - 5600 = 0 \)

We need to solve this quadratic equation for x.

Factorize 5600. We need two numbers that multiply to -5600 and add to 100.

Consider factors of 5600: 140 and 40. \(140 \times 40 = 5600\). We need a difference of 100. Use +140 and -40. \(140 - 40 = 100\).

\( x^2 + 140x - 40x - 5600 = 0 \)

\( x(x + 140) - 40(x + 140) = 0 \)

\( (x - 40)(x + 140) = 0 \)

The possible solutions are x = 40 or x = -140.

Since cost price (x) must be positive, x = 40.

His cost was Rs 40.

Answer: b. 40 (or d. 40)

4. If a merchant makes a profit of 20% after giving a 20% discount, what should be his mark-up? [যদি একজন ব্যবসায়ী 20% ডিসকাউন্ট দেওয়ার পর 20% লাভ করেন, তাহলে তার মার্ক-আপ কেমন হওয়া উচিত?]

  • 25
  • 40
  • 30
  • 50

Solution:

Let Cost Price (CP) = 100.

Profit = 20%. Selling Price (SP) = CP + 20% of CP = 100 + 20 = 120.

The SP (120) was obtained after giving a 20% discount on the Marked Price (MP).

SP = MP - 20% of MP = MP - 0.20 MP = 0.80 MP.

120 = 0.80 MP

MP = 120 / 0.80 = 1200 / 8 = 150.

Mark-up is the amount added to the CP to get the MP.

Mark-up Amount = MP - CP = 150 - 100 = 50.

Mark-up Percentage = \( \frac{\text{Mark-up Amount}}{CP} \times 100 \)

Mark-up Percentage = \( \frac{50}{100} \times 100 = 50\% \)

Answer: d. 50

5. In a class 20% students get A, 35% students get B, 20% get C and 15% students get D and 20 failed in an exam. How many students get A in that exam? [একটি শ্রেণীতে 20% শিক্ষার্থী A পায়, 35% শিক্ষার্থী B পায়, 20% C পায় এবং 15% শিক্ষার্থী D পায় এবং 20 জন পরীক্ষায় ফেল করে। সেই পরীক্ষায় কতজন শিক্ষার্থী A পায়?]

  • 40
  • 60
  • 70
  • 80

Solution:

Let the total number of students be T.

Percentage of students who passed (got A, B, C, or D) = 20% + 35% + 20% + 15% = 90%.

Percentage of students who failed = 100% - 90% = 10%.

We are given that the number of students who failed is 20.

10% of T = 20

0.10 T = 20

T = 20 / 0.10 = 200.

Total number of students = 200.

Number of students who got A = 20% of T = 0.20 × 200 = 40.

Answer: a. 40

6. In a 100m race, Srabon defeats Subha by 5 seconds. If the speed of Srabon is 18 kmph, then the speed of Subha is: [100 মিটার দৌড়ে, শ্রাবণ সুভাষকে 5 সেকেন্ডে পরাজিত করে। যদি শ্রাবণের গতি 18 কিমি প্রতি ঘণ্টা হয়, তাহলে সুভাষের গতি কত?]

  • 15.4 kmph
  • 14.5 kmph
  • 14.4 kmph
  • 14 kmph

Solution:

Distance = 100 m = 0.1 km.

Speed of Srabon (\(S_S\)) = 18 kmph.

Time taken by Srabon (\(T_S\)) = Distance / Speed = 0.1 km / 18 kmph = 1 / 180 hours.

Convert \(T_S\) to seconds: \( (1/180) \text{ hours} \times 3600 \text{ s/hour} = 3600 / 180 = 20 \) seconds.

Srabon defeats Subha by 5 seconds. This means Subha takes 5 seconds longer than Srabon to finish the race.

Time taken by Subha (\(T_B\)) = \(T_S + 5 = 20 + 5 = 25\) seconds.

Speed of Subha (\(S_B\)) = Distance / Time = 100 m / 25 s = 4 m/s.

Convert \(S_B\) to kmph:

\( 4 \text{ m/s} \times \frac{3600 \text{ s}}{1 \text{ hr}} \times \frac{1 \text{ km}}{1000 \text{ m}} = 4 \times 3.6 \) kmph = 14.4 kmph.

Answer: c. 14.4 kmph

7. In a race of 200 meters B, can give a start of 10 meters to A, and C can give a start of 20 meters to B. The start that C can give to A, in the same race is: [200 মিটার দৌড়ে B, A কে 10 মিটারের স্টার্ট দিতে পারে এবং C, B কে 20 মিটারের স্টার্ট দিতে পারে। একই দৌড়ে C, A কে কত স্টার্ট দিতে পারবে?]

  • 30 meters
  • 25 meters
  • 29 meters
  • 27 meters

Solution:

"B can give a start of 10 meters to A" means: In the time B runs 200 m, A runs 200 - 10 = 190 m.

Ratio of speeds \( \frac{S_B}{S_A} = \frac{200}{190} = \frac{20}{19} \).

"C can give a start of 20 meters to B" means: In the time C runs 200 m, B runs 200 - 20 = 180 m.

Ratio of speeds \( \frac{S_C}{S_B} = \frac{200}{180} = \frac{10}{9} \).

We want to find the start C can give to A. We need the ratio \(S_C / S_A\).

\( \frac{S_C}{S_A} = \frac{S_C}{S_B} \times \frac{S_B}{S_A} = \frac{10}{9} \times \frac{20}{19} = \frac{200}{171} \).

This means in the time C runs 200 m, A runs 171 m.

The start C can give to A = 200 m - 171 m = 29 meters.

Answer: c. 29 meters

8. The number of polynomials having zeroes as -2 and 5 Is: [-2 এবং 5 শূন্য বিশিষ্ট বহুপদীর সংখ্যা হল:]

  • 1
  • 2
  • 3
  • More than 3

Solution:

If a polynomial has zeroes -2 and 5, then it must have factors (x - (-2)) = (x + 2) and (x - 5).

The simplest polynomial with these zeroes is \( P(x) = (x+2)(x-5) = x^2 - 3x - 10 \).

However, any constant multiple of this polynomial will also have the same zeroes.

For any non-zero constant 'k', the polynomial \( k \times P(x) = k(x^2 - 3x - 10) \) also has zeroes at x = -2 and x = 5.

Since there are infinitely many possible values for k, there are infinitely many polynomials having -2 and 5 as zeroes.

Therefore, the number is more than 3.

Answer: d. More than 3

9. If one pipe A can fill a tank in 20 minutes, then 5 pipes, each of 20% efficiency of A, can fill the tank in: [যদি একটি পাইপ A একটি ট্যাঙ্ক 20 মিনিটে পূর্ণ করতে পারে, তাহলে A এর 20% কর্মদক্ষতা সম্পন্ন 5টি পাইপ ট্যাঙ্কটি কত সময়ে পূর্ণ করতে পারবে?]

  • 80 min
  • 100 min
  • 20 min
  • 25 min

Solution:

Let the rate of pipe A be \(R_A\).

Time taken by A = 20 minutes. Volume of tank V = 1.

Rate \(R_A = \text{Work / Time} = 1 / 20\) tank/minute.

Efficiency of each new pipe = 20% of A's efficiency = 0.20 * \(R_A\).

Rate of one new pipe (\(R_{new}\)) = 0.20 * (1/20) = (1/5) * (1/20) = 1/100 tank/minute.

There are 5 such pipes working together.

Combined rate of 5 new pipes = 5 * \(R_{new}\) = 5 * (1/100) = 5/100 = 1/20 tank/minute.

Time taken by 5 new pipes = Work / Combined rate

Time = 1 / (1/20) = 20 minutes.

Answer: c. 20 min

10. Tom is chasing Jerry. In the same interval of time Tom jumps 8 times while Jerry jumps 6 times. But the distance covered by Tom in 7 jumps is equal to the distance covered by Jerry in 5 jumps. The ratio of Speed of Tom and Jerry is: [টম জেরি কে তাড়া করছে। একই সময়ে টম ৮ বার লাফ দেয় যখন জেরি ৬ বার লাফ দেয়। কিন্তু টম ৭ লাফে যে দূরত্ব অতিক্রম করে তা জেরি ৫ লাফে অতিক্রম করা দূরত্বের সমান। টম এবং জেরির গতির অনুপাত হল:]

  • 48 : 35
  • 28 : 15
  • 24 : 20
  • 20 : 21

Solution:

Let \(d_T\) be the distance Tom covers in one jump.

Let \(d_J\) be the distance Jerry covers in one jump.

Given: Distance covered by Tom in 7 jumps = Distance covered by Jerry in 5 jumps.

\( 7 d_T = 5 d_J \implies \frac{d_T}{d_J} = \frac{5}{7} \). Let \(d_T = 5k\) and \(d_J = 7k\).

Let the interval of time be T.

In time T, Tom jumps 8 times. Distance covered by Tom = \( 8 \times d_T = 8 \times 5k = 40k \).

In time T, Jerry jumps 6 times. Distance covered by Jerry = \( 6 \times d_J = 6 \times 7k = 42k \).

Speed of Tom (\(S_T\)) = Distance / Time = 40k / T.

Speed of Jerry (\(S_J\)) = Distance / Time = 42k / T.

Ratio of speeds (Tom : Jerry) = \( S_T : S_J = \frac{40k}{T} : \frac{42k}{T} \).

Ratio = 40k : 42k = 40 : 42.

Simplify by dividing by 2: Ratio = 20 : 21.

Answer: d. 20 : 21

11. The ratio of ducks and frogs in a pond is 37 : 39 respectively. The average number of ducks and frogs in the pond is 152. What is the number of frogs in the pond? [একটি পুকুরে হাঁস ও ব্যাঙের অনুপাত যথাক্রমে ৩৭:৩৯। পুকুরে হাঁস ও ব্যাঙের গড় সংখ্যা ১৫২। পুকুরে ব্যাঙের সংখ্যা কত?]

  • 148
  • 152
  • 156
  • 144

Solution:

Let the number of ducks be 37x and the number of frogs be 39x.

Total number of ducks and frogs = 37x + 39x = 76x.

Average number = Total number / 2.

Given Average number = 152.

\( \frac{76x}{2} = 152 \)

\( 38x = 152 \)

\( x = \frac{152}{38} = 4 \).

Number of frogs = 39x = 39 × 4 = 156.

12. In given figure, the value of ∠C is [প্রদত্ত চিত্রে, ∠C এর মান]

  • 90°
  • 45°
  • 30°
  • 60°

Solution:

The figure shows a right-angled triangle ABC, with the right angle at B.

Sides given: AB = 8 cm, AC = 16 cm.

We need to find angle C.

In right triangle ABC, relative to angle C:

AB is the Opposite side.

AC is the Hypotenuse.

The trigonometric ratio relating Opposite and Hypotenuse is sine.

Section D – [Higher Math & Physics]

01. Evaluate \(\int x^n \, dx\)

  • \( x^n + c \)
  • \( \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + c \)
  • \( \frac{n}{n+1} x^n + c \)
  • \( (\sqrt{x})^3 + c \)

This is the standard power rule for integration.

\[ \int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + c \]

02. What is the value of \(\frac{d}{dx} \ln(\sqrt{x})\)?

  • \( 1/\sqrt{x} \)
  • \( 1/2x \)
  • \( 1/2\sqrt{-(lnx)} \)
  • \( 2\sqrt{x} \)

Solution:

First, simplify the expression using logarithm properties:

\( \ln(\sqrt{x}) = \ln(x^{1/2}) = \frac{1}{2} \ln x \)

Now differentiate with respect to x:

\[ \frac{d}{dx} \left( \frac{1}{2} \ln x \right) = \frac{1}{2} \frac{d}{dx}(\ln x) = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{x} = \frac{1}{2x} \]

Alternatively, using the chain rule: Let \(y=\ln(\sqrt{x})\), \(u=\sqrt{x}\). Then \(y=\ln u\).

\( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du}\frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{u} \times \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}} \times \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} = \frac{1}{2x} \).

Answer: b. \( 1/2x \)

03. What is the value of \(\lim_{x \to 0} (1 + 3x)^{5/x}\)?

  • \( e \)
  • \( e^{15} \)
  • \( e^0 \)
  • \( 1 \)

Solution:

This limit is of the indeterminate form \(1^\infty\) as \(x \to 0\).

We use the standard limit form \( \lim_{z \to 0} (1 + z)^{1/z} = e \).

Let \(L = \lim_{x \to 0} (1 + 3x)^{5/x}\).

Rewrite the exponent: \( (1 + 3x)^{5/x} = \left[ (1 + 3x)^{1/(3x)} \right]^{3x \times (5/x)} = \left[ (1 + 3x)^{1/(3x)} \right]^{15} \).

Let \(z = 3x\). As \(x \to 0\), \(z \to 0\).

The expression inside the square brackets approaches \( \lim_{z \to 0} (1 + z)^{1/z} = e \).

Therefore, the limit is:

\[ L = \left[ \lim_{z \to 0} (1 + z)^{1/z} \right]^{15} = e^{15} \]

Answer: b. \( e^{15} \)

04. Find the coordinates of the focus of the parabola x2 = 6y [x2 = 6y পরাবৃত্তের ফোকাসের স্থানাঙ্ক নির্ণয় কর।]

  • (-3/2, 0)
  • (0, 3/2)
  • (0, -3/2)
  • (0, 0)

Solution:

The equation \(x^2 = 6y\) is of the standard form \(x^2 = 4ay\), which represents a parabola opening upwards with its vertex at the origin (0, 0).

Comparing the equations: \(4a = 6\).

\( a = 6/4 = 3/2 \).

For a parabola of the form \(x^2 = 4ay\), the focus is located at the point (0, a).

Therefore, the focus is at \( (0, 3/2) \).

Answer: b. (0, 3/2)

06. At maximum height, what is the value of potential energy? [ সর্বোচ্চ উচ্চতায়, বিভব শক্তির মান কত?]

  • 0
  • Maximum
  • Minimum
  • None

Solution:

When an object is thrown vertically upwards, its kinetic energy is converted into potential energy as it rises.

Potential energy (PE) due to gravity is given by PE = mgh, where h is the height above a reference level.

At the maximum height (h_max), the height is greatest. Assuming the reference level for potential energy is the starting point (h=0), the potential energy will be maximum at the maximum height.

PE_max = mgh_max.

Answer: b. Maximum

07. If the largest resultant of two vectors is 28 units and smallest resultant is 4 units then resultant will be 5 times of the smaller resultant If the angle act then the resultant will be 5 times of the smallest resultant? [If the angle act then... ] (Sentence seems garbled) [যদি দুইটি ভেক্টরের সর্বোচ্চ লব্ধি 28 একক এবং সর্বনিম্ন লব্ধি 4 একক হয়। যদি কোণ এমনভাবে কাজ করে তাহলে লব্ধি ক্ষুদ্রতম লব্ধির 5 গুণ হবে?] (Assume find angle when resultant is 5*4=20 units)

  • 90°
  • 45°
  • 60°
  • 180°

Solution:

Let the magnitudes of the two vectors be A and B.

Largest resultant occurs when vectors are parallel (\(\theta = 0^\circ\)): \(R_{max} = A + B\).

Smallest resultant occurs when vectors are antiparallel (\(\theta = 180^\circ\)): \(R_{min} = |A - B|\).

Given: \(A + B = 28\) (Eq 1)

Given: \(|A - B| = 4\). Let's assume A > B, so \(A - B = 4\) (Eq 2).

Solve the system:

Add Eq 1 and Eq 2: \((A + B) + (A - B) = 28 + 4 \implies 2A = 32 \implies A = 16\).

Substitute A=16 into Eq 1: \(16 + B = 28 \implies B = 12\).

The magnitudes are 16 units and 12 units.

The resultant R when the angle between them is \(\theta\) is given by \(R^2 = A^2 + B^2 + 2AB \cos\theta\).

We need to find the angle \(\theta\) when the resultant is 5 times the smallest resultant.

Required resultant R = 5 × \(R_{min}\) = 5 × 4 = 20 units.

Substitute into the formula:

\( 20^2 = 16^2 + 12^2 + 2(16)(12) \cos\theta \)

\( 400 = 256 + 144 + 2(192) \cos\theta \)

\( 400 = 400 + 384 \cos\theta \)

\( 0 = 384 \cos\theta \)

\( \cos\theta = 0 \)

This occurs when \(\theta = 90^\circ\).

Answer: a. 90°

08. A car travels a distance of 200 km at a speed of 50 km/h and then travels a distance of 300 km at a speed of 60 km/h. What is the average speed of the car for the entire journey? [একটি গাড়ি 50 কিমি/ঘন্টা বেগে 200 কিমি দূরত্ব অতিক্রম করে এবং তারপর 60 কিমি/ঘন্টা বেগে 300 কিমি দূরত্ব অতিক্রম করে। পুরো যাত্রার জন্য গাড়ির গড় গতি কত?]

  • 54.56 km/h
  • 55.56 km/h
  • 56.56 km/h
  • 57.56 km/h

Solution:

Average speed = Total Distance / Total Time.

Part 1: Distance \(d_1 = 200\) km, Speed \(s_1 = 50\) km/h.

Time \(t_1 = d_1 / s_1 = 200 / 50 = 4\) hours.

Part 2: Distance \(d_2 = 300\) km, Speed \(s_2 = 60\) km/h.

Time \(t_2 = d_2 / s_2 = 300 / 60 = 5\) hours.

Total Distance = \(d_1 + d_2 = 200 + 300 = 500\) km.

Total Time = \(t_1 + t_2 = 4 + 5 = 9\) hours.

Average Speed = Total Distance / Total Time = 500 km / 9 hours.

Average Speed \(= 55.555...\) km/h.

This is approximately 55.56 km/h.

Answer: b. 55.56 km/h

09. Which one is the mathematical form of Kepler's third law on planetary motion? [নিচের কোনটি কেপলারের গ্রহীয় গতির তৃতীয় সূত্রের গাণিতিক রূপ?]

  • T13/R12 = T23/R22
  • T12/R13 = T22/R23
  • T12/R12 = T22/R22
  • T12/R1 = T22/R2

Solution:

Kepler's Third Law states that the square of the orbital period (T) of a planet is directly proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis (R, often used for radius in near-circular orbits) of its orbit.

Mathematically: \(T^2 \propto R^3\), or \(T^2 = k R^3\) where k is a constant for all objects orbiting the same central body.

This means the ratio \( \frac{T^2}{R^3} \) is constant.

Therefore, for two planets (or satellites) 1 and 2 orbiting the same body:

\[ \frac{T_1^2}{R_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{R_2^3} \]

Answer: b. T12/R13 = T22/R23

10. A person is pulling an object of weight 100N through a frictionless floor of a slope of angle 300 and height 5m. If the object moves with uniform speed, then what will be the work done? [একজন ব্যক্তি 100N ওজনের একটি বস্তুকে 300 কোণের ঢালের একটি ঘর্ষণহীন তলের মধ্যে দিয়ে 5m উচ্চতায় টানছে। যদি বস্তুটি অভিন্ন গতিতে চলে, তাহলে কৃত কাজ কত হবে?] (Angle likely 30°)

  • 100 J
  • 300 J
  • 500J
  • 700 J

Solution:

Work done against gravity when lifting an object to a certain height is equal to the change in potential energy, regardless of the path taken (if friction is ignored).

Work Done (W) = Change in Potential Energy = mgh.

Weight of the object (mg) = 100 N.

Height (h) = 5 m.

Work Done = Weight × Height = 100 N × 5 m = 500 J.

Since the object moves with uniform speed, the net work done (including work by the pulling force and work by gravity) is zero according to the work-energy theorem. However, the question asks for the work done *by the person* (or the pulling force). The work done by the person must overcome the work done by gravity to lift the object to that height.

The angle of the slope (assuming 30°) and frictionless floor details are relevant if calculating the pulling force, but not for the work done against gravity to achieve the final height.

Answer: c. 500J
