1. Srabon and Subha, Invest 5000 and 3000 taka at interest rate of 15% for 10 years, how much interest will they both get at the end of the period? [শ্রাবণ এবং শুভা, 10 বছরের জন্য 15% সুদের হারে 5000 এবং 3000 টাকা বিনিয়োগ করে, মেয়াদ শেষে তারা উভয়ই কত সুদ পাবে?] (Assuming Simple Interest)

  • 7500 Tk
  • 12000 Tk
  • 14500 Tk
  • 20000 Tk

Solution:

Calculate Simple Interest (SI) for each person using SI = P × R × T.

Rate R = 15% = 0.15. Time T = 10 years.

Srabon: P1 = 5000 Tk.

SI1 = 5000 × 0.15 × 10 = 5000 × 1.5 = 7500 Tk.

Subha: P2 = 3000 Tk.

SI2 = 3000 × 0.15 × 10 = 3000 × 1.5 = 4500 Tk.

Total Interest = SI1 + SI2 = 7500 + 4500 = 12000 Tk.

Answer: b. 12000 Tk

3. The class consists of 50 students, out of which 30 are girls. The mean of marks scored by girls in a test is 73 (out of 100), and that of boys is 71. Determine the mean score of the whole class. [শ্রেণীতে 50 জন শিক্ষার্থী রয়েছে, যার মধ্যে 30 জন মেয়ে। একটি পরীক্ষায় মেয়েদের প্রাপ্ত নম্বরের গড় 73 (100 এর মধ্যে), এবং ছেলেদের গড় 71। পুরো ক্লাসের গড় স্কোর নির্ণয় করুন।]

  • 72.2
  • 80.4
  • 74.5
  • 76.8

Solution:

Total students = 50.

Number of girls = 30.

Number of boys = Total students - Number of girls = 50 - 30 = 20.

Mean score of girls = 73.

Total score of girls = Mean × Number of girls = 73 × 30 = 2190.

Mean score of boys = 71.

Total score of boys = Mean × Number of boys = 71 × 20 = 1420.

Total score of whole class = Total score of girls + Total score of boys = 2190 + 1420 = 3610.

Mean score of whole class = Total score of whole class / Total students

Mean score = 3610 / 50 = 361 / 5 = 72.2.

Answer: a. 72.2

4. A basket contains 6 blue, 4 red, 4 green and 3 yellow balls. If four balls are picked up at random, what is the probability that all 4 are reds? [ একটি ঝুড়িতে 6টি নীল, 4টি লাল, 4টি সবুজ এবং 3টি হলুদ বল রয়েছে। যদি এলোমেলোভাবে চারটি বল তোলা হয়, তাহলে 4টিই লাল হওয়ার সম্ভাবনা কত?]

  • 4/15
  • 4/17
  • 1/2380
  • 2/455

Solution:

Total number of balls = 6 (blue) + 4 (red) + 4 (green) + 3 (yellow) = 17 balls.

We are picking 4 balls at random.

Total number of ways to pick 4 balls from 17 = \( \binom{17}{4} \).

\( \binom{17}{4} = \frac{17!}{4!(17-4)!} = \frac{17!}{4!13!} = \frac{17 \times 16 \times 15 \times 14}{4 \times 3 \times 2 \times 1} = 17 \times 4 \times 5 \times 7 / (3 \times 2 \times 1) = 17 \times 2 \times 5 \times 7 = 17 \times 70 = 2380 \).

Total possible outcomes = 2380.

We want the probability that all 4 balls picked are red.

Number of red balls = 4.

Number of ways to pick 4 red balls from 4 red balls = \( \binom{4}{4} = 1 \).

Number of favourable outcomes = 1.

Probability = (Favourable Outcomes) / (Total Outcomes) = 1 / 2380.

Answer: c. 1/2380

6. The ratio of the sides of two similar triangles is 3:5. If the perimeter of the smaller triangle is 24 cm, what is the perimeter of the larger triangle? [ দুটি অনুরূপ ত্রিভুজের বাহুগুলির অনুপাত 3:5। যদি ছোট ত্রিভুজটির পরিধি 24 সেমি হয়, তাহলে বড় ত্রিভুজটির পরিধি কত?]

  • 36 cm
  • 40 cm
  • 48 cm
  • 60 cm

Solution:

For similar triangles, the ratio of their corresponding sides is equal to the ratio of their perimeters.

Ratio of sides (Smaller : Larger) = 3 : 5.

Ratio of perimeters (Smaller : Larger) = 3 : 5.

Let P_small be the perimeter of the smaller triangle and P_large be the perimeter of the larger triangle.

\( \frac{P_{small}}{P_{large}} = \frac{3}{5} \)

Given P_small = 24 cm.

\( \frac{24}{P_{large}} = \frac{3}{5} \)

Cross-multiply: \( 24 \times 5 = 3 \times P_{large} \)

\( 120 = 3 \times P_{large} \)

\( P_{large} = 120 / 3 = 40 \) cm.

Answer: b. 40 cm

9. A is twice as good workman as B. If they work together, they can complete a job in 18 days. If A alone does the job, in how many days he will complete the job? [A হল B-এর চেয়ে দ্বিগুণ ভালো কর্মী। যদি তারা একসাথে কাজ করে, তারা 18 দিনে একটি কাজ সম্পন্ন করতে পারে। যদি A একা কাজটি করে, তাহলে সে কত দিনে কাজটি সম্পন্ন করবে?]

  • 27 days
  • days
  • 36 days
  • 40 days

Solution:

Let the rate of B be \(R_B\) jobs/day.

A is twice as good, so the rate of A (\(R_A\)) = \(2 \times R_B\).

Working together, their combined rate = \(R_A + R_B = 2 R_B + R_B = 3 R_B\).

Time taken together = 18 days.

Work = Combined Rate × Time

1 job = \( (3 R_B) \times 18 \)

\( 1 = 54 R_B \implies R_B = 1/54 \) job/day.

Rate of A = \( R_A = 2 R_B = 2 \times (1/54) = 1/27 \) job/day.

Time taken by A alone = Work / Rate of A

Time for A = 1 / (1/27) = 27 days.

Answer: a. 27 days

10. In an exam, 90% passed History and 85% passed English. If none of the students failed in both subjects and 225 passed in both subjects, calculate the number of students who have attended the exam? [একটি পরীক্ষায়, 90% ইতিহাসে এবং 85% ইংরেজিতে পাস করেছে। যদি কোনো শিক্ষার্থী উভয় বিষয়ে ফেল না করে এবং 225 জন উভয় বিষয়ে পাস করে, তাহলে পরীক্ষায় অংশগ্রহণকারী শিক্ষার্থীর সংখ্যা নির্ণয় করুন?]

  • 250
  • 275
  • 300
  • 325

Solution:

Let H be the set of students who passed History.

Let E be the set of students who passed English.

Let T be the total number of students.

Number passed History, |H| = 0.90 T.

Number passed English, |E| = 0.85 T.

"None failed in both subjects" means every student passed at least one subject. This implies the set of students who passed either History or English or both is the entire set of students.

So, |H ∪ E| = T.

We are given that the number who passed in both subjects, |H ∩ E| = 225.

Use the inclusion-exclusion principle:

|H ∪ E| = |H| + |E| - |H ∩ E|

Substitute the known values:

T = (0.90 T) + (0.85 T) - 225

T = 1.75 T - 225

225 = 1.75 T - T

225 = 0.75 T

T = 225 / 0.75 = 225 / (3/4) = 225 × (4/3) = 75 × 4 = 300.

The total number of students is 300.

Answer: c. 300

11. If x + y = a and x - y = b, then 2xy = ? [যদি x + y = a এবং x - y = b, তাহলে 2xy = ?]

  • (a2 - b2) / 2
  • (b2 - a2) / 2
  • (a - b) / 2
  • (ab) / 2

Solution:

We have two identities involving (x+y) and (x-y):

1) \( (x+y)^2 = x^2 + 2xy + y^2 \)

2) \( (x-y)^2 = x^2 - 2xy + y^2 \)

Subtract the second identity from the first:

\( (x+y)^2 - (x-y)^2 = (x^2 + 2xy + y^2) - (x^2 - 2xy + y^2) \)

\( (x+y)^2 - (x-y)^2 = 4xy \)

Substitute \( x+y = a \) and \( x-y = b \):

\( a^2 - b^2 = 4xy \)

We need to find 2xy. Divide the equation by 2:

\( \frac{a^2 - b^2}{2} = 2xy \)

Answer: a. (a2 - b2) / 2

08. Which one is food preservatives? [কোনটি খাদ্য সংরক্ষক?]

  • NaNO3
  • CH3COOH
  • CH3COONa

Solution:

Food preservatives inhibit microbial growth or undesirable chemical changes.

  • Na2SO4 (Sodium sulfate): Generally not used as a preservative.
  • NaNO3 (Sodium nitrate): Used as a preservative, particularly in cured meats, to inhibit bacterial growth (like Clostridium botulinum) and maintain color. (Nitrites like NaNO2 are also used).
  • CH3COOH (Acetic acid): Vinegar; used as a preservative due to its acidity.
  • CH3COONa (Sodium acetate): Can act as a buffer or preservative, often in conjunction with acetic acid.

Both Sodium Nitrate and Acetic Acid/Sodium Acetate have preservative properties. Nitrates/nitrites are well-known meat preservatives. Acetic acid (vinegar) is a common household preservative.

Let's reconsider standard chemical preservatives. Sodium nitrate (and nitrite) are common industrial preservatives. Sodium benzoate is also very common but not listed. Vinegar (acetic acid) is common. Sodium acetate is less common as a primary preservative itself but acts as a buffer. Given the options, Sodium Nitrate is a widely recognized food preservative.

09. In SATP, what is the molar volume of gas? [SATP-তে গ্যাসের মোলার আয়তন কত?]

  • 22.4 L
  • 22.8L
  • 24.4L
  • 24.8L

Solution:

SATP stands for Standard Ambient Temperature and Pressure.

Defined by IUPAC as:

  • Temperature = 25 °C = 298.15 K
  • Pressure = 100 kPa = 1 bar (exactly 100,000 Pa)

Using the Ideal Gas Law: PV = nRT.

Molar Volume (V/n) = RT / P.

R (ideal gas constant) ≈ 8.314 J/(mol·K) or 8.314 Pa·m3/(mol·K).

Molar Volume = (8.314 Pa·m3/(mol·K) × 298.15 K) / (100,000 Pa)

Molar Volume ≈ 2478.9 m3/mol / 100,000 ≈ 0.024789 m3/mol.

Convert m3 to Litres (1 m3 = 1000 L):

Molar Volume ≈ 0.024789 × 1000 L/mol ≈ 24.789 L/mol.

This is closest to 24.8 L.

(Note: At STP - Standard Temperature and Pressure - 0°C and 100 kPa, the molar volume is ≈ 22.7 L. At the older STP definition of 0°C and 1 atm (101.325 kPa), it is ≈ 22.4 L).

Answer: d. 24.8L

10. ______ is a non-redox reaction. [ ______ একটি নন-রিডক্স বিক্রিয়া।]

  • Rusting of iron
  • Dissolving salt in water
  • Dissolving Zinc in dil. H2SO4
  • Burning of candle

Solution:

Redox (Reduction-Oxidation) reactions involve a change in the oxidation states of atoms.

  • Rusting of iron: Iron (Fe, oxidation state 0) reacts with oxygen (O2, state 0) to form iron oxides (like Fe2O3, Fe state +3, O state -2). Oxidation states change, so it's redox.
  • Dissolving salt in water: E.g., NaCl(s) → Na+(aq) + Cl-(aq). The ions already exist in the solid lattice with states +1 and -1, and they just get hydrated in water. No change in oxidation state occurs. This is a physical process or simple dissolution, not typically considered redox.
  • Dissolving Zinc in dil. H2SO4: Zn(s) + H2SO4(aq) → ZnSO4(aq) + H2(g). Zinc (state 0) becomes Zn2+ (state +2) (oxidation). Hydrogen in H2SO4 (state +1) becomes H2 gas (state 0) (reduction). Oxidation states change, so it's redox.
  • Burning of candle: Wax (hydrocarbons, C approx -2, H +1) reacts with oxygen (O2, state 0) to form CO2 (C +4, O -2) and H2O (H +1, O -2). Oxidation states change, so it's redox (combustion).

Dissolving salt (like NaCl) in water is a non-redox process.

Answer: b. Dissolving salt in water
