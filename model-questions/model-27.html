Section C – [General Math]

1. If Subha wants to accumulate 10000 taka, after 5 years, on a 10% compounding interest rate, how much must she Invest now? [যদি শুভা 5 বছর পর, 10% চক্রবৃদ্ধি সুদের হারে, মোট 10000 টাকা জমাতে চায়, তাহলে তাকে এখন কত টাকা বিনিয়োগ করতে হবে?]

  • 6223.46
  • 6426.56
  • 6211.18
  • 6314.56

Solution:

This requires the formula for the Present Value (P) of a future Amount (A) under compound interest.

Formula: \( P = \frac{A}{(1 + r)^n} \)

Where:

  • A = Future Amount = 10000 taka
  • r = Annual interest rate = 10% = 0.10
  • n = Number of years = 5
  • P = Principal (Initial Investment) = ?

\( P = \frac{10000}{(1 + 0.10)^5} = \frac{10000}{(1.1)^5} \)

Calculate \( (1.1)^5 \):

\( (1.1)^2 = 1.21 \)

\( (1.1)^4 = (1.21)^2 = 1.4641 \)

\( (1.1)^5 = (1.1)^4 \times 1.1 = 1.4641 \times 1.1 = 1.61051 \)

\( P = \frac{10000}{1.61051} \approx 6209.21 \)

Let's check the options. Option c (6211.18) is the closest.

Answer: c. 6211.18

2. Two cars started at the same time, one from X to Y and other from Y to X. If they arrived at Y and X respectively in 16 hours and 25 hours after they passed each other, the ratio of the speeds of the two cars was? [দুটি গাড়ি একই সময়ে যাত্রা শুরু করে, একটি X থেকে Y এবং অন্যটি Y থেকে X। যদি তারা একে অপরকে অতিক্রম করার পর যথাক্রমে 16 ঘন্টা এবং 25 ঘন্টা পরে Y এবং X এ পৌঁছায়, তাহলে গাড়ি দুটির গতির অনুপাত কত ছিল?]

  • 3:1
  • 4:5
  • 5:4
  • 5:6

Solution:

Let the speeds of the two cars starting from X and Y be \(S_X\) and \(S_Y\) respectively.

Let them meet after time 't'.

After meeting, the car from X takes \(t_X = 16\) hours to reach Y.

After meeting, the car from Y takes \(t_Y = 25\) hours to reach X.

There is a direct formula relating the speeds and the times taken *after* meeting: \( \frac{S_X}{S_Y} = \sqrt{\frac{t_Y}{t_X}} \)

Substitute the given times:

\( \frac{S_X}{S_Y} = \sqrt{\frac{25}{16}} = \frac{\sqrt{25}}{\sqrt{16}} = \frac{5}{4} \)

The ratio of the speeds (Speed of car from X : Speed of car from Y) is 5 : 4.

Answer: c. 5:4

3. The mean of four numbers is 71.5. If three of the numbers are 58, 76, and 88, what is the value of the fourth number? [চারটি সংখ্যার গড় 71.5। যদি তিনটি সংখ্যা 58, 76, এবং 88 হয়, তাহলে চতুর্থ সংখ্যাটির মান কত?]

  • 64
  • 60
  • 76
  • 82

Solution:

Mean = (Sum of numbers) / (Count of numbers).

Given Mean = 71.5.

Count of numbers = 4.

Sum of numbers = Mean × Count = 71.5 × 4 = 286.

The three known numbers are 58, 76, 88.

Sum of the three known numbers = 58 + 76 + 88 = 222.

Let the fourth number be x.

Sum of numbers = (Sum of three known numbers) + x

286 = 222 + x

x = 286 - 222 = 64.

The fourth number is 64.

Answer: a. 64

4. A standard deck of playing cards (52 cards) is shuffled ( এলোমেলোভাবে তাস সাজানো) thoroughly. What is the probability of drawing a heart or a diamond? [একটি স্ট্যান্ডার্ড তাসের ডেক (52 কার্ড) ভালোভাবে নাড়ানো হয়। একটি হার্ট বা একটি ডায়মন্ড আঁকার সম্ভাবনা কত?]

  • 13/52
  • 26/52
  • 39/52
  • 26/52

Solution:

In a standard deck of 52 cards:

Number of Hearts = 13.

Number of Diamonds = 13.

The events of drawing a Heart and drawing a Diamond are mutually exclusive (a card cannot be both a Heart and a Diamond).

Probability (Heart or Diamond) = P(Heart) + P(Diamond).

P(Heart) = 13/52.

P(Diamond) = 13/52.

P(Heart or Diamond) = 13/52 + 13/52 = 26/52.

Simplify 26/52 = 1/2.

(Options b and d are the same and represent the correct probability before simplification).

Answer: b. 26/52 (or d. 26/52)

7. If tan x + cot x = 5, then tan2x + cot2x is? [যদি tan x + cot x = 5, তাহলে tan2x + cot2x কত?]

  • 23
  • 24
  • 25
  • 26

Solution:

Given: \( \tan x + \cot x = 5 \).

We want to find \( \tan^2 x + \cot^2 x \).

Square both sides of the given equation:

\( (\tan x + \cot x)^2 = 5^2 \)

\( \tan^2 x + 2 (\tan x)(\cot x) + \cot^2 x = 25 \)

Recall that \( \cot x = 1 / \tan x \), so \( (\tan x)(\cot x) = \tan x \times \frac{1}{\tan x} = 1 \).

\( \tan^2 x + \cot^2 x + 2 = 25 \)

\( \tan^2 x + \cot^2 x = 25 - 2 = 23 \).

12. If log10 2 = 0.3010, the value of log10 80 is

  • 1.6020
  • 1.9030
  • 3.9030
  • None of these

Solution:

\( \log_{10} 80 = \log_{10} (8 \times 10) \)

Using \( \log(ab) = \log a + \log b \):

\( = \log_{10} 8 + \log_{10} 10 \)

We know \( \log_{10} 10 = 1 \).

\( \log_{10} 8 = \log_{10} (2^3) \)

Using \( \log(a^c) = c \log a \):

\( = 3 \log_{10} 2 \).

So, \( \log_{10} 80 = 3 \log_{10} 2 + 1 \).

Given \( \log_{10} 2 = 0.3010 \).

\( \log_{10} 80 = 3(0.3010) + 1 = 0.9030 + 1 = 1.9030 \).

13. If x > 2 and y < -2, then which of the following must be true?

  • x/y > 1
  • x/y < -1
  • x/y < 0
  • xy > 0

Solution:

Given \( x > 2 \) (x is positive and greater than 2).

Given \( y < -2 \) (y is negative and less than -2).

Consider the ratio x/y:

Since x is positive and y is negative, their ratio x/y must be negative.

So, \( x/y < 0 \). This matches option c.

Let's analyze further:

Since x > 2 and |y| > 2, consider the magnitude |x/y| = |x|/|y|.

Example 1: x=3, y=-3. x/y = -1.

Example 2: x=4, y=-3. x/y = -4/3 < -1.

Example 3: x=3, y=-4. x/y = -3/4 > -1.

Option d (xy > 0) is false because x is positive and y is negative, so their product xy must be negative.

The only statement that must be true is x/y < 0.

Answer: c. x/y < 0

14. In a certain sequence, each term is 7 greater than the term before it. If the first term of the sequence is 39, what is the 13th term? [একটি নির্দিষ্ট ধারায়, প্রতিটি পদ তার পূর্ববর্তী পদের চেয়ে 7 বড়। যদি ধারাটির প্রথম পদ 39 হয়, তাহলে 13তম পদ কত?]

  • 102
  • 109
  • 116
  • 123

Solution:

This describes an arithmetic sequence.

First term (a1) = 39.

Common difference (d) = 7.

We need to find the 13th term (a13).

The formula for the nth term is \( a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d \).

Substitute n=13, a1=39, d=7:

\( a_{13} = 39 + (13-1) \times 7 \)

\( a_{13} = 39 + (12) \times 7 \)

\( a_{13} = 39 + 84 = 123 \).

Answer: d. 123

15. If a parallelogram is inscribed in a circle, then what will be the parallelogram? [একটি বৃত্তে অন্তর্লিখিত সামান্তরিককে কি বলে?]

  • Square
  • Rectangle
  • Trapezium
  • Rhombus

Solution:

If a parallelogram is inscribed in a circle (meaning all its vertices lie on the circle), it must be a rectangle.

Reasoning: In a cyclic quadrilateral, opposite angles are supplementary (add to 180°). In a parallelogram, opposite angles are equal. Let the angles be A, B, C, D. A=C and B=D. Also A+C=180° and B+D=180°. If A=C and A+C=180°, then 2A=180° => A=90°. Similarly, B=D=90°. A parallelogram with all angles equal to 90° is a rectangle.

(A square is a special type of rectangle, but not all parallelograms inscribed in circles are squares. A rhombus inscribed in a circle must be a square. A trapezium inscribed in a circle must be an isosceles trapezium).

Answer: b. Rectangle

01. The radius of a circle is increasing at the rate of 2 m/s. Then what is the rate of change of area? [একটি বৃত্তের ব্যাসার্ধ 2 m/s হারে বৃদ্ধি পাচ্ছে। ক্ষেত্রফল পরিবর্তনের হার কত?]

  • 10π m2/s
  • 15π m2/s
  • 20π m2/s
  • 40π m2/s

Solution:

Area of a circle \( A = \pi r^2 \).

We are given the rate of change of radius with respect to time: \( \frac{dr}{dt} = 2 \) m/s.

We need to find the rate of change of area with respect to time, \( \frac{dA}{dt} \).

Differentiate the area formula with respect to time (t) using the chain rule:

\( \frac{dA}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt}(\pi r^2) = \pi \frac{d}{dt}(r^2) \)

\( \frac{dA}{dt} = \pi (2r \frac{dr}{dt}) = 2 \pi r \frac{dr}{dt} \)

Substitute the given value \( \frac{dr}{dt} = 2 \):

\( \frac{dA}{dt} = 2 \pi r (2) = 4 \pi r \) m2/s.

The rate of change of area depends on the radius 'r' at the instant considered. The question doesn't specify the radius. However, looking at the options:

  • If \(4\pi r = 10\pi\), then r = 2.5 m.
  • If \(4\pi r = 15\pi\), then r = 3.75 m.
  • If \(4\pi r = 20\pi\), then r = 5 m.
  • If \(4\pi r = 40\pi\), then r = 10 m.

Without knowing the radius, we cannot get a specific numerical answer. Assuming the question implicitly expects the answer when r=5m or r=10m based on common problem values.

Answer: (Depends on radius 'r'. If r=5, answer is 20π; if r=10, answer is 40π)

02. What will be the enclosed area of 1st quadrant bounded by y = sin x, y = cos x, x = π/4? [প্রথম চতুর্ভাগে y = sin x, y = cos x, x = π/4 দ্বারা আবদ্ধ ক্ষেত্রের ক্ষেত্রফল কত?] (Intersection is at pi/4, bounded by x=pi/4 makes no sense)

  • √2 – 1
  • 2√2 – 1
  • 1 - ½
  • None

Solution:

The curves y=sinx and y=cosx intersect when sinx = cosx, which in the first quadrant occurs at x = π/4.

The question asks for the area bounded by y=sinx, y=cosx, and the line x=π/4. This doesn't define a closed region. It likely meant bounded by y=sinx, y=cosx, and the y-axis (x=0).

Assuming the bounds are x=0 to x=π/4.

In the interval [0, π/4], cos x ≥ sin x. So, cos x is the upper curve.

Area = \( \int_0^{\pi/4} (\cos x - \sin x) \, dx \)

Area = \( [\sin x - (-\cos x)]_0^{\pi/4} = [\sin x + \cos x]_0^{\pi/4} \)

Evaluate at limits:

Upper limit (x=π/4): \( \sin(\pi/4) + \cos(\pi/4) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{2}} = \sqrt{2} \).

Lower limit (x=0): \( \sin(0) + \cos(0) = 0 + 1 = 1 \).

Area = (Value at upper limit) - (Value at lower limit) = \( \sqrt{2} - 1 \).

This matches option a.

Answer: a. √2 – 1 (Assuming bounds 0 to π/4)

03. If a ≤ b then which one of the following will always be true?

  • b. a-3 ≤ b-3
  • c. 3-a ≤ 3-b
  • d. a2 ≤ ab

Solution:

Given \( a \le b \).

  • a. Multiplying by -1 reverses the inequality sign: \( -a \ge -b \). Option a is incorrect.
  • b. Subtracting the same number (3) from both sides does not change the inequality direction: \( a - 3 \le b - 3 \). Option b is correct.
  • c. Multiply by -1: \( -a \ge -b \). Add 3: \( 3 - a \ge 3 - b \). Option c is incorrect.
  • d. If a= -2, b= -1, then a ≤ b is true. a2=4, ab=2. Here a2 > ab. If a=2, b=3, then a ≤ b is true. a2=4, ab=6. Here a2 < ab. If a=-3, b=2, then a ≤ b is true. a2=9, ab=-6. Here a2 > ab. Option d is not always true.

06. (i x j). k=?

  • 1
  • -1
  • 0
  • -k2

Solution:

Using the properties of standard unit vectors \(\hat{i}, \hat{j}, \hat{k}\):

The cross product \( \hat{i} \times \hat{j} = \hat{k} \).

The expression becomes \( (\hat{k}) \cdot \hat{k} \).

The dot product of a unit vector with itself is the square of its magnitude.

\( \hat{k} \cdot \hat{k} = |\hat{k}|^2 \).

Since \(\hat{k}\) is a unit vector, its magnitude is 1.

\( \hat{k} \cdot \hat{k} = 1^2 = 1 \).

This is the scalar triple product \([\hat{i} \, \hat{j} \, \hat{k}]\), representing the volume of the unit cube, which is 1.

Answer: a. 1

07. In what angle a projectile should be shot so that horizontal range and maximum height will be equal? [কত কোণে নিক্ষেপ করলে একটি প্রাসের অনুভূমিক পাল্লা তার সর্বোচ্চ উচ্চতার সমান হবে?]

  • 45°
  • 90°
  • 76°
  • 0°

Solution:

Range \( R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g} = \frac{v_0^2 (2\sin\theta \cos\theta)}{g} \).

Maximum Height \( H = \frac{v_0^2 \sin^2\theta}{2g} \).

Set R = H:

\( \frac{v_0^2 (2\sin\theta \cos\theta)}{g} = \frac{v_0^2 \sin^2\theta}{2g} \)

Cancel \( v_0^2 / g \) (assuming \(v_0 \neq 0, g \neq 0\)):

\( 2\sin\theta \cos\theta = \frac{\sin^2\theta}{2} \)

Assuming \( \sin\theta \neq 0 \) (i.e., \(\theta \neq 0\)), divide by \(\sin\theta\):

\( 2\cos\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{2} \)

\( 4\cos\theta = \sin\theta \)

Divide by \(\cos\theta\) (assuming \(\cos\theta \neq 0\), i.e., \(\theta \neq 90^\circ\)):

\( 4 = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta} = \tan\theta \)

\( \theta = \arctan(4) \).

\( \arctan(4) \approx 75.96^\circ \approx 76^\circ \).

Answer: c. 76°

08. The momentum of an object of mass 100 kg is 200 kgm/s. What is kinetic energy? [100 কেজি ভরের একটি বস্তুর ভরবেগ 200 kgm/s। গতিশক্তি কত?]

  • 200 J
  • 400 J
  • 600 J
  • 800 J

Solution:

Momentum (p) = mass (m) × velocity (v).

Kinetic Energy (KE) = \( \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \).

Relationship between KE and p: \( KE = \frac{p^2}{2m} \).

Given:

Mass (m) = 100 kg.

Momentum (p) = 200 kg·m/s.

Substitute into the relationship:

KE = \( \frac{(200)^2}{2 \times 100} = \frac{40000}{200} = \frac{400}{2} = 200 \) Joules.

Answer: a. 200 J

  • 2500 kg
  • 25000
  • 250000

10. Parking orbit is— [পার্কিং কক্ষপথ হল-]

  • Airplane route
  • Orbit of Polar satellite
  • Orbit of Geo-static satellite
  • Orbit of Earth

Solution:

A parking orbit is a temporary orbit used during the launch of a satellite or spacecraft. After reaching the parking orbit, the spacecraft waits for the optimal time before firing its engine again to enter the final desired orbit (e.g., geostationary orbit, lunar trajectory, interplanetary trajectory). While geostationary satellites often use a parking orbit (typically a low Earth orbit or Geostationary Transfer Orbit) as an intermediate step, the parking orbit itself is not the final geostationary orbit.

However, the term is most strongly associated with placing satellites, particularly geostationary ones, into their final position. If forced to choose from the options, the context often relates to achieving geostationary orbit.

Answer: c. Orbit of Geo-static satellite (as an intermediate step or related context)
